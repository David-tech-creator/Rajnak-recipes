title: Juicy Upside-Down Plum Cake

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '45 mins'
servings: 8
kitchenNote: Flip while warm. Cold caramel sticks.
signoff: Tuck in.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 50 g butter, melted
  - 1 dl raw cane sugar
  - 5 medium plums, halved and stoned
  - 100 g butter, softened
  - 2 dl caster sugar
  - 2 large eggs
  - 3 dl plain flour
  - 1.5 tsp baking powder
  - 1.5 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 2 dl whole milk
  - 2 dl crème fraîche, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C and line the bottom of a 24 cm round cake tin with parchment.
  - Mix the 50 g melted butter with the raw cane sugar and spread over the base of the tin.
  - Slice the plum halves into thin wedges and arrange them in a pattern on top of the sugar layer — this becomes the top of the cake.
  - Beat the 100 g softened butter with the caster sugar until pale and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time.
  - Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and vanilla sugar. Fold into the batter alternately with the milk, ending with the flour, until just combined.
  - Pour the batter gently over the plums and smooth the top. Bake on the lower shelf for about 45 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean.
  - Let the cake rest in the tin for 20 minutes, then invert onto a serving plate. Serve warm with a spoon of crème fraîche.
---

A juicy Swedish plommonkaka — the caramelised plums sit gorgeously on top after you flip it out of the tin. Best while still warm, with cold crème fraîche to balance the sweetness.

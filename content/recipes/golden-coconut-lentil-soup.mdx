  Two technical points carry the soup. Bloom the turmeric and chili
  flakes in oil before the liquid, so the spices become fat-soluble
  and round out the bitterness. Purée only half the pot for a textured
  middle ground between brothy and silky.

ingredients:
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 yellow onion, diced
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 0.5 tbsp ground turmeric
  - 1 pinch crushed red pepper flakes
  - 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  - 200 g red or yellow lentils, rinsed
  - 1 l water
  - 1 chicken stock cube
  - 1 vegetable stock cube
  - 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  - 0.5 tbsp salt, or to taste
  - Fresh cilantro and toasted coconut flakes, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic for 2–3 minutes until soft and translucent.
  - Stir in the turmeric and red pepper flakes and cook one minute more.
  - Add the carrots and lentils, then the water and both stock cubes. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes, until the lentils are soft and broken down.
  - Stir in the coconut milk. Using an immersion blender, purée about half the soup for a silky-textured base while keeping some bite — or blend the whole pot if you prefer it smooth.
  - Taste and add salt, half a teaspoon at a time, until properly seasoned (about 1.5 tsp total). Adjust the pepper flakes if you want more heat.
  - Ladle into bowls and finish with fresh cilantro and a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes.

A vibrant, golden bowl from Geneva's Impact Hub canteen, originally adapted from Budget Bytes. Cheap, fast, vegan if you skip the chicken cube, and ridiculously satisfying.

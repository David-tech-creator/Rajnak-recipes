ingredients:
  - 4 ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges
  - 1 cucumber, thickly sliced
  - 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  - 1 green pepper, sliced into rings
  - 200 g Kalamata olives
  - 200 g feta cheese, in one slab
  - 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  - 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp dried oregano
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Arrange the tomatoes, cucumber, red onion and green pepper in a wide, shallow bowl.
  - Scatter the olives over the vegetables.
  - Lay the slab of feta on top — Greek salad never has crumbled feta.
  - Drizzle generously with olive oil and a smaller drizzle of red wine vinegar.
  - Sprinkle the oregano over the feta along with a grind of black pepper and a small pinch of salt (the olives and feta are already salty).
  - Serve immediately with bread for mopping up the juices.
---

The salad of a Greek summer holiday — ripe tomatoes, briny olives, and a thick slab of feta. Resist the urge to toss; it's eaten in layered bites.

ingredients:
  - 400 g Swedish macaroni (or short pasta)
  - 550 g falukorv, peeled and sliced
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 1 yellow onion, chopped
  - 2 dl grated mild cheese (Västerbotten or mature cheddar)
  - 2 dl sour cream or thick Greek yoghurt
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - Salt, to taste
  - White pepper, to taste
  - Chopped chives, to serve
  - Ketchup, to serve
instructions:
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil and cook the macaroni until tender, about 8 minutes. Drain, reserving a small splash of pasta water.
  - Meanwhile, slice the falukorv into half-moons about 1 cm thick.
  - Melt the butter in a wide frying pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
  - Add the falukorv to the pan and fry until lightly browned and crisp at the edges, about 5 minutes more.
  - Tip the drained macaroni into the pan. Stir in the sour cream, mustard, and most of the cheese. Loosen with a splash of pasta water if needed.
  - Taste, season with salt and pepper, scatter the remaining cheese and the chives on top, and serve with ketchup on the side.
---

The Swedish school-night supper — buttery short macaroni, pink sausage, cheesy sour-cream sauce, ketchup on the side. Twenty minutes flat. Adults can pretend it's grown-up by adding mustard.

ingredients:
  - 5 dl whole milk
  - 50 g fresh yeast (or 2 sachets dry yeast)
  - 150 g butter, melted
  - 1.5 dl caster sugar
  - 1 tsp ground cardamom
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 14 dl plain flour, plus more for dusting
  - 'For the filling:'
  - 150 g butter, very soft
  - 1.5 dl caster sugar
  - 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
  - 1 tsp ground cardamom
  - 'To finish:'
  - 1 egg, beaten
  - Pearl sugar, for sprinkling
instructions:
  - Warm the milk to finger-warm (37 °C). Crumble the yeast into a large bowl and dissolve in a splash of the milk, then add the rest along with the melted butter, sugar, cardamom, and salt.
  - Work in the flour a little at a time until you have a smooth, slightly tacky dough. Knead for ten minutes, cover, and leave to rise for 45 minutes until doubled.
  - Mix the filling ingredients to a thick paste. Tip the dough onto a floured surface and roll into a large rectangle about 40 by 50 cm.
  - Spread the cinnamon butter evenly to the edges. Fold the dough into thirds lengthwise, then cut into 24 thin strips. Twist each strip and knot into a bun, placing on baking trays lined with paper.
  - Cover with a clean towel and prove for 30 minutes until puffy. Heat the oven to 225 °C.
  - Brush the buns with beaten egg, scatter generously with pearl sugar, and bake for 8–12 minutes until deep golden. Cool under a towel so they stay soft.
---

The Swedish staple — twisted, cardamom-scented, knotted properly so they hold their shape. Best eaten the day they're baked, with strong coffee for a proper fika.

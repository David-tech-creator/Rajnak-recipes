ingredients:
  - 1 kg waxy potatoes (skins on)
  - 6 eggs
  - 2 Hungarian sausages (gyulai or kolbász), or 250 g chorizo
  - 400 ml crème fraîche or sour cream
  - 2 tbsp butter, for greasing
  - 3 tbsp dried breadcrumbs, for the dish
  - 1 tsp salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 1 tsp sweet paprika (optional, for the top)
instructions:
  - Scrub the potatoes and boil in their skins in salted water for 20–25 minutes, until tender but still firm. Cool slightly, then peel and slice into 5-mm rounds.
  - Hard-boil the eggs (9 minutes from boiling), cool in cold water, peel, and slice.
  - Slice the sausage into thin coins.
  - Butter a deep ovenproof dish and dust the inside with breadcrumbs.
  - Layer: a layer of potatoes, a sprinkle of salt and pepper, a few spoons of crème fraîche, a layer of egg, more crème fraîche, a layer of sausage. Repeat, finishing with potatoes.
  - Spread a thick final layer of crème fraîche over the top and dust with paprika if you like.
  - Bake at 200 °C for 30–35 minutes, until bubbling at the edges and golden on top. Let it rest 5 minutes before serving.
---

A Hungarian comfort classic — layered potatoes, eggs, sausage and sour cream baked into a golden, bubbling casserole. Nagymama's version, eaten with a sharp cucumber salad on the side.

ingredients:
  - 300 g plain flour
  - 200 g caster sugar
  - 2 tsp baking powder
  - ½ tsp baking soda
  - ½ tsp salt
  - 2 large eggs
  - 120 ml buttermilk
  - 120 ml whole milk
  - 120 g butter, melted and slightly cooled
  - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - 300 g fresh or frozen blueberries
  - 2 tbsp coarse sugar (for the tops)
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper cases.
  - In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  - In a second bowl, beat the eggs lightly, then whisk in the buttermilk, milk, melted butter, vanilla, and lemon zest.
  - Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and fold gently with a spatula — stop the moment the flour disappears. The batter should look lumpy.
  - Toss the blueberries with a teaspoon of flour, then fold them in carefully (this keeps them suspended instead of sinking).
  - Divide the batter between the cases — they should be full to the brim. Scatter coarse sugar on top of each.
  - Bake for 20-22 minutes, until deeply golden and springy when pressed.
  - Cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
---

The two-temperature trick — full-to-the-brim cases and a hot oven — gives you proper domed muffin tops. Lemon zest is what wakes up the blueberries; don't skip it.

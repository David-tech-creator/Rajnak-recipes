  Rabarberkaka is a fixture of the Swedish spring, baked in every
  home and café from late April when the first stalks push out of
  the ground. Rhubarb came to Sweden from China via the

  into the surface (rather than folding it in) gives that
  hallmark look of jewelled pink pieces half-sunk into a buttery
  yellow cake.

ingredients:
  - 500 g rhubarb, cut into 2-cm pieces
  - 150 g unsalted butter, softened
  - 200 g caster sugar, plus 2 tbsp for the rhubarb
  - 3 eggs
  - 250 g plain flour
  - 2 tsp baking powder
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 1.5 dl sour cream or yoghurt
  - Pinch of salt
  - 2 tbsp pearl sugar or demerara, for the top
  - Icing sugar, to dust
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C. Grease and line a 24-cm springform tin.
  - Toss the rhubarb pieces with 2 tbsp sugar and set aside while you make the batter.
  - Beat the butter and 200 g sugar with an electric whisk for 4 minutes, until pale and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating after each.
  - Whisk the flour, baking powder, vanilla sugar, and salt together. Fold half into the batter, then fold in the sour cream, then the rest of the flour.
  - Spread the batter into the tin. Scatter the rhubarb evenly over the top, pushing it gently into the surface. Sprinkle with pearl sugar.
  - Bake for 40–45 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean and the rhubarb is soft and slightly caramelised.
  - Cool in the tin for 10 minutes, then transfer to a rack. Dust with icing sugar and serve warm with cream.

A Swedish-style rhubarb cake — buttery, sour-tipped, with the rhubarb sinking into the surface as it bakes. Spring on a plate, especially with whipped cream.

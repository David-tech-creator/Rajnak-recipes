story: |
  Cottage cheese — the curdled, drained fresh cheese the British call
  curds and the Germans Hüttenkäse — has been a Northern European
  breakfast staple for centuries. The Swedish keso, the Danish
  hytteost, and the Polish twaróg are all close cousins; in every
  one of these kitchens the same bowl appears in summer, cool curds
  mounded with whatever fruit is ripe.

  The pairing is older than it looks. The high-protein, low-fat
  cheese became a fixture of the 1970s health-food movement in the
  US, but the format — fresh cheese, raw fruit, a drizzle of honey,
  a pinch of cinnamon — is straight out of the Central European
  dairy tradition that gave us quark and kesella.
ingredients:
  - 400 g cottage cheese
  - 2 clementines, peeled and segmented
  - 1 large banana, sliced
  - 1 crisp apple, diced
  - 2 tbsp raisins
  - 3 tbsp mixed nuts (walnuts, almonds), roughly chopped
  - 1 tbsp honey (optional)
  - 1 tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  - A few mint leaves, to serve
instructions:
  - Spoon the cottage cheese into a wide bowl or two breakfast bowls.
  - Arrange the clementine segments around the cottage cheese.
  - Scatter the sliced banana and diced apple over the top.
  - Sprinkle with raisins and chopped nuts.
  - Drizzle with honey if you like a sweeter breakfast and dust with cinnamon.
  - Finish with a few torn mint leaves and serve immediately, before the apple browns.
---

A five-minute breakfast or light lunch — protein-rich cottage cheese with whatever fruit is in the bowl. The trick is contrast: soft cheese, crunchy nuts, juicy citrus. A spoon of honey if the fruit isn't sweet enough.

  Májas tojás — chicken liver folded into a soft omelette — is a
  weekday Hungarian breakfast or quick supper, the kind of dish that
  uses up offal in the fridge without ceremony. Paprika, onion, and
  lard are the trinity that defines almost any Hungarian sauté.

  The technique is restraint. Livers want a quick sear and a pink
  centre; cook them grey and they turn dry and chalky. Paprika goes
  in off the heat to keep it from burning. The omelette itself should
  be barely set when the filling lands, since it carries cooking onto
  the plate.

ingredients:
  - 250 g chicken livers, trimmed
  - 1 tbsp lard or rapeseed oil
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 1 tsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 small green pepper, finely diced (optional)
  - 5 large eggs
  - 2 tbsp whole milk
  - 1 tbsp butter
  - 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  - Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
instructions:
  - Trim the chicken livers, removing any green bits and sinew. Pat dry and cut into bite-sized pieces.
  - Heat the lard in a frying pan over medium heat. Soften the onion for 4 minutes until translucent and golden.
  - Pull the pan off the heat, stir in the paprika so it doesn't burn, then return to the heat and add the livers and the diced green pepper.
  - Cook the livers for 3-4 minutes, turning, until just cooked through but still pink inside. Season with salt and pepper. Tip onto a plate.
  - Whisk the eggs with the milk, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  - Wipe the pan, melt the butter over medium heat, and pour in the eggs. Stir gently with a fork for 30 seconds, then let it set on the bottom for another minute.
  - Spoon the liver mixture over one half of the omelette. Fold the other half over and slide onto a warm plate.
  - Scatter with chopped parsley and serve at once.
---

A Hungarian breakfast or quick supper — paprika-spiced chicken liver tucked inside a soft omelette. Cook the livers fast and don't overdo them; pink inside, just cooked through.

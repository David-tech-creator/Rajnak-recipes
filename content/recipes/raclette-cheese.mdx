---
title: 'Raclette Cheese with Potatoes, Charcuterie & Cornichons'
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/raclette-cheese.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 800 g small waxy potatoes (charlotte or ratte)
  - 600 g raclette cheese, sliced 5 mm thick
  - 250 g air-dried beef (Bündnerfleisch)
  - 200 g cured ham (rohschinken or speck)
  - 200 g dry-cured saucisson, sliced
  - 1 jar cornichons
  - 1 jar pickled cocktail onions
  - 1 small jar pickled mushrooms
  - Freshly ground black pepper
  - Sweet paprika and ground caraway, to sprinkle (optional)
  - Fresh fruit (grapes, pear) to serve (optional)
instructions:
  - Boil the unpeeled potatoes in well-salted water for 20 minutes, until tender to a knife. Keep them warm.
  - Heat the raclette grill at the table. Each guest takes a small pan (coupelle), lays in a slice of raclette, and slides it under the heating element.
  - While the cheese melts, halve the warm potatoes onto plates and lay out the charcuterie and pickles.
  - When the cheese is bubbling and golden at the edges, scrape it over a halved potato.
  - Eat with cornichons, pickled onions, and a slice of cured meat. Sprinkle with black pepper, paprika, or caraway to taste.
  - Repeat until satisfied — refill pans, pour more wine, keep going.
---

A slow Swiss winter meal: melt the cheese, scrape it over hot potatoes, eat with pickles and cured meat. The point isn't speed; it's an evening at the table.

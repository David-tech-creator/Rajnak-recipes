ingredients:
  - 600 g beef sirloin or pork loin, cut into thin strips
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil
  - 1 large onion, finely sliced
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 tsp ground cumin
  - 1 tsp sweet paprika
  - 0.5 tsp ground cinnamon
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 dl beef stock
  - 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  - Seeds of 1 pomegranate
  - 3 dl basmati rice
  - 6 dl water
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 tbsp butter
  - 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until it runs clear. Bring the water to a boil with salt and butter, add the rice, cover, and simmer on low for 12 minutes. Rest off the heat 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  - Pat the meat strips dry and season with salt and pepper.
  - Heat the oil in a wide pan over high heat. Sear the meat in two batches so it browns rather than steams — 2 minutes per batch. Set aside.
  - In the same pan, lower the heat to medium. Add the onion and cook 6 minutes until soft and golden. Stir in the garlic, cumin, paprika, and cinnamon and cook 1 minute more.
  - Return the meat to the pan with any juices. Add the soy sauce, stock, and pomegranate molasses. Simmer 5 minutes, until the sauce coats the meat.
  - Pile the rice on a platter, spoon the meat over the top, and scatter generously with pomegranate seeds, mint, and parsley.

Icu's quick, fragrant Levantine-style supper — strips of meat with warm spices, sweet-sour pomegranate, and fluffy basmati. Looks like a feast but takes 45 minutes start to finish.

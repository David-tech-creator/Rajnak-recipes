servings: 8
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 9 slices good white sandwich loaf, crusts off
  - 2 dl sour cream (for the outer cream)
  - 0.5 dl mayonnaise (for the outer cream)
  - 3 eggs, hard-boiled
  - 350 g crayfish tails, peeled and drained
  - 1 dl mayonnaise (for the filling)
  - 1 dl crème fraîche (for the filling)
  - 2 tsp grated horseradish
  - 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  - 1 dl chopped fresh dill, plus more to garnish
  - 200 g cold-smoked salmon
  - 1 small head butter lettuce, leaves separated
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
  - 1 lemon, sliced thin, to garnish
instructions:
  - Lay 3 slices of bread side-by-side on a tray. Using a plate as a template, cut out a single round about 18 cm across. Repeat with the other 6 slices, so you have three round bases.
  - Hard-boil the eggs (9 minutes), cool, peel, and chop fine. Roughly chop the crayfish tails.
  - In a bowl, mix the filling mayonnaise, crème fraîche, horseradish, lemon zest, lemon juice, and chopped dill. Stir in the chopped eggs and crayfish. Season well with salt and pepper.
  - Place the first round on a serving plate. Spread half the crayfish filling. Top with the second round, spread the rest of the filling. Finish with the third round and press down gently.
  - Mix the outer 2 dl sour cream with 0.5 dl mayonnaise and spread thickly over the top and sides of the cake, smoothing into a clean dome.
  - Cover loosely and chill for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight, so the layers settle.
  - Just before serving, decorate the top with cold-smoked salmon roses, thin lemon slices, fresh dill, and butter lettuce leaves around the base.
---

The queen of Swedish sandwich cakes — creamy, majestic, and beautiful. A traditional smörgåstårta is half theatre, half lunch: it has to look stunning at the table, then taste rich and cool when sliced. Make it the day before.

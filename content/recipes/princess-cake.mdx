ingredients:
  - 4 eggs
  - 2 dl caster sugar
  - 1.5 dl plain flour
  - 1 dl potato starch
  - 2 tsp baking powder
  - 3 dl whole milk, for the pastry cream
  - 3 egg yolks
  - 1 dl caster sugar (for the cream)
  - 2 tbsp cornflour
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 5 dl double cream
  - 3 tbsp raspberry jam
  - 500 g green marzipan
  - Icing sugar, for rolling
  - 1 pink marzipan rose, to decorate
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C and line a 24-cm round cake tin with parchment.
  - Whisk the eggs and 2 dl sugar with an electric whisk until pale, thick, and tripled in volume — about 5 minutes. Sift in the flour, potato starch, and baking powder and fold gently. Pour into the tin and bake for 25 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean. Cool completely, then slice horizontally into three layers.
  - For the pastry cream, whisk the egg yolks, 1 dl sugar, cornflour, and vanilla sugar in a saucepan. Pour in the milk and cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until thick. Tip into a bowl, press cling film onto the surface, and chill until cold.
  - Whip the double cream to firm peaks.
  - Place the bottom sponge on a cake board. Spread with raspberry jam, then half the pastry cream. Top with the middle sponge and the rest of the pastry cream. Add the top sponge and cover the whole cake — top and sides — in a thick dome of whipped cream, smoothing into a tall, rounded shape.
  - Roll the green marzipan on icing sugar to a 35-cm circle, about 3 mm thick. Drape it over the cake, smoothing down the sides. Trim the bottom and decorate with the marzipan rose and a dusting of icing sugar.
  - Chill for at least 2 hours before slicing.
---

The Swedish prinsesstårta: layers of sponge, vanilla pastry cream, raspberry jam, and a dome of whipped cream, all wrapped in pale green marzipan. Birthdays, graduations, anything worth celebrating.

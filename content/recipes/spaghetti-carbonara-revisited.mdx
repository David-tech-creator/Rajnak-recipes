---
title: 'Spaghetti Carbonara Revisited with Zucchini & Tomatoes'
date: '2022-02-13'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/spaghetti-carbonara-revisited.jpg
prepTime: '10 mins'
cookTime: '20 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 400 g spaghetti or tagliatelle
  - 200 g pancetta or bacon, in small pieces
  - 2 medium zucchini, sliced 1 cm thick
  - 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 1 beef bouillon cube
  - 3 dl single cream
  - 1 small clove garlic, finely chopped
  - 60 g parmesan, finely grated, plus more to serve
  - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - Fresh basil leaves, to serve (optional)
instructions:
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until just al dente.
  - Meanwhile, heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Fry the pancetta until the fat has rendered and the pieces are crisp.
  - Add the zucchini slices and cook for 4 minutes, until just tender. Add the cherry tomatoes and garlic and cook another 2 minutes.
  - Dissolve the bouillon cube in 0.5 dl hot water and pour into the pan. Add the cream and let it bubble gently for 3 minutes.
  - Drain the pasta, reserving a small cup of water. Tip the spaghetti into the sauce, off the heat, with the parmesan. Toss well, loosening with pasta water if needed.
  - Season generously with black pepper. Serve in shallow bowls with extra parmesan and a few torn basil leaves on top.
---

The family's looser, brighter take on carbonara — cream instead of raw egg, with zucchini and tomatoes thrown in for vegetables. Italian purists will object; everyone eating it will not.

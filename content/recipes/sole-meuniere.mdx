  Sole meunière — "miller's wife's sole" — gets its name from the
  flour dusting, the only nod to the miller. The dish is the
  cornerstone of classical French bistro cooking: a whole Dover
  sole, lightly floured, fried in foaming butter, finished in
  beurre noisette with lemon and parsley.

  Julia Child ate it as her first meal in France, at La Couronne
  in Rouen on 3 November 1948 — "the most exciting meal of my
  life," she later wrote — and credited that single plate with
  setting the course of her career. The brown butter (beurre
  noisette, "hazelnut butter") must come off the heat the moment
  it smells nutty; another five seconds and it's burnt.

ingredients:
  - 4 whole Dover sole (about 300 g each), skinned and trimmed
  - 4 tbsp plain flour, for dusting
  - 100 g unsalted butter, plus 50 g extra to finish
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil
  - 1 lemon, half juiced, half sliced
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  - 1 kg large floury potatoes, cut into 1-cm fries
  - Neutral oil for deep-frying
  - 300 g green beans, trimmed
  - 200 g cherry tomatoes
  - 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  - Flaky sea salt and white pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - For the fries, soak the potato batons in cold water 10 minutes, drain, and dry very well. Heat oil to 150 °C and blanch the chips 6 minutes — they should be soft but not coloured. Lift out and rest while you cook the fish.
  - Blanch the green beans in salted water for 4 minutes, drain, and toss in a pan with a knob of butter, the garlic, and the cherry tomatoes. Cook 3 minutes over medium heat until the tomatoes start to soften. Keep warm.
  - Season the sole on both sides with salt and white pepper. Dust lightly in flour and tap off the excess.
  - Heat 50 g butter with the oil in a large frying pan until foaming and just beginning to colour. Lay in the sole (presentation side down) and fry 3 minutes, until deep golden. Flip carefully and cook another 2 minutes. Lift onto warm plates.
  - Heat the deep-frying oil to 180 °C and finish the fries until crisp and gold. Drain on paper, season with sea salt.
  - Wipe the fish pan, add the remaining butter, and cook over medium-high heat until it foams and smells nutty — beurre noisette. Off the heat, stir in the lemon juice and parsley.
  - Pour the brown butter over the sole. Plate the fries, the bean-and-tomato sauté, and the fish together. Serve with the lemon slices.

The French bistro fish that built a generation of cooks: flour-dusted sole in nutty brown butter with lemon. Julia Child's first meal in Rouen. Worth doing right — buy a whole sole, ask the fishmonger to skin it.

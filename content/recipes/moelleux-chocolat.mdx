ingredients:
  - 100 g dark chocolate (70%), chopped
  - 100 g butter, plus extra for the moulds
  - 100 g caster sugar
  - 2 large eggs
  - 2 large egg yolks
  - 40 g plain flour
  - 1 pinch salt
  - Cocoa powder, for dusting the moulds
  - Icing sugar, to dust
  - Crème fraîche or vanilla ice cream, to serve
  - Fresh raspberries, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Butter four 150-ml ramekins generously, then dust the insides with cocoa powder, tapping out the excess.
  - Melt the chocolate and butter together in a bowl set over barely simmering water, stirring until smooth. Set aside.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk the sugar, eggs, and egg yolks until pale and slightly thickened, about two minutes.
  - Fold the melted chocolate into the egg mixture, then sift in the flour and salt and fold until just combined.
  - Divide the batter between the ramekins, filling them three-quarters full. Place on a tray and bake for exactly 10–12 minutes — the tops should be set with a slight wobble in the centre.
  - Run a knife around each cake, turn out onto a plate, dust with icing sugar, and serve immediately with crème fraîche or ice cream and raspberries.
---

The French bistro dessert that's mostly about timing — bake until the surface is set but the middle still runs when you cut it. Get the ramekins in the oven the moment you sit down to eat.

servings: 4

kitchenNote: 'Two minutes or two hours. Nothing between.'
signoff: 'Buon appetito.'
ingredients:
  - 600 g cleaned squid (tubes and tentacles), cut into rings
  - 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  - 1 small red chilli, finely chopped
  - Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  - 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for cooking
  - 200 g rocket
  - 100 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 1 small fennel bulb, very thinly shaved
  - 1 stalk celery, very thinly sliced
  - A handful of pitted black olives
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  - Sea salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Pat the squid rings and tentacles very dry on kitchen paper — moisture is the enemy of a good sear.
  - Whisk the olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, half the chopped garlic, and half the chilli together. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Toss the rocket, fennel, celery, cherry tomatoes, and olives in a wide salad bowl with most of the dressing.
  - Heat a heavy pan or griddle until smoking hot. Add a little olive oil, then the squid in a single layer. Don't crowd the pan.
  - Cook the squid 60-90 seconds, tossing once. Add the remaining garlic and chilli for the last 20 seconds.
  - Tip the hot squid straight onto the dressed salad. Scatter with parsley and the remaining dressing.
  - Serve immediately with bread to mop up.
---

Squid wants either thirty seconds or thirty minutes — anything in between is rubber. This salad goes for the fast route. Have the salad dressed and waiting before the squid hits the pan.

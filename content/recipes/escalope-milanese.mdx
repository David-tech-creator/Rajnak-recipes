title: 'Escalope Milanese (Rántott Hús) with Potatoes & Vegetables'
date: '2022-01-23'

image: /images/recipes/escalope-milanese.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '40 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 4 veal slices (about 150 g each)
  - 1 dl plain flour
  - 2 eggs, beaten
  - 2 dl fine breadcrumbs
  - 3 dl rapeseed oil, for frying
  - 600 g potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  - 2 parsnips, peeled and cut into batons
  - 3 carrots, peeled and cut into batons
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 tsp salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Lemon wedges, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Toss the potatoes, parsnips and carrots with olive oil, salt and pepper on a baking tray. Roast for 30–35 minutes, turning once, until golden and tender.
  - While the vegetables roast, pound the veal slices between two sheets of cling film until 5 mm thick. Season with salt.
  - Set out three shallow bowls — flour in the first, beaten eggs in the second, breadcrumbs in the third.
  - Dredge each slice first in flour (shake off the excess), then through the egg, then press firmly into the breadcrumbs to coat.
  - Heat the rapeseed oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Fry the schnitzels two at a time for 2–3 minutes per side until deep golden and crisp. Drain on kitchen paper.
  - Serve straight away with the roast vegetables and a wedge of lemon.
---

Hungary's beloved breaded cutlet — "rántott hús" — the Sunday dinner of childhood. Plenty of oil and medium heat is the secret to that signature crackle.

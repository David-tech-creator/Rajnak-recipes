ingredients:
  - 200 g butter, soft
  - 250 g caster sugar
  - 4 large eggs
  - 250 g self-raising flour
  - 1 tsp baking powder
  - 4 tbsp whole milk
  - Zest of 2 unwaxed lemons
  - 'For the lemon syrup:'
  - Juice of 2 lemons
  - 100 g caster sugar
  - 'For the glaze:'
  - 150 g icing sugar
  - 2–3 tbsp lemon juice
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 170 °C. Butter a 1-litre loaf tin and line with baking paper.
  - Cream the butter and sugar in a stand mixer until pale and fluffy, about three minutes.
  - Beat in the eggs one at a time, then fold in the flour, baking powder, milk, and lemon zest until smooth.
  - Pour into the tin and bake for 45–50 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean.
  - While the cake bakes, warm the lemon juice and sugar in a small pan until the sugar dissolves. As soon as the cake is out, prick it all over and spoon over the syrup. Let it soak in as the cake cools.
  - Once cool, whisk the icing sugar with enough lemon juice for a thick pourable glaze. Drizzle over the cake and let set before slicing.

The simple lemon loaf you always wish you had on the counter — buttery, soaked with sharp syrup, and finished with a tangy glaze. Keeps three days in a tin.

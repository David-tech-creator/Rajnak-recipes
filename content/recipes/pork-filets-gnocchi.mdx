kitchenNote: Rest the pork as long as you seared it.
signoff: Tuck in.
difficulty: Medium
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g pork tenderloin, cut into 2-cm medallions
  - 500 g potato gnocchi (fresh or vacuum-packed)
  - 300 g green beans, trimmed
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 shallot, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp plain flour
  - 3 dl beef or veal stock
  - 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (or Worcestershire)
  - 1 tsp redcurrant jelly
  - 1 dl single cream
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
  - Fresh thyme, to serve
instructions:
  - Season the pork medallions generously with salt and pepper. Heat the butter and olive oil in a heavy pan over high heat and sear the pork for 2 minutes per side, until golden but still pink in the middle. Lift onto a warm plate to rest.
  - Reduce the heat and soften the shallot in the same pan for 3 minutes. Sprinkle in the flour, stir for a minute, then whisk in the stock until smooth.
  - Add the soy sauce and redcurrant jelly, simmer for 5 minutes, then pour in the cream. Taste and season. Tip in any resting juices from the pork.
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop in the gnocchi; when they float (about 2 minutes), scoop them out with a slotted spoon.
  - Cook the green beans in the same water for 4 minutes, drain, and toss with a knob of butter and salt.
  - Plate the pork over the gnocchi, beans alongside, and spoon the brown sauce generously over everything. Scatter thyme and serve.
---

A bistro-style supper of seared pork, pillowy gnocchi, and a glossy brown sauce. The redcurrant jelly is what gives the sauce its restaurant depth without any showing-off.

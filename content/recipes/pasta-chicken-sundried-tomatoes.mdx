servings: 4
kitchenNote: 'Soak dry tomatoes in warm water first. Reserve the oil from the jar.'
story: |
  Creamy chicken and sundried-tomato pasta is the dish of the
  1990s — the Italian-American invention that swept European
  weeknight kitchens once jars of pomodori secchi sott'olio
  arrived in every supermarket. It is not strictly Italian:
  in Italy cream and chicken almost never meet pasta. But the
  template is sturdy enough to have outlived the trend.

  Sundried tomatoes are intensely sweet and concentrated; a
  little goes a long way, and they need their oil for the sauce
  to round out properly. The spinach folded in at the end is
  the rule that lifts the dish from heavy to balanced — a green
  vegetable wilted to almost nothing, sharpening the cream.
difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g tagliatelle or pappardelle
  - 2 chicken fillets (about 400 g), cut into bite-size pieces
  - 100 g sundried tomatoes in oil, drained and sliced
  - 200 g baby spinach, washed
  - 4 dl single cream
  - 1 chicken stock cube
  - 2 tbsp butter or olive oil
  - 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  - 50 g parmesan, finely grated, plus more to serve
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil and cook the tagliatelle until just al dente.
  - Meanwhile, heat the butter in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken, season with salt and pepper, and brown for 4 minutes, until golden.
  - Add the garlic and sundried tomatoes and cook for another 2 minutes, until fragrant.
  - Pour in the cream and crumble in the stock cube. Stir until dissolved and let the sauce bubble gently for 3 minutes to thicken.
  - Add the spinach by the handful, letting it wilt into the sauce.
  - Drain the pasta and tip it into the sauce, tossing well with the parmesan. Serve immediately with extra parmesan on top.
---

A creamy weeknight pasta combining tender chicken pieces with sundried tomatoes and fresh spinach. Comes together while the water boils.

title: 'Pesto Salmon Fillet & Crispy Potato Slices'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '25 mins'
servings: 4
signoff: Tuck in.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 4 salmon fillets (about 150 g each), skinless
  - 100 g Philadelphia cream cheese
  - 4 tbsp good green pesto
  - 50 g sundried tomatoes, drained and sliced
  - 800 g waxy potatoes, sliced thin (3 mm)
  - 3 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 tsp flaky sea salt
  - 200 g crème fraîche
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - 1 tbsp lemon juice
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Toss the sliced potatoes with olive oil and sea salt, spread on a baking tray in a single layer, and roast for 25 minutes, turning once, until crisp and golden.
  - Place the salmon fillets in an oiled baking dish. Cut a deep pocket into each fillet without cutting all the way through.
  - Spoon the cream cheese into each pocket, top with a generous layer of pesto, and finish with sundried tomato slices.
  - Bake the salmon at 200 °C for 12–15 minutes, until the fish is just cooked through and flakes at a press.
  - Stir the crème fraîche with the lemon zest and juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Serve the salmon over a bed of crispy potato slices, with the lemon crème fraîche spooned alongside.
---

A dinner-party plate that's secretly very simple: salmon stuffed with pesto and cream cheese, paper-thin potato crisps roasted in oil, and a bright lemon sauce to cut through. Family favourite for Sunday evenings.

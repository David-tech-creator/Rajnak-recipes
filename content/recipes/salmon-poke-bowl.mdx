story: |
  Poke (pronounced poh-KAY) is Hawaiian — from the Hawaiian verb
  meaning "to slice" or "to cut crosswise" — and started as
  fishermen's food: trim cubes of just-caught reef fish, salt,
  seaweed, kukui nut. The Japanese plantation workers brought
  soy sauce and sesame in the late nineteenth century, and the
  modern poke bowl was born.

  The diaspora version that swept the mainland (and Europe) in
  the 2010s added rice, avocado, mango, and any number of
  toppings the original would not recognise — but the rule still
  holds: the raw fish must be exceptional, and it must be eaten
  the same day it's cut.

ingredients:
  - 300 g sushi-grade salmon fillet, skinless
  - 3 tbsp Japanese soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp sesame oil
  - 1 tbsp sweet soy (ketjap manis) or honey
  - 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  - 1 small clove garlic, grated
  - 2 dl sushi rice
  - 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - 1 ripe mango, diced
  - 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  - 1 dl shelled edamame beans
  - 1/2 cucumber, sliced thin
  - 2 spring onions, sliced
  - 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  - Pickled ginger and a sheet of nori, to serve (optional)
instructions:
  - Rinse the rice until the water runs clear. Cook with 2.5 dl water — bring to a boil, reduce to low, cover, and simmer for 12 minutes. Off the heat, leave covered another 10 minutes.
  - Warm the rice vinegar with the sugar and a pinch of salt until dissolved. Fold gently into the cooked rice and let cool to lukewarm.
  - Boil the edamame for 4 minutes in salted water, drain, and cool.
  - Whisk the soy, sesame oil, sweet soy, ginger, and garlic in a bowl. Dice the salmon into 1.5-cm cubes and gently turn through the marinade.
  - Divide the rice between two deep bowls. Arrange small piles of salmon, mango, avocado, edamame, and cucumber on top.
  - Scatter with spring onions and sesame seeds. Add pickled ginger and torn nori on the side if using.

A Hawaiian-Japanese bowl that has crossed every ocean: sweet mango, buttery salmon, salty soy, and cool rice. Everything except the salmon can be prepped in advance.

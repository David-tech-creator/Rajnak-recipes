ingredients:
  - 1 large pizza dough (about 350 g)
  - 1.5 dl pizza tomato sauce
  - 4 dl coarsely grated pizza cheese or Emmentaler
  - 200 g shredded kebab meat, pan-fried
  - 1 yellow onion, thinly sliced
  - 3 pickled pepperoncini, sliced
  - 1 ripe tomato, sliced
  - 1 tsp dried oregano
  - A few fresh basil leaves, to finish
  - 2 dl Greek yogurt
  - 1 clove garlic, grated
  - 2 tbsp lemon juice
  - 1 tsp sriracha or other hot chili sauce, to taste
  - Salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Heat the oven as hot as it will go, ideally 275 °C, with a pizza stone or heavy baking sheet inside.
  - Stretch the dough thin on a piece of baking parchment. Spread the tomato sauce to the edges and scatter the cheese over.
  - Distribute the fried kebab meat, sliced onion, tomato, and pepperoncini evenly across the pizza.
  - Slide the pizza onto the hot stone and bake for 8 to 12 minutes, until the base is crisp and the cheese is golden and bubbling at the edges.
  - Meanwhile whisk together the yogurt, garlic, lemon juice, sriracha, salt, and pepper for the garlic-chili sauce.
  - Sprinkle the hot pizza with oregano and fresh basil, then drizzle generously with the garlic-chili sauce just before serving.
---

The late-night classic of every Swedish pizzeria — invented in the 1990s and now the most-ordered pizza in the country. The yogurt-garlic sauce on top is what separates it from the rest.

ingredients:
  - 4 live lobsters (about 500–600 g each)
  - 3 l water
  - 1 dl coarse sea salt
  - 1 small bunch dill, plus more to serve
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 1 lemon, cut in wedges
  - 'For the herb butter:'
  - 200 g butter
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 2 tbsp finely chopped parsley
  - 1 tbsp finely chopped chives
  - Sea salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Good crusty bread, to serve
instructions:
  - Bring the water to a rolling boil in a large pot. Add the salt, dill, and sugar.
  - Lower the lobsters head-first into the boiling water. Cover, return to the boil, and cook for 8 minutes for a 500 g lobster (10 for 600 g).
  - Lift the lobsters out with tongs and let them rest on a board for two minutes.
  - For the butter, melt the butter gently in a small saucepan, skim any foam, and stir in the garlic, parsley, chives, salt, and pepper.
  - Split each lobster lengthwise with a heavy knife and crack the claws. Arrange on a big platter with lemon wedges and extra dill.
  - Serve immediately with little pots of warm herb butter and bread for mopping.
---

The Swedish August feast — lobsters cooked simply in salty dill water, cracked at the table, dipped in garlicky herb butter. The only fuss is buying them alive that morning.

---
title: Asparagus Soup
date: '2022-03-20'

image: /images/recipes/asparagus-soup.jpg
prepTime: '10 mins'
cookTime: '25 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 700 g green asparagus, woody ends snapped off
  - 1 large shallot, finely chopped
  - 1 small leek, white part only, sliced
  - 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  - 30 g butter
  - 1 l vegetable stock, hot
  - 1.5 dl double cream
  - 1 tsp sea salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - A squeeze of lemon juice
  - 2 tbsp chopped chives, to serve
instructions:
  - Cut the tips off the asparagus and set them aside. Slice the rest of the stalks into 2-cm pieces.
  - Melt the butter in a heavy pot. Sweat the shallot and leek over low heat until soft and translucent, about 6 minutes.
  - Add the asparagus pieces and diced potato. Stir to coat in the butter.
  - Pour in the hot stock, bring to a simmer, and cook gently for 15 minutes until the vegetables are very tender.
  - Meanwhile, blanch the reserved asparagus tips in salted water for 90 seconds, then refresh in cold water.
  - Blend the soup until completely smooth. Pass through a fine sieve back into the pot for a silkier finish.
  - Stir in the cream, season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon. Reheat gently without boiling.
  - Ladle into bowls, scatter with the blanched tips and chopped chives.
---

A silky, elegant soup that announces spring. Use green asparagus when it's in season — thin stalks are best. The blanched tips give it texture and prove what you started with.

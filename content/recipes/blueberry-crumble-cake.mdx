title: Blueberry Crumble Cake with Vanilla Ice Cream
date: '2026-05-23'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '25 mins'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g blueberries (or raspberries)
  - 1 dl caster sugar (for the berries)
  - Zest and juice of 2 limes (optional)
  - "For the crumble:"
  - 2.25 dl plain flour
  - 3 dl rolled oats
  - 150 g butter, at room temperature
  - 1.25 dl caster sugar
  - 1 tsp ground cardamom
  - 1 pinch of salt
  - "To serve:"
  - 4 generous scoops good vanilla ice cream
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 180°C.
  - Toss the berries with 1 dl sugar and the lime zest and juice in an ovenproof dish.
  - In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, sugar, cardamom, and salt. Add the butter and rub it in with your fingertips until you have a rough, clumpy crumble — not a smooth dough.
  - Taste a small piece — it should be sweet enough; add a touch more sugar if not.
  - Scatter the crumble evenly over the berries, leaving the top loose and shaggy.
  - Bake on the middle rack for about 20-25 minutes, until the top is deep golden and the berry juices bubble up around the edges.
  - Rest for 5 minutes and serve warm with vanilla ice cream melting into it.
---

Cardamom is the Swedish twist that lifts this from generic to memorable. Use whatever berries are in season — blueberries, raspberries, even half-and-half with rhubarb. The crumble should be loose and uneven so some bits stay sandy and others go crisp.

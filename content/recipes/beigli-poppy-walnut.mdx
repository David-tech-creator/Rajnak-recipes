  - ½ piece of lemon zest (or untreated bio)

  - 1 pcs egg yolk
  - 1.2 dl of milk
  - 1 pcs eggs (the lekenéshez)

1. Mix the flour with the salt, yeast (if fresh, run it in the milk first and add it later), powdered sugar and grated lemon

zest. Add the butter and fat and crumble with the flour. Once the crumbs are pea, add the eggs with the milk and work

together to form a homogeneous dough. Knead further on the board until nice tight and put in the fridge for 1 hour when

ready. Tip: At this point, you can spend up to 48 hours in the fridge with the dough. This way you can make it earlier.

2. Until then, we make the fillings. Mix the minced walnuts with the powdered sugar and the grated lemon zest, then

add enough milk to keep the walnuts moist and creamy, but do not allow the milk to flow or stand under it. Prepare the

poppy seed filling in another bowl in the same way. That is, first mix the poppy seeds with the sugar and lemon zest, then

slowly add enough milk to keep it moist but not churning in the milk.

3. After cooling, cut the dough into 6 equal parts and roll out dumplings. Lined with 2 pieces of 30 x 10 cm loaf of bread

crosswise with baking paper. Extend the first dough to the same size as the shape and place it on the bottom. Smooth

the walnut filling over it and sprinkle with raisins to taste. Stretch the next dough and flatten the top of the filling. Cover

with the remaining filling and raisins, then stretch out a third dough and close the row. Make the poppy seeds in the

same way. That is, spread a thinly stretched dough on the bottom of the mold. Smooth half of the poppy seed filling over

it, sprinkle with raisins to taste, and then cover with a thin sheet of dough. This can be done with the remaining poppy

seed filling and the last sheet of dough.

4. One egg is separated and the two loaves are lightly greased with the yolk and left in the refrigerator to dry and rise for

45 minutes. When the time is up and dry to the touch, we smear it lightly with the protein and let it rise for another 45

minutes, but this time on the kitchen table. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 200 degrees. Before baking, prick the loaf

with a toothpick and bake them for a nice golden brown in 25-30 minutes. Both can fit side by side at the same time.

For the walnut filling:

• 20 dkg walnuts (ground)

• 12 dkg of vanilla powdered sugar

• ¼ piece lemon zest

• 6-7 tablespoons milk

• 5 dkg raisins (to taste)

For the poppy seed filling:

• 20 dkg poppy seeds (ground)

• 12 dkg powdered sugar

• ¼ piece lemon zest

• 6-7 tablespoons milk

• 5 dkg raisins (to taste)

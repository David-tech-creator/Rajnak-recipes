---
title: "Beigli (Poppy Seed & Walnut)"
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Christmas & Easter
image: /images/recipes/beigli-poppy-walnut.jpg
prepTime: '1 h (plus 1 h chilling)'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 16
featured: false
ingredients:
  - "For the dough:"
  - 150 g bread flour
  - 125 g plain flour
  - ½ tsp salt
  - 8 g dried yeast (or 30 g fresh)
  - 2 tbsp icing sugar
  - Zest of ½ untreated lemon
  - 100 g cold butter, cubed
  - 40 g cold lard (or extra butter)
  - 1 egg yolk
  - 1.2 dl milk, lukewarm
  - 1 whole egg, beaten, for glazing
  - "For the walnut filling:"
  - 200 g walnuts, finely ground
  - 120 g vanilla-sugar (or sugar + 1 tsp vanilla)
  - Zest of ¼ lemon
  - 6-7 tbsp milk
  - 50 g raisins
  - "For the poppy seed filling:"
  - 200 g poppy seeds, ground
  - 120 g icing sugar
  - Zest of ¼ lemon
  - 6-7 tbsp milk
  - 50 g raisins
instructions:
  - Whisk the two flours with salt, dried yeast, icing sugar, and lemon zest. Rub in the cold butter and lard with your fingertips until it looks like fine crumbs. Add the egg yolk and milk and bring it together into a smooth dough. Wrap and chill at least 1 hour.
  - For each filling, stir the nuts (or poppy seeds) with the sugar and lemon zest, then add milk a tablespoon at a time until just moist and spreadable, never sloppy.
  - Divide the dough into 6 equal pieces. On a lightly floured board, roll each into a rectangle about 30 x 20 cm.
  - For each loaf, layer three sheets of dough with filling and raisins between them, finishing with dough on top. Tuck the ends under and place seam-side down in a baking-paper-lined tin.
  - Brush the loaves lightly with egg yolk and rest, uncovered, in the fridge for 45 minutes until dry to the touch.
  - Brush with egg white and rest at room temperature another 45 minutes. Meanwhile, heat the oven to 200°C.
  - Prick the loaves all over with a toothpick (this is the secret — it prevents cracks). Bake for 25-30 minutes, until deep golden brown.
  - Cool completely before slicing. Slice thin to show off the dark spiral inside.
---

The Hungarian Christmas pastry: a tight spiral of buttery dough wrapped around dark poppy seed and walnut fillings. Annuskam's recipe — the pricking is non-negotiable. Both loaves bake side by side; one of each filling per family.

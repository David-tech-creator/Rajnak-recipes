title: Variety of Ceviche
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/ceviche-variety.jpg
prepTime: '25 mins'
cookTime: '0 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g very fresh white fish fillet (sea bass, bream, or hake), skin off, pin-boned
  - 200 g raw peeled prawns, halved lengthwise
  - 200 g sashimi-grade salmon, diced (for a third variety)
  - Juice of 8 limes
  - Juice of 2 lemons
  - 1 small red onion, very thinly sliced
  - 1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  - 1 small cucumber, peeled and finely diced
  - 1 ripe mango, diced (for the prawn version)
  - 1 ripe avocado, diced (for the salmon version)
  - A small bunch coriander, chopped
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - Sea salt
instructions:
  - Cut the white fish into 1.5-cm cubes and divide between three chilled bowls along with the prawns and salmon.
  - Soak the sliced red onion in iced water for 5 minutes, then drain — this takes the bite out.
  - Pour about a third of the lime and lemon juice over each bowl, season with salt, and stir gently.
  - Leave the white fish to cure 8-10 minutes (it should turn opaque); the prawn 5 minutes; the salmon just 2 minutes — you want it still rosy.
  - Drain off most of the citrus juice from each bowl, leaving the fish just glazed.
  - White fish ceviche: stir in red onion, chilli, cucumber, coriander, and a little olive oil.
  - Prawn ceviche: stir in mango, half the coriander, and a little olive oil.
  - Salmon ceviche: stir in avocado, the rest of the coriander, and olive oil.
  - Serve at once on chilled plates, three small piles per person, with tortilla chips or plantain crisps.
---

Three colours, three textures, one tasting plate. The fish must be top quality and very fresh — there's nowhere to hide. Cure times matter: white fish wants longer than salmon. Make and eat within thirty minutes.

story: |
  Töltött paprika — stuffed paprika — is one of the great everyday
  dishes of the Hungarian kitchen, alongside pörkölt and gulyás. The
  pale-green, almost white Hungarian wax pepper (TV paprika) is the
  right vessel: thin-walled, mild, and just bitter enough to balance
  the sweet tomato sauce.

  The dish requires two parallel acts of restraint. The meat filling is
  loose, not packed, so the rice can swell and the texture stays
  tender. The sauce is finished with a flour-and-water slurry, not a
  roux, and seasoned with a surprising spoonful of sugar — the sweetness
  is what makes it Hungarian rather than Italian.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 8 medium Hungarian wax peppers or pale green bell peppers
  - 500 g minced pork (or pork and beef mix)
  - 100 g long-grain rice, half-cooked
  - 1 yellow onion, finely chopped
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 large egg
  - 1 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 tsp salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 1 tbsp lard or neutral oil
  - 1 l passata or thick tomato sauce
  - 2 tbsp sugar
  - 1 tbsp plain flour
  - 1 dl cold water
  - Sour cream, to serve
instructions:
  - Slice the tops off the peppers and remove the seeds and white ribs, keeping the peppers whole.
  - In a bowl, mix the minced meat with the rice, half the chopped onion, garlic, egg, paprika, salt, and a few grinds of pepper. Knead together with clean hands until evenly combined.
  - Stuff each pepper firmly with the meat mixture, leaving a small dome on top.
  - In a wide, heavy pot, gently sauté the remaining onion in the lard until softened. Pour in the passata and 5 dl water, add the sugar and a pinch of salt, and bring to a simmer.
  - Lower the stuffed peppers carefully into the tomato sauce, cover, and simmer gently for about 50 minutes, until the peppers are tender and the meat is cooked through.
  - Whisk the flour into the cold water and stir into the sauce. Simmer another 5 minutes until the sauce is glossy and thickened. Serve with a spoonful of sour cream and good bread to mop the sauce.
---

A weeknight classic from every Hungarian kitchen. The sweet tomato sauce is just as important as the filling — keep some bread on hand to finish it.

ingredients:
  - 8 slices day-old white bread (preferably brioche or wienerbröd)
  - 3 dl warm milk
  - 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 0.5 tsp ground cinnamon
  - Pinch of salt
  - Neutral oil or butter, for frying
  - Lingonberry jam or strawberry jam, to serve
  - Icing sugar, for dusting
instructions:
  - Whisk the eggs with the sugar, vanilla sugar, cinnamon and salt.
  - Pour the warm milk into a shallow dish.
  - Dip each bread slice briefly in the warm milk, just enough to soak it through without falling apart.
  - Transfer to the egg mixture and turn to coat both sides.
  - Heat a generous slick of oil or butter in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Fry the bread slices two at a time until deeply golden, about 1–2 minutes per side.
  - Drain briefly on kitchen paper, dust with icing sugar and serve immediately with a spoon of jam.
---

The Swedish "Poor Knights" — what to do with a half-stale loaf and a quiet Sunday morning. Eat them straight off the pan, never reheated.

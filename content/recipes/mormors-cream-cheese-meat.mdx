  A Swedish home-cook casserole that hides the work of a Sunday roast
  inside a baking dish: seared pork fillet, sweet onions, rice, and a

  The crispy rice on the bottom is the prize. Use a wide glass dish

  layer below catches and crisps. Slice the pork thin and sear hard —
  it finishes cooking in the oven, so you only want colour at this
  stage, not doneness.

ingredients:
  - 2 large pork fillets (about 1.4 kg total)
  - 1 kg long-grain rice
  - 2 large yellow onions
  - 2 meat bouillon cubes
  - 8 dl sour cream or crème fraîche (4 × 2 dl)
  - 3 tbsp rapeseed oil, for searing
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Cook the rice with the crumbled bouillon cubes in plenty of water until just tender. Drain.
  - Slice the pork fillets into 7–8 mm thick rounds. Beat each lightly with a meat hammer.
  - Salt the meat slices all over. Heat the oil in a wide frying pan over high heat and sear the meat in batches until just coloured on both sides — don't cook through.
  - Lay the seared slices in one or two low glass baking dishes. Season with black pepper.
  - Slice the onions into thin rings and lay over the meat. Cover everything with the cooked rice and season the rice with a little more salt.
  - Smear the sour cream over the top in an even layer. Bake at 220 °C for 15–20 minutes, until the surface is golden and lightly bubbling.

Mormor's Sunday-lunch trick — seared pork hidden under sweet onion, rice, and a thick crème fraîche crust that goes golden in the oven. The rice underneath catches all the meat juices.

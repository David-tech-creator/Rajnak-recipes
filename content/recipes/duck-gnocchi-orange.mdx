title: 'Duck with Gnocchi, Brown Sauce & Orange Slices'

ingredients:
  - 4 duck breasts (about 200 g each)
  - 500 g fresh gnocchi
  - 2 oranges
  - 2 dl chicken or veal stock
  - 1 dl red wine
  - 1 tbsp butter
  - 1 tbsp flour
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Score the skin of the duck breasts in a cross-hatch pattern, season with salt and pepper. Place skin-side down in a cold dry pan, then bring up to medium heat. Render the fat for 8–10 minutes until the skin is deep golden.
  - Flip and cook another 4 minutes for medium-rare. Move to a board and rest, loosely tented, for 8 minutes.
  - Peel one orange, cut into supremes. Juice the other orange.
  - In the duck pan, drain off most of the fat. Add butter, sprinkle the flour and stir for a minute. Pour in the wine, stock, orange juice, soy sauce, and sugar. Simmer until glossy and slightly thick.
  - Cook the gnocchi in plenty of salted water until they float — about 2 minutes — then drain and toss with a splash of olive oil.
  - Slice the rested duck. Plate over the gnocchi, spoon over the orange sauce, and garnish with the orange supremes.
---

A small homage to duck à l'orange but quick enough for a weeknight. The pink duck against the bright orange and the soft pillows of gnocchi is what makes it.

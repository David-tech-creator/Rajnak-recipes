  A composed warm salad in the looser Italian-Californian style that
  became standard in European home kitchens through the 1990s and
  2000s. The structure — grilled protein laid over sliced fruit and

  The aged balsamic is the part worth not skipping. Cheap supermarket
  balsamic is sharp and one-note; a proper aged Modena balsamic
  thickens, sweetens, and clings to the avocado. Pound the chicken
  even — uneven thickness is the difference between juicy and dry.

ingredients:
  - 4 chicken breast fillets
  - 2 ripe avocados
  - 4 ripe tomatoes
  - 3 tbsp olive oil
  - 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar (a good aged one)
  - 1 small bunch fresh basil, leaves only
  - 1 tsp flaky sea salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Pound the chicken breasts to an even 1.5-cm thickness. Season with salt, pepper and 1 tbsp olive oil.
  - Heat a griddle pan or grill over medium-high. Grill the chicken 5 minutes per side, until charred outside and just cooked through. Rest 5 minutes.
  - While the chicken rests, slice the avocados and tomatoes.
  - Arrange the avocado and tomato slices on each plate, slightly overlapping.
  - Slice the rested chicken on the bias and lay across the avocado and tomato.
  - Drizzle generously with the remaining olive oil and the balsamic vinegar, scatter the basil leaves and flaky salt over the top, and finish with a grind of pepper.

A summer plate that always looks better than the work would suggest. Use a real aged balsamic — the cheap stuff falls flat against the creamy avocado.

title: Avocado Mousse with Shrimp
date: '2022-01-19'

image: /images/recipes/avocado-mousse-shrimp.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '0 mins'
servings: 4
difficulty: Easy
story: |
  Räkor — small, sweet, hand-peeled cold-water shrimp — are a Swedish
  obsession. They appear on smörgås, in skagenröra, and any time a
  cook wants to dress a starter up without fuss. The fjord and North
  Sea varieties are the gold standard; never substitute pink salad
  prawns.

  Pairing them with avocado is a 1970s Scandinavian dinner-party
  flourish that never quite went out of fashion. The mousse stays
  bright if you don't skip the lemon, and the shells make the plate
  do the work.
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Handpicked shrimp only. Never frozen rings.'
signoff: 'Smaklig måltid.'
ingredients:
  - 2 large ripe avocados
  - 2 dl sour cream
  - 200 g cooked peeled shrimp (handpicked North Sea or fjord shrimp if you can)
  - 1 tbsp lemon juice
  - 1 tsp sea salt
  - Freshly ground black pepper
  - A pinch of cayenne (optional)
  - 1 tbsp chopped dill, for garnish
  - 4 sprigs dill, whole, for garnish
instructions:
  - Halve the avocados lengthwise and remove the stones. Scoop the flesh into a blender, keeping the shells intact for serving.
  - Add the sour cream, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and cayenne if using.
  - Blend until completely smooth and pale green.
  - Taste and adjust salt and lemon.
  - Spoon the mousse back into the four reserved avocado shells, mounding it slightly.
  - Pile the shrimp generously on top of each one.
  - Garnish with chopped dill, a whole sprig, and a final twist of black pepper. Serve at once.
---

A starter Mamma made when guests were coming and there wasn't much time. The trick is good shrimp — small, hand-peeled, sweet — and just enough lemon to keep the avocado bright.

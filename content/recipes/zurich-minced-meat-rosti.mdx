---
title: Zürich Minced Meat with Rösti (Zürcher Geschnetzeltes)
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/zurich-minced-meat-rosti.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '40 mins'
servings: 4
kitchenNote: Squeeze the potato dry. Sauce off the heat.
signoff: En guete.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g floury potatoes, scrubbed
  - 60 g butter
  - 0.5 tsp salt
  - 400 g veal escalope, cut into 1 cm wide strips
  - 100 g veal kidney, trimmed and sliced, optional
  - 1 tbsp plain flour, for dusting
  - 50 g butter, for the pan
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 200 g button mushrooms, thinly sliced
  - 1 tsp lemon juice
  - 1 dl dry white wine
  - 2 dl single cream
  - 1 dl beef or veal stock
  - 1 tbsp cornflour
  - 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Boil the potatoes in their skins until just tender — about 20 minutes. Drain and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, until cool enough to handle (ideally chill overnight). Peel and coarsely grate.
  - Heat the 60 g butter in a wide non-stick pan. Add the grated potato and season with salt. Press into a flat cake and cook over medium heat for about 12 minutes until deep golden underneath, then flip and cook the other side for another 10 minutes.
  - Pat the veal dry, season with salt and pepper, and dust lightly with flour. Heat a knob of butter in a large frying pan until foaming. Brown the meat (and kidney, if using) in batches for 2 to 3 minutes — don't crowd the pan. Remove and keep warm in a 60 °C oven.
  - In the same pan with a little more butter, soften the chopped onion. Add the mushrooms with the lemon juice and cook 2 minutes until they release their water.
  - Pour in the white wine and reduce by half. Whisk the cornflour into the cream with the stock and pour into the pan. Simmer 3 minutes until the sauce is velvety. Season.
  - Return the meat and any juices to the pan, fold through half the parsley, and warm through just until heated — do not boil or the veal will toughen. Slide the rösti onto a board and cut into wedges. Serve the geschnetzeltes alongside, scattered with remaining parsley.
---

The Zürich signature: tender strips of veal in a white wine and cream sauce, served with a golden rösti. The Betty Bossi version of a dish every Swiss home cook owns by heart.

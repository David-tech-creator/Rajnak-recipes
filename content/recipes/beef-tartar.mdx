ingredients:
  - 500 g beef fillet, very cold, finely hand-chopped
  - 2 small shallots, very finely diced
  - 2 tbsp small capers, drained
  - 2 tbsp cornichons, finely diced
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  - A few drops Tabasco
  - 4 very fresh egg yolks
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp chives, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp good olive oil
  - Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - Toast or fresh baguette, to serve
instructions:
  - Use only the freshest, best-quality beef and a very sharp knife. Trim off any sinew, then chop the fillet by hand into a fine, even dice — do not mince.
  - Keep the chopped beef cold while you prepare everything else.
  - Combine the shallots, capers, cornichons, mustard, Worcestershire, Tabasco, herbs, and olive oil in a chilled bowl.
  - Fold the chopped beef into the seasoning mix gently with a fork. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  - Divide the tartare between four chilled plates, shape into neat rounds, and make a small well in the centre of each.
  - Slide an egg yolk carefully into each well.
  - Serve at once with thin toast or warm baguette.
---

Use only sashimi-grade beef from a butcher you trust. Everything must be cold — the bowl, the knife, the plates. Mix at the table just before the first bite.

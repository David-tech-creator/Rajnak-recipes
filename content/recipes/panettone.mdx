---
title: Panettone
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Christmas & Easter
image: /images/recipes/panettone.jpg
prepTime: '40 mins'
cookTime: '40 mins'
servings: 8
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g strong white bread flour
  - 100 g caster sugar
  - 14 g instant dry yeast (2 sachets)
  - 1 tsp fine salt
  - 4 large egg yolks
  - 2 whole eggs
  - 200 ml lukewarm milk
  - 150 g unsalted butter, softened
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - Zest of 1 orange
  - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - 100 g raisins, soaked in warm rum for 30 minutes
  - 100 g mixed candied peel, finely chopped
  - 1 egg, beaten, for the glaze
instructions:
  - Whisk the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Add the egg yolks, whole eggs, milk, lemon zest, orange zest, and vanilla, and bring together into a sticky dough.
  - Knead in a stand mixer with a dough hook for 10 minutes, then add the softened butter a tablespoon at a time, kneading until the dough is smooth, glossy, and elastic — about another 10 minutes.
  - Cover and leave to rise in a warm place for 2 hours, until doubled.
  - Drain the raisins. Tip the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently work in the raisins and candied peel.
  - Shape into a tight ball and drop it into a panettone paper mould (about 15 cm wide). Leave to prove for another 2 hours, until risen well above the rim.
  - Brush the top with beaten egg, cut a cross in the surface, and bake at 170 °C for 40–45 minutes. Cover loosely with foil if it browns too quickly.
  - Skewer two long wooden sticks through the base and hang the panettone upside down to cool — this stops the dome collapsing. Leave for at least 4 hours before slicing.
---

The Italian Christmas bread that takes patience but rewards you with something far better than anything in a box. Tear into wedges with a glass of Vin Santo on Christmas morning.

story: |
  The composed rainbow salad is a French bistro form — the salade
  composée arranged in segments on a plate rather than tossed
  together — pulled into the modern Instagram-era kitchen. Each
  vegetable keeps its colour and texture; the dressing is shaken
  on at the table. The structure is the same one Escoffier used
  for niçoise.

  Raw beetroot is the ingredient worth talking about: shaved or
  grated thin, it stays crunchy and earthy, nothing like the cooked
  version most people grow up on. Pomegranate seeds for jewels,
  pumpkin seeds for crunch, feta for the salty cap — the rule of
  any composed salad is that every forkful should give you three
  different things at once.
difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 small head Romaine lettuce, torn
  - 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced thin
  - 1 small red onion, sliced thin
  - 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 1 small cucumber, sliced
  - 1 ripe avocado, cubed
  - 100 g feta cheese, crumbled
  - 1 small carrot, shaved into ribbons
  - 1 small raw beetroot, grated
  - A handful pomegranate seeds
  - A handful toasted pumpkin seeds
  - 'For the dressing:'
  - 4 tbsp olive oil
  - 1.5 tbsp lemon juice
  - 1 tsp honey
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - In a small jar, shake together the olive oil, lemon juice, honey, mustard, salt, and pepper until emulsified.
  - Spread the torn lettuce over a wide platter as a base.
  - Arrange the bell pepper, red onion, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, avocado, carrot ribbons, and grated beetroot in colourful piles on top.
  - Scatter the feta over the salad.
  - Finish with the pomegranate seeds and toasted pumpkin seeds.
  - Drizzle the dressing over just before serving so the leaves stay crisp.
---

Margaux's go-to summer salad — every colour you can find on a plate, dressed lightly with lemon and honey. Photogenic, but it's mostly a way to use up whatever's in the drawer.

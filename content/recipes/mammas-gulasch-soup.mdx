ingredients:
  - 300 g beef chuck, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 2 yellow onions, chopped
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1–2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 150 g carrots, sliced
  - 1 parsnip, diced
  - 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  - 1 medium tomato, chopped
  - 1.5 l water
  - 1 Knorr beef bouillon cube (per 0.5 l water)
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - A handful csipet tarhonya (pinched egg pasta)
  - Chopped parsley, to serve
instructions:
  - Cut the beef into 2-cm cubes. Chop the onions.
  - Heat the oil in a heavy pot or pressure cooker and fry the onions over medium heat until lightly coloured, about 8 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika so it doesn't burn. Add the beef and stir to coat. Season with a little salt.
  - Pour in the water, crumble the bouillon, and add the carrots, parsnip, potatoes, and tomato.
  - Simmer covered for about an hour until the meat is tender (or 30–40 minutes in a pressure cooker after coming up to pressure).
  - Once tender, stir in the csipet tarhonya and cook another 5 minutes until they float. Finish with parsley and serve.
---

The soup that turns up at every family gathering — beef and paprika slow-cooked until soft, then little pinched egg pasta thrown in at the end. Better the next day.

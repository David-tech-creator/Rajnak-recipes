story: |
  Sachertorte was invented in 1832 by a sixteen-year-old
  apprentice, Franz Sacher, when the Viennese court chef of
  Prince Metternich fell ill and the dessert had to be improvised
  for important guests. The cake became famous; the recipe
  became contested. Hotel Sacher and Demel pastry shop fought a
  seven-year lawsuit in the 1950s — the "Sweet Seven Years'
  War" — over who owned the title "Original Sachertorte." The
  hotel won.

  The structure is precise: a dense, slightly dry chocolate
  sponge — almost a brownie — split and sealed in apricot jam,
  glazed in a hard chocolate icing poured (never spread) in a
  single motion. The dryness of the cake is by design: it asks
  for the mound of unsweetened whipped cream served alongside,
  which is what makes the slice eat right.

  Vienna, with a strong coffee. The way it's done.

ingredients:
  - 140 g good dark chocolate (55-60% cocoa), chopped
  - 140 g unsalted butter, softened
  - 110 g icing sugar
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 6 eggs, separated, at room temperature
  - 110 g caster sugar
  - 140 g plain flour, sifted
  - Pinch of salt
  - 200 g good apricot jam
  - 200 g dark chocolate (for the glaze)
  - 250 g caster sugar (for the glaze)
  - 1.5 dl water (for the glaze)
  - Whipped cream, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 170 °C. Butter a 22-cm springform tin and line the base with parchment.
  - Melt the chopped chocolate in a bowl over barely simmering water and let it cool slightly.
  - Beat the butter, icing sugar, and vanilla sugar with an electric whisk until very pale and fluffy. Beat in the egg yolks one at a time, then fold in the melted chocolate.
  - In a clean bowl, whisk the egg whites with the salt to soft peaks. Add the caster sugar gradually, whisking to firm, glossy peaks.
  - Fold a third of the whites into the chocolate mixture to loosen, then fold in the rest, alternating with the sifted flour. Pour into the tin and bake for 50 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean.
  - Cool the cake completely. Slice horizontally in half. Warm the apricot jam, pass through a sieve, and spread between the layers and all over the outside of the cake. Chill 30 minutes to set the apricot glaze.
  - For the chocolate glaze, simmer the sugar and water for 5 minutes until syrupy. Remove from the heat, stir in the chopped chocolate until smooth and glossy, and let it cool to just-warm.
  - Set the cake on a rack over a tray and pour the glaze in one steady motion over the centre, letting it run down the sides. Don't smooth with a knife — let gravity do the work. Set at room temperature.
  - Serve in thin wedges with a mound of unsweetened whipped cream — the Vienna way.
---

The Vienna original from Hotel Sacher, 1832 — a dense dark chocolate cake masked with apricot jam and finished in a hard chocolate glaze. The unsweetened whipped cream isn't decoration; it's structurally necessary.

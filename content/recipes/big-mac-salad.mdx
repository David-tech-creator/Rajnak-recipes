ingredients:
  - 1 large iceberg lettuce, diced
  - 500 g beef mince (15% fat)
  - 1 yellow onion, finely diced
  - 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  - 150 g sharp cheddar, grated
  - 1 tsp neutral oil, for frying
  - Sea salt and black pepper
  - "Big Mac sauce:"
  - 1 dl mayonnaise
  - 0.5 dl sour cream
  - 0.5 dl Greek yoghurt
  - 1 tbsp tomato paste
  - 1 tsp Sriracha
  - 1 tsp honey
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 4 small gherkins, finely diced
  - 1 tbsp gherkin brine
  - Sesame seeds, to serve
instructions:
  - Stir together the mayonnaise, sour cream, yoghurt, tomato paste, Sriracha, honey, mustard, diced gherkins, and brine. Set aside so the flavours meld.
  - Heat the oil in a large pan over medium-high. Brown the mince, breaking it up, until no liquid is left.
  - Season with salt and pepper. Push the meat to one side and soften the onion and garlic in the same pan for 2 minutes, then mix everything together.
  - Pull the pan off the heat and scatter the grated cheddar over the hot meat so it just melts.
  - Pile the diced iceberg into bowls.
  - Spoon the hot meat-and-cheese over the lettuce.
  - Drizzle generously with the Big Mac sauce and finish with sesame seeds.
---

Everything you want from a Big Mac, none of the bun. The sauce is the whole point — make extra. Eat right away while the cheese is still molten over cold crunchy lettuce.

story: |
  Tiramisù means, roughly, "pick me up" — a reference to the coffee
  and the sugar that wake you back up after a long lunch. The dish was
  almost certainly invented in the Veneto in the 1960s or 1970s, with
  competing claims from Treviso (Le Beccherie restaurant, 1972) and
  Friuli; Italy now grants it protected traditional status.

  This version is built on Maida Heatter's method — the great American
  dessert writer's approach of cooking the egg yolks over a double
  boiler with sugar, which sterilises the eggs and gives the cream a
  pale, mousse-like lift. Folding in whipped cream and whipped egg
  whites at the end makes it noticeably lighter than the dense
  mascarpone-block version, without losing the structure for slicing.

  Make it the day before. The flavours need time to settle, and a
  tiramisù eaten the same day always tastes a little raw.

ingredients:
  - 300 ml very strong espresso, warm or at room temperature
  - 90 ml Grand Marnier
  - 40 to 45 ladyfingers (savoiardi)
  - 450 g mascarpone, at room temperature
  - 60 ml dark rum
  - 4 large pasteurized eggs, separated
  - 100 g caster sugar, divided
  - 480 ml double cream
  - 0.5 tsp pure vanilla extract
  - A pinch of salt
  - Unsweetened cocoa powder, to dust
instructions:
  - Whisk the espresso and Grand Marnier together in a shallow bowl. One at a time, quickly dip each side of a ladyfinger into the mixture — a quick dip on each side, not a soak, or they'll turn soggy. Arrange them in one solid layer in a large baking dish (about 23 by 33 cm), cutting any to fit. Reserve the remaining espresso mixture and ladyfingers for the second layer.
  - In a large bowl, beat the mascarpone and rum together on medium speed until smooth. Set aside.
  - Set up a double boiler. Whisk the egg yolks with 50 g of the sugar over simmering water until light, pale, and foamy, about 5 minutes. Off the heat, beat the warm yolks into the mascarpone on medium speed until combined.
  - Whip the cream and vanilla to medium peaks (3 to 4 minutes) — between soft and stiff. Fold gently into the mascarpone mixture.
  - With a clean whisk, beat the egg whites and salt until foamy. Slowly pour in the remaining 50 g sugar and beat to stiff but not dry peaks. Fold into the mascarpone cream.
  - Spread half the mascarpone cream evenly over the soaked ladyfingers. Dip the remaining ladyfingers in the espresso mixture and arrange on top, then spread the remaining cream over. Refrigerate uncovered for 2 to 3 hours.
  - Sift a thick layer of cocoa powder over the top, cover, and refrigerate at least 8 hours and up to 1 day. Slice with a clean knife, wiping the blade between cuts.

Viki's tiramisu — built on Maida Heatter's classic method. The double-boiled yolks and whipped egg whites make it lighter than the usual mascarpone block. Make it a day ahead.

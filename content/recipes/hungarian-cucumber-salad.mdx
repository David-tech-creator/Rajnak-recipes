prepTime: '15 mins'

servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 large cucumber (or 2 small)
  - 1 tsp fine salt
  - 2 dl sour cream
  - 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp sweet Hungarian paprika, plus more for dusting
  - 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  - 0.5 tsp sugar
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Fresh dill, chopped (optional)
instructions:
  - Peel the cucumber, leaving alternating strips of skin if you like a bit of colour. Slice as thinly as possible — a mandoline is ideal.
  - Place the cucumber slices in a colander, sprinkle with the salt, toss, and leave to drain for 15 minutes. This is what makes the salad crisp, not soggy.
  - Squeeze the cucumber gently in your hands to release the water, then pat dry between two clean tea towels.
  - In a bowl, whisk the sour cream with the vinegar, paprika, garlic, sugar, and a grind of pepper.
  - Fold the cucumber into the dressing and chill at least 20 minutes.
  - Just before serving, dust with a little more paprika and a scatter of dill.
---

The cooling companion to pörkölt and other Hungarian stews — the sour cream and paprika tame the meat sauce beautifully. Don't skip the salting step; it's the difference between a crisp and a watery salad.

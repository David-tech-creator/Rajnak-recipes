ingredients:
  - 4 chicken breast fillets (about 600 g total)
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 dl plain flour
  - 2 eggs, beaten with 2 tbsp milk
  - 2 dl breadcrumbs
  - 4 tbsp rapeseed oil, for frying
  - 'For the cheese sauce:'
  - 100 g Philadelphia cream cheese (0.5 pack)
  - 1.5 dl finely grated cheese (Gruyère or mature cheddar)
  - 2.5 dl crème fraîche
  - 1–2 tsp Dijon mustard
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 'To serve:'
  - Boiled new potatoes
  - A green salad
instructions:
  - Trim any white sinew from the chicken fillets. Slice each one across into 2 or 3 small oval pieces. Season all over with salt.
  - Set up three shallow plates: flour in one, egg-and-milk in the second, breadcrumbs in the third.
  - Dredge each chicken piece in flour, dip in the egg, then press firmly into the breadcrumbs to coat well.
  - Heat the oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Fry the chicken in batches for 3–4 minutes per side, until deep golden and cooked through. Drain on kitchen paper.
  - For the sauce, beat the Philadelphia until smooth, then stir in the grated cheese, crème fraîche, and mustard. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Serve the crisp chicken with the cheese sauce on the side, new potatoes, and a green salad.
---

The Hungarian-Swiss family supper — crisp breaded chicken with a cool, mustardy cream-cheese sauce that no one expects to love as much as they do. Cold leftovers on bread are also good.

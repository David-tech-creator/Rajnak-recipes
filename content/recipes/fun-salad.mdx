story: |
  The pear-avocado-cottage cheese salad is a Swedish dinner-party
  invention from the 1970s — the era when keso (cottage cheese)
  was promoted across Scandinavia as the modern protein, when
  iceberg lettuce was the only crisp green in the supermarket,
  and when avocado had just arrived from Israel and Spain.

  The combination reads oddly on paper and works on the plate:
  sweet pear and creamy avocado against the sharpness of red
  onion and olive, all bound by mild fresh cheese. The honey
  in the dressing is the bridge — the same trick that makes a
  Waldorf salad cohere. Dress it at the last possible moment;
  cottage cheese loosens under acid and the pear browns fast.
difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 head iceberg lettuce, chopped
  - 1 ripe pear, sliced
  - 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  - 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  - 100 g black olives, pitted
  - 200 g cottage cheese (keso)
  - 3 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp honey
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Arrange the chopped iceberg lettuce on a wide platter or shallow bowl.
  - Scatter the pear and avocado slices across the lettuce, then the red onion and olives.
  - Spoon the cottage cheese in dollops across the top.
  - Whisk the olive oil, vinegar, honey, salt and pepper into a quick dressing.
  - Drizzle over the salad just before serving.
  - Toss gently at the table — or let everyone help themselves.

The family "Fun Salad" — the one nobody can explain the name of, but everyone asks for. Pear and avocado and cottage cheese is a strange trio that works.

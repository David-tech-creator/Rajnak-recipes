  they've gone tender. Salt too early and the skins toughen and refuse
  to soften, no matter how long you simmer. Even better the next day,
  once the smoke and the beer have settled in.

ingredients:
  - 2 yellow onions, chopped
  - 5 cloves garlic, minced
  - 2 stalks celery, chopped
  - 1 red chilli, finely chopped
  - 3 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 2 tsp ground cumin
  - 2 tsp smoked paprika
  - 500 g black or beluga lentils, rinsed
  - 330 ml lager beer
  - 1 vegetable bouillon cube
  - 800 g crushed tomatoes
  - 1 green bell pepper, diced
  - 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 'To serve:'
  - 2 ripe avocados, diced
  - 1 big handful nacho chips
  - 2 dl sour cream or crème fraîche
  - A small bunch coriander, leaves picked
  - 1 lime, cut in wedges
instructions:
  - Chop the yellow onions, garlic, chilli, and celery. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  - Sweat the onion, garlic, chilli, and celery until soft and lightly coloured, about 8 minutes. Stir in the cumin and smoked paprika and cook for one minute.
  - Add the lentils, beer, crushed tomatoes, and crumbled bouillon. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil and simmer uncovered for 25–30 minutes, until the lentils are tender and the sauce has reduced.
  - Just before serving, stir in the chopped green pepper and red onion so they stay crisp.
  - Ladle the stew into bowls and top with avocado, a handful of nacho chips, a spoon of sour cream, and a generous scatter of coriander.
  - Serve with lime wedges to squeeze over.

Like tacos in a bowl — smoky lentils with beer and tomato, finished with everything you'd pile on a taco. Mondays through Fridays, even better the next day.

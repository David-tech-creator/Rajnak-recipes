story: |
  A Hungarian cheese platter is closer to a sausage platter with cheese
  on the side. Csabai and gyulai — both protected-origin smoked, paprika-
  spiced sausages from the southern Great Plain town of Békéscsaba and
  Gyula respectively — anchor the board. Trappista and Pannónia are
  the everyday semi-hard cheeses; both have been on Hungarian shelves
  since the 1880s.

  The green Hungarian peppers (light, mild, almost banana-shaped) and a
  small jar of acacia honey are the unexpected combinations that make
  it work. Walnuts in their shells with a nutcracker is the traditional
  Christmas Eve flourish.
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Take the cheese out an hour before serving.'
signoff: 'Jó étvágyat.'
ingredients:
  - 1 small wedge mature cheddar
  - 1 wedge Trappista or Pannónia (Hungarian semi-hard cheese)
  - 1 small wedge blue cheese
  - 1 ball mozzarella di bufala, drained
  - 1 dry Hungarian sausage (csabai or gyulai), sliced
  - 100 g salami, sliced
  - 4 small green Hungarian peppers, sliced into rings
  - 1 small bunch red grapes
  - 1 small bunch green grapes
  - 4 radishes, halved
  - 1 small jar good honey
  - A handful of walnuts in their shells
  - 1 fresh baguette or sliced rye
instructions:
  - Take all the cheeses out of the fridge an hour before serving so they come to room temperature.
  - Arrange the cheeses across a large wooden board with space between them.
  - Tuck the sliced csabai and salami in folded ribbons between the cheeses.
  - Fill the gaps with bunches of grapes, halved radishes, and pepper rings.
  - Set the jar of honey somewhere central with a small spoon, and pile the walnuts at one end with a nutcracker.
  - Serve the bread alongside, sliced.
---

The Christmas Eve appetiser plate at our table — Hungarian charcuterie meets Swedish-style cheese board. The peppers and honey are the unexpected combinations that make it work.

title: Salmon Tartar
date: '2026-05-23'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '0 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 400 g sushi-grade salmon fillet, skinless and pin-boned
  - 1 small shallot, finely diced
  - 1 tbsp small capers, drained and chopped
  - 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp chives, finely chopped
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - 2 tsp lemon juice
  - 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - 1/2 tsp grated lemon zest
  - Flaky sea salt and black pepper, to taste
  - 1 ripe avocado, diced (optional, for serving)
  - 4 toasted brioche slices or crostini, to serve
instructions:
  - Chill the salmon in the freezer for 15 minutes to firm it up — this makes it easier to cut clean dice.
  - With a sharp knife, slice the salmon into 5-mm strips, then cubes. Do not use a food processor — the texture is half the point.
  - Tip into a chilled bowl and gently fold through the shallot, capers, dill, chives, mustard, lemon juice, olive oil, and lemon zest. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Taste — adjust lemon, salt, or mustard.
  - Spoon into a chilled metal ring on each plate to shape into a neat round (or just pile gracefully). Top with diced avocado if using.
  - Serve immediately with hot toasted brioche or crisp crostini.
---

A French bistro starter that lives or dies by the salmon — buy the best you can, eat it within hours of cutting. The mustard and capers give it the savoury edge that lifts it above a sushi bowl.

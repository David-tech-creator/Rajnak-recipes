  Game season in the Swiss Alps runs from September into November, when
  every village restaurant puts a Wildteller on the menu: venison or
  chamois, with spätzli, red cabbage, and a small mountain of
  lingonberry jam. The pairing of dark meat, buttery noodles, and tart
  red berries is the whole point.

  Chanterelles (Eierschwämmli) arrive at the same time as the game and
  belong in the same pan — earthy, golden, and ready to soak up the
  pan juices. Pinot Noir from the Valais is the classic glass; a Swiss
  Pinot from Graubünden also works.

ingredients:
  - 600 g deer (venison) loin or steaks
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil
  - 250 g chanterelles, cleaned
  - 1 shallot, finely chopped
  - 1 dl dry red wine
  - 2 dl game stock
  - 1 dl cream
  - 400 g fresh spätzli
  - 200 g red cabbage, finely sliced and braised
  - 4 tbsp lingonberry jam
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Pat the venison dry and season with salt and pepper. Heat oil and half the butter in a heavy pan and sear the meat 2–3 minutes per side until well browned. Move to a warm plate and rest.
  - In the same pan, sweat the shallot in the remaining butter until soft. Add the chanterelles and cook until they release their water and turn golden.
  - Deglaze with the red wine, reduce by half, then add the stock and cream. Simmer until the sauce coats the back of a spoon. Season.
  - Boil the spätzli in salted water until they float, drain, then toss with a knob of butter.
  - Slice the rested venison against the grain. Plate with the spätzli, spoon the chanterelle sauce over the meat, and serve with braised red cabbage and a generous spoon of lingonberry jam.
---

A Swiss-Alpine autumn classic: rich game meat, buttery spätzli, and the sharp sweetness of lingonberry. Pair with a Pinot Noir from the Valais.

---
title: Cheese Platter
date: '2022-02-05'
category: Christmas & Easter
image: /images/recipes/cheese-platter.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '0 mins'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 wedge aged hard cheese (Västerbotten, Comté, or aged Gouda)
  - 1 wedge soft white cheese (Brie or Camembert)
  - 1 wedge blue cheese (Roquefort, Stilton, or gorgonzola)
  - 1 small goat cheese log
  - 1 small bunch red grapes
  - 1 small bunch green grapes
  - 6 radishes, halved
  - 2 mini red and yellow peppers, sliced
  - A small jar fig jam or quince paste
  - A handful walnuts
  - Crackers, knäckebröd, and a sliced baguette
instructions:
  - Take all the cheeses out of the fridge at least an hour before serving so they reach room temperature — this is non-negotiable.
  - Arrange the cheeses on a large board or marble slab, leaving generous space between each one. Use cheese knives appropriate to each — a wire for the blue, a flat blade for the soft.
  - Tuck bunches of grapes, halved radishes, and pepper slices into the gaps.
  - Place the fig jam or quince paste alongside the blue cheese with a small spoon.
  - Scatter walnuts across the board.
  - Set crackers, knäckebröd, and bread alongside in a basket.
  - Serve at room temperature, with a glass of red wine or port.
---

A holiday board for the dessert hour. The rule of three: one hard, one soft, one blue, plus one wild card. Everything at room temperature or you taste nothing.

---
title: Tenderloin Gratin
date: '2022-01-03'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/tenderloin-gratin.jpg
prepTime: '30 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 6
kitchenNote: Rest the pork. The mash forgives nothing.
signoff: Smaklig måltid.
featured: true
ingredients:
  - 2 to 2.5 kg potatoes, peeled
  - 100 g butter
  - 2 dl milk, warm
  - 4 to 5 medium tomatoes, sliced
  - 700 to 800 g pork tenderloin
  - 300 to 400 g button mushrooms, sliced
  - 5 dl single cream
  - 150 g thin smoked ham slices
  - 200 g sliced melting cheese (Gruyère or Emmentaler)
  - 2 tbsp butter, for frying
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Boil the potatoes in salted water until soft, drain, and mash with the butter, warm milk, and salt. Keep the mash slightly firm — you'll be piping it.
  - Lay the tomato slices in a single layer across the base of a large oven-proof dish and season with salt and pepper. Pipe or spoon the mashed potato in a wall around the edge of the dish.
  - Pat the pork tenderloin dry, season, and sear in butter over high heat about 3 minutes per side until browned but still pink in the middle. Slice into rounds and arrange over the tomatoes.
  - In the same pan, fry the sliced mushrooms in the meat juices until golden. Pour in the cream, scrape up all the brown bits, and simmer until the sauce is slightly thickened.
  - Pour the mushroom cream over the meat. Cover with the ham slices and finally the cheese slices.
  - Bake at 220 °C for about 20 minutes, until the potato wall is golden and the cheese is bubbling. Rest 5 minutes before serving.
---

A showpiece Sunday dish from the family — pork tenderloin nested in mashed potato, mushroom cream, ham, and melted cheese. It feels extravagant but goes together in a single oven dish.

ingredients:
  - 2 dl quinoa, rinsed
  - 4 dl water
  - 300 g sashimi-grade tuna, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  - 2 tbsp wakame seaweed (dried), rehydrated in cold water
  - 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  - 1 dl rice vinegar
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 2 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp sesame oil
  - 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  - 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  - 1 tbsp chopped chives
  - 1 sheet nori, cut into thin strips
instructions:
  - Quick-pickle the onion: combine rice vinegar with sugar and a pinch of salt, pour over the sliced onion, and leave to sit while you prepare the rest.
  - Cook the quinoa in the water with a pinch of salt — bring to a boil, cover, reduce to low, simmer 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork and let cool slightly.
  - Whisk the soy sauce, sesame oil, and grated ginger for a quick dressing.
  - Drain the rehydrated wakame and squeeze out the excess water.
  - Divide the quinoa between two wide bowls. Top each with tuna cubes, avocado slices, wakame, and a generous heap of pickled onion.
  - Drizzle the dressing over the bowls and finish with sesame seeds, chives, and nori strips.

A quick poke-style bowl for when you want something fresh and protein-heavy. Use the best tuna you can find — it makes the dish.

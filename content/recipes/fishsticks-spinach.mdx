story: |
  Fiskpinnar med stuvad spenat — fishsticks with creamed spinach — is
  the canonical Swedish school-and-childhood Friday dinner. The
  fishsticks come from a freezer box (Findus invented them in 1953);
  the creamed spinach is the only part still made from scratch in most
  homes.

  The béchamel trick worth knowing: whisk milk and flour cold in a
  glass before they meet the hot butter, and the sauce will set without
  lumps every time. Boiled potatoes are the traditional third element,
  and a wedge of lemon never hurts.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 16 frozen fishsticks
  - 500 g frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  - 4 dl whole milk
  - 3 tbsp plain flour
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  - Pinch of grated nutmeg
  - 1 tsp salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Boiled potatoes, to serve (optional)
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 220°C. Spread the fishsticks on a baking tray and bake according to the package directions, until crisp and golden.
  - In a glass, whisk the milk and flour together until smooth and no lumps remain.
  - Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat until it just starts to take on colour. Pour in the milk-flour mixture and whisk constantly.
  - Continue whisking until the sauce thickens and bubbles, about 3–4 minutes. Off the heat, stir in the garlic, nutmeg, salt and pepper.
  - Add the drained spinach to the white sauce and return to medium heat for 2–3 minutes, stirring, until heated through.
  - Plate the fishsticks alongside a generous spoon of the creamed spinach. Boiled potatoes on the side if you want a fuller plate.
---

The childhood Friday dinner — fishsticks straight from the oven with proper homemade creamed spinach. The béchamel trick of pre-mixing milk and flour saves you from lumps every time.

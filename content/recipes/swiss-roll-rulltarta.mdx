---
title: Swiss Roll (Rulltårta)
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/swiss-roll-rulltarta.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '8 mins'
servings: 8
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 3 large eggs
  - 1.5 dl caster sugar, plus extra for rolling
  - 1.5 dl plain flour
  - 1 tsp baking powder
  - 2 tbsp cold water
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 250 g good raspberry or strawberry jam
  - 2 dl whipping cream, optional
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 250 °C. Line a 30 by 40 cm baking sheet with parchment paper.
  - Whisk the eggs and sugar with an electric mixer until very pale, thick, and tripled in volume — at least 4 minutes.
  - Sift the flour and baking powder together. Fold gently into the egg foam together with the cold water and vanilla sugar, keeping as much air as possible.
  - Spread the batter evenly across the prepared sheet and bake for 5 to 7 minutes, until just set and barely golden — don't overbake or it will crack when you roll it.
  - Sprinkle a sheet of fresh parchment with caster sugar, flip the warm sponge onto it, and peel off the baking paper. Roll the sponge up loosely from the short end (paper and all) and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  - Unroll gently, spread thickly with jam (and whipped cream if using), then roll back up tight. Trim the ends and dust with more caster sugar before slicing.
---

The Swedish fika cake every grandmother has in her repertoire. The trick is rolling the sponge while it's still warm so it doesn't crack — and don't skimp on the jam.

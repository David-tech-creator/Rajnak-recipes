story: |
  Stir-frying technique is Cantonese in origin — wok hei, "breath of
  the wok," is the slight smoky flavour you get only when the metal is
  ferociously hot and the food moves constantly. Most home cooks can't
  reach that heat on a domestic hob, but the principles still apply:
  pan screaming hot, food in a single layer, never crowd.

  Frozen wok vegetable mixes are a Nordic supermarket convenience that
  actually works in stir-fry, since they release water then dry out and
  caramelise. Soy sauce only at the very end — added early, it burns
  off into bitterness.

ingredients:
  - 500 g chicken fillet, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 600 g frozen wok vegetable mix
  - 300 g jasmine or long-grain rice
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce, plus more to serve
  - 1 clove garlic, finely chopped (optional)
  - 1 tsp grated fresh ginger (optional)
  - Sea salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Rinse the rice well, then cook in 6 dl salted water: bring to the boil, cover, lower the heat, and cook 12 minutes. Rest, covered, for another 5.
  - Pat the chicken dry and season with salt and pepper.
  - Heat the oil in a large pan or wok over high heat. Stir-fry the chicken for 4-5 minutes until lightly browned and just cooked through.
  - Tip in the frozen vegetable mix straight from the freezer. Stir-fry over high heat — they will release water, then dry out and start to colour.
  - Add the garlic and ginger if using and stir for another minute.
  - Add the cooked rice to the pan and toss everything together for 2 minutes.
  - Splash in soy sauce, taste, and adjust seasoning.
  - Serve in deep bowls with extra soy on the side.
---

The fastest dinner in the book — frozen wok veg do the work. Don't add liquid; the vegetables release plenty of their own as they thaw. Soy at the very end so it doesn't burn off.

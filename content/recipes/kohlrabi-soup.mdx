title: Kohlrabi Soup (with Carrots and Sour Cream)

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 4
kitchenNote: 'Temper the sour cream so it never breaks.'
signoff: 'Jó étvágyat.'
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g kohlrabi (or cauliflower), peeled and cubed
  - 4 large carrots, sliced
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 tbsp plain flour
  - 2 vegetable bouillon cubes
  - 1.5 l water
  - A handful flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 200 ml sour cream, to serve
instructions:
  - Peel the kohlrabi and cut into 2-cm cubes. Slice the carrots into rounds.
  - Heat the oil in a soup pot over medium heat. Add the kohlrabi and carrots and sweat for five minutes without colouring.
  - Sprinkle in the flour and stir for one minute so it coats the vegetables.
  - Pour in the water, crumble in the bouillon cubes, and bring to the boil. Lower to a gentle simmer and cook for 20–25 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  - Taste and season with salt and pepper, then stir in most of the parsley.
  - Ladle into bowls, top each with a spoon of sour cream, and finish with the remaining parsley.
---

A pale green Hungarian-Swiss kitchen soup that uses up the kohlrabi nobody knows what to do with. Light, sweet from the carrots, mellow with sour cream. Bread on the side.

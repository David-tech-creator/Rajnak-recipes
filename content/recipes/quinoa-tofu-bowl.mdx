story: |
  The grain bowl is the defining lunch format of the 2010s — a
  composed plate descended in spirit from the Korean bibimbap and
  Japanese donburi but reassembled in California: a base of cooked
  grain, a protein, two or three vegetables, a creamy element, and
  a sauce. Quinoa and tofu are the canonical vegan version.

  Two pieces of technique matter. First, the tofu has to be pressed
  — wrapped in a towel under a weight for at least twenty minutes —
  before frying, or the surface water keeps it from browning.
  Second, the soy marinade goes on after the tofu is crisp, not
  before; the salt and sugar in the soy would otherwise scorch in
  the pan. Hummus warmed with paprika is the late North African
  touch the bowl needs to come together.
difficulty: Easy

ingredients:
  - 4 dl quinoa, rinsed
  - 2 vegetable stock cubes
  - 600 g firm tofu, drained and pressed
  - 1 dl Japanese soy sauce
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil
  - 2 ripe avocados
  - 250 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 250 g good hummus
  - 1 tsp sweet paprika, plus more to sprinkle
  - 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  - A small handful coriander or parsley, to serve
instructions:
  - Bring 8 dl water to a boil with the crumbled stock cubes. Add the quinoa, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes, until the grains have opened. Fluff with a fork and leave to steam off the heat.
  - Cut the tofu into 2-cm cubes. Heat the oil in a non-stick pan over high heat and fry the tofu in a single layer, turning, until all sides are golden — about 6 minutes.
  - Tip the hot tofu into a shallow dish and pour the soy over it. Leave to marinate for at least 10 minutes.
  - Slice the avocados. Stir the paprika into the hummus.
  - 'Build four bowls: quinoa at the bottom, then a pile of marinated tofu, sliced avocado, cherry tomatoes, and a generous spoonful of paprika hummus.'
  - Sprinkle with extra paprika and chopped coriander, tuck a lemon wedge in the side, and serve.
---

A trendy vegan bowl from Köket — nutty quinoa, deeply soy-soaked tofu, cool avocado, and a creamy hummus warmed with smoky paprika. Each element is honest on its own; together they balance.

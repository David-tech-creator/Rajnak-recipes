ingredients:
  - 8 lamb cutlets (about 800 g total)
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed oil or butter
  - 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  - 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  - 800 g small waxy potatoes, halved
  - 4 large tomatoes, halved across the equator
  - 200 g soft goat cheese
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 tbsp herbes de Provence
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Toss the potatoes with a little olive oil and salt and roast on a tray for 30 minutes, turning once, until golden.
  - Arrange the halved tomatoes on a second tray, cut side up. Top each with a slice of goat cheese, drizzle with olive oil, scatter with herbes de Provence, and season.
  - Roast the tomatoes alongside the potatoes for the last 15 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbling.
  - Season the lamb cutlets all over. Heat the rapeseed oil or butter in a heavy frying pan over high heat.
  - Add the cutlets with the thyme and garlic. Cook for two minutes a side for pink, basting with the herb butter, then rest on a plate for three minutes.
  - Serve the lamb with the roasted potatoes and goat-cheese tomatoes, spooning over any pan juices.

A Friday-night plate that looks fancier than it is — pink lamb cutlets, garlicky potatoes, sweet roasted tomatoes melting under their cap of goat cheese. Pour something red.

---
title: "Chicken, Green Pea, and Carrot Soup"
date: '2022-01-02'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/chicken-green-pea-carrot-soup.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '45 mins'
servings: 4
featured: true
ingredients:
  - 600 g mixed small chicken pieces (wings, drumsticks, livers)
  - 800 g frozen carrot-and-pea mix (or 4 carrots sliced + 300 g frozen peas)
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 3 chicken or beef bouillon cubes
  - 1.5 l water
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 small bunch parsley, chopped
  - "Mannagrynsnockerli (semolina dumplings):"
  - 1 egg
  - About 50 g semolina (grits)
  - Pinch of salt
instructions:
  - Heat the rapeseed oil in a heavy pot. Soften the chopped onion for 3 minutes.
  - Add the carrot-and-pea mix and a generous pinch of salt. Stir and fry gently for 4 minutes.
  - Tip in the chicken pieces and turn them over for 3 minutes so they take on a little colour.
  - Pour in 1.5 l of water and crumble in the 3 bouillon cubes. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  - Skim off the grey foam that rises and continue cooking, partly covered, until the chicken is tender — about 30 minutes.
  - Meanwhile, beat the egg with a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Stir in semolina a spoonful at a time until you have a soft, scoopable dough.
  - Five minutes before the soup is done, drop small teaspoons of the dough into the simmering soup. They will firm up and rise to the surface as they cook.
  - Skim once more, taste for salt, scatter with parsley, and serve.
---

The hearty everyday soup of our kitchen — chicken, sweet carrots, soft peas, and tiny semolina dumplings. The skimming matters: that's the difference between a clear broth and a cloudy one.

---
title: "Green Peas with Eggs, Bacon and White Sauce (Borsó Főzelék)"
date: '2022-03-06'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/borso-fozelek.jpg
prepTime: '10 mins'
cookTime: '25 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Whisk the roux smooth before it joins the peas.'
signoff: 'Jó étvágyat.'
ingredients:
  - 600 g frozen green peas
  - 200 g smoked streaky bacon, diced
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 30 g butter
  - 30 g plain flour
  - 4 dl whole milk
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  - 4 eggs
  - 1 tbsp white wine vinegar (for poaching)
instructions:
  - Fry the bacon in a dry pan over medium heat until crisp and golden. Lift out with a slotted spoon and reserve, leaving the fat behind.
  - Soften the chopped onion in the bacon fat for 4 minutes, then add the peas and 2 dl water. Simmer covered until the peas are tender, about 8 minutes.
  - Meanwhile make the white sauce: melt butter in a small pan, stir in flour and cook for one minute. Whisk in the milk in a steady stream and cook until thickened and silky, about 4 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and the sugar.
  - Stir the white sauce into the peas. Adjust seasoning.
  - Bring a wide pan of water to a bare simmer with the vinegar. Crack each egg into a cup and slide into the water; poach 3 minutes for soft yolks.
  - Spoon the peas into shallow bowls. Top each portion with a poached egg and a shower of crisp bacon.
  - Finish with parsley and a grind of pepper.
---

A főzelék is the Hungarian one-pot vegetable dish — somewhere between soup and stew, and never sad. Always topped with something rich: bacon and a runny egg here. Comfort food, weekday lunch.

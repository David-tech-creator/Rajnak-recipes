difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 250 g spaghetti or linguine
  - 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  - 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  - 300 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 1 tbsp tomato paste
  - 2 large handfuls baby spinach
  - 1 dl reserved pasta water
  - 0.5 lemon, juiced
  - A pinch of cayenne pepper
  - 50 g Parmesan, finely grated, plus more to serve
  - Salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Bring a large pot of well-salted water to the boil and cook the pasta until al dente. Reserve a cup of the cooking water before draining.
  - While the pasta cooks, warm the olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook gently for a minute, until fragrant but not coloured.
  - Add the cherry tomatoes and tomato paste, season with salt, and cook 5 to 7 minutes, until the tomatoes burst and form a quick sauce.
  - Add the drained pasta to the pan with a generous splash of pasta water. Toss vigorously, then fold in the spinach until just wilted.
  - Squeeze in the lemon juice, add a pinch of cayenne, and finish with the Parmesan stirred in off the heat. Serve with more Parmesan and pepper at the table.
---

Viki's go-to weeknight pasta — fifteen minutes, one pan, and the lemon at the end is what makes it. Bon appetit!

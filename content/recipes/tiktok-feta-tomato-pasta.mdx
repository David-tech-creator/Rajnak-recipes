prepTime: '5 mins'

servings: 4
kitchenNote: Feta block whole. Tomatoes should blister.
signoff: Tuck in.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g cherry or grape tomatoes
  - 1 shallot, quartered
  - 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  - 1.25 dl extra virgin olive oil
  - 1 block (225 g) feta cheese
  - 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  - 300 g short pasta (penne, fusilli, or rigatoni)
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - 1 handful fresh basil leaves
  - Kosher salt
  - Crushed red pepper flakes, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Combine the tomatoes, shallot, garlic, and most of the olive oil in an ovenproof skillet or medium baking dish. Season with salt and red pepper flakes and toss to combine.
  - Place the feta block in the centre of the tomato mixture and drizzle the remaining olive oil over the top. Scatter the thyme sprigs across the dish.
  - Bake for 40 to 45 minutes, until the tomatoes are bursting and the feta is golden on top.
  - Meanwhile, cook the pasta in a large pot of well-salted water until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.
  - Tip the cooked pasta into the skillet with the roasted tomatoes and feta. Add a splash of reserved pasta water and the lemon zest, then stir vigorously until everything melts into a creamy sauce.
  - Tear the basil over the top and serve immediately.
---

The recipe that broke the internet in 2021 — and rightly so. Five ingredients, one oven dish, and a sauce that comes together from a block of feta melting into roasted tomatoes.

ingredients:
  - 1 small chèvre log (about 200 g), cut into 8 slices
  - 8 slices baguette (1 cm thick)
  - 2 tbsp honey
  - 1 tsp dried thyme
  - 1 large bag mixed leaves (lamb's lettuce or rocket)
  - 150 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 0.5 small red onion, sliced thin
  - 50 g walnuts, lightly toasted
  - 4 figs (or 1 small pear), quartered
  - 'For the dressing:'
  - 3 tbsp walnut oil (or olive oil)
  - 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - 0.5 tsp honey
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven grill to high. Arrange the baguette slices on a tray.
  - Top each slice with a round of chèvre, drizzle with honey, and sprinkle with thyme.
  - Slide under the grill for 4–6 minutes, watching carefully, until the cheese is bubbling and golden at the edges.
  - Meanwhile, shake the dressing ingredients together in a small jar.
  - Toss the leaves, tomatoes, red onion, walnuts, and figs gently with most of the dressing and arrange on four plates.
  - Top each salad with two warm chèvre toasts straight from the grill and drizzle with the remaining dressing.
---

The French bistro salad you'd happily eat every Tuesday — warm honey-glazed goat cheese on toast, leaves with walnuts and figs, sharp mustard dressing. A meal in itself.

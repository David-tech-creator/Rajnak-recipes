title: Sushi, Katsu & Tempura
date: '2022-04-22'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/sushi-katsu-tempura.jpg
prepTime: '40 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 4
kitchenNote: Cold hands for sushi. Hot oil for tempura.
signoff: Itadakimasu.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 4 dl sushi rice, rinsed until water runs clear
  - 6 dl water (1.5 cups per cup of rice)
  - 0.5 dl Mizkan sushi seasoning
  - 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  - 1 cucumber, cut into batons
  - Nori sheets, optional
  - 4 pork or chicken cutlets, about 150 g each
  - 2 eggs
  - 1.25 dl water
  - 1 dl plain flour
  - 200 g panko breadcrumbs
  - Tonkatsu (thick) sesame seeds
  - Vegetable or rice oil, for frying
  - 1 zucchini, sliced less than 1 cm thick
  - 2 carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  - 8 button mushrooms, scaled (lightly scored)
  - 100 g tempura mix
  - 160 ml ice-cold water
  - Kikkoman noodle soup base (men-tsuyu), to serve
  - Salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Cook the rinsed rice with the water in a covered pot, then stir in the sushi seasoning and let it cool to room temperature. Cut the avocado and cucumber while you wait.
  - For the katsu, whisk the eggs with the 1.25 dl water and the flour together with chopsticks to make a loose batter — small lumps are fine. Season the cutlets with salt and pepper.
  - Dip each cutlet first in the batter, then press firmly into the panko. Fry in 2 cm of hot oil until deep golden on both sides, about 3 minutes per side. Drain on paper.
  - Toast the tonkatsu sesame seeds in a dry pan, then crush them lightly in a mortar to release the oil.
  - For the tempura, whisk the tempura mix with the 160 ml ice water until just combined and still a little lumpy. Dip the zucchini, carrot, and mushroom into the batter and fry in hot oil until pale gold and crisp, about 2 minutes.
  - Mix one part Kikkoman noodle base with two parts water for the dipping sauce. Serve the sushi rice with avocado and cucumber, the sliced katsu with crushed sesame on top, and the tempura with its dipping sauce.
---

A Japanese feast we put together for special evenings — three classics at once. Cook the rice first, fry the katsu next, and finish with the tempura so it lands on the table hot and crisp.

  Pasta alla Norma is Catania's flagship dish, said to be named
  for Vincenzo Bellini's opera Norma — supposedly an exclamation
  of approval from playwright Nino Martoglio, who declared the
  pasta "a real Norma!" meaning a masterpiece. Bellini was born
  in Catania; the city claims him, and the pasta, as its own.

  The dish is built on four Sicilian ingredients: aubergine,
  tomatoes, basil, and ricotta salata — the salted, aged ricotta
  that grates over the top like parmesan but tastes of the
  island. Salting and pressing the aubergine pulls out bitter
  juices and lets the cubes fry to a deep gold instead of
  steaming.

ingredients:
  - 800 g aubergine, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 2 tbsp coarse salt, for the aubergine
  - 400 g penne pasta
  - 1 yellow onion, finely chopped
  - 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  - 1.5 dl olive oil, divided
  - 2 tbsp tomato purée
  - 400 g good-quality crushed tomatoes
  - 1 pinch salt
  - 1 pinch sugar
  - 1 pinch black pepper
  - 1 pinch chilli flakes
  - 0.5 dl pasta cooking water
  - 2 dl parmesan, finely grated
  - 250 g ricotta (or salted ricotta salata)
  - 1 small bunch fresh basil
instructions:
  - Toss the aubergine cubes with the 2 tbsp coarse salt in a colander and let drain for 20 minutes to draw out the bitter juices.
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the penne according to the packet instructions. Reserve 0.5 dl pasta water before draining.
  - Meanwhile, gently fry the onion and garlic in 0.5 dl olive oil over medium heat for 5 minutes. Stir in the tomato purée and cook for 30 seconds, then add the crushed tomatoes, salt, sugar, pepper, chilli flakes, and pasta water. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  - Rinse the aubergine well and press dry between layers of kitchen paper.
  - Heat the remaining 1 dl olive oil in a large frying pan over high heat. Fry the aubergine for about 5 minutes, until deeply golden and tender all through.
  - Toss the drained pasta with the tomato sauce, torn basil, half the aubergine, and half the ricotta.
  - Serve in deep bowls, topped with the remaining aubergine, ricotta, and a generous shower of parmesan.
---

A Sicilian classic from Catania, said to be named for Bellini's opera Norma. The salting of the aubergine and the high-heat fry give it the silky, almost meaty texture the dish is loved for.

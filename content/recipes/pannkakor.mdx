ingredients:
  - 200 g plain flour
  - 2 eggs
  - 2 dl water
  - 3 dl milk
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed or sunflower oil, plus more for frying
  - Pinch of salt
  - 1 l filmjölk (A-fil) or 8 small natural yoghurts, for the túró filling
  - 2 egg yolks
  - 150 g vanilla sugar
  - 3 tbsp icing sugar
  - 2 tbsp raisins
  - Cocoa powder and sugar, or apple and cinnamon, to serve
instructions:
  - Whisk the flour with the eggs and water using an electric whisk until smooth. Add the milk and oil and whisk again. Rest the batter for 15 minutes.
  - Heat a small frying pan over medium-high heat with a film of oil or butter. Pour in a thin ladle of batter, swirl to coat, and cook until the edges lift and the underside is golden — about a minute per side.
  - Stack the pancakes on a warm plate as you go. Serve with cocoa powder mixed with twice as much sugar, or grated apple with cinnamon, or strawberry jam.
  - For the túró filling, gently heat the filmjölk in a saucepan over low heat for 5–8 minutes, without stirring, until the curds separate from the whey.
  - Line a sieve with a coffee filter, pour in the curds, and let drain until the liquid runs out. Cool completely.
  - Beat the drained curds with the egg yolks, vanilla sugar, and icing sugar. Fold in the raisins.
  - Spoon the filling onto each pancake, roll up, and arrange in a buttered baking dish. Warm in a 200 °C oven for 10 minutes, until hot through, and serve.
---

Mamma's Swedish-Hungarian pancakes: thin Swedish pannkakor folded around a sweet Hungarian túró cheese filling. Eaten plain with jam on weekday mornings; with the filling for Sunday treats.

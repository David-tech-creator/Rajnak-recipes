  - 200g flour
  - 2 eggs
  - 2 dl water
  - 3 dl milk
  - 1 cup rapeseed/sunflower oil
  - Cocoa powder (optional)
  - Sugar (optional)
  - Apple and cinnamon (optional)
  - 8 natural yogurt (pearl yogurt) or 1 liter A-fil
  - 2 egg yolks
  - 150g vanilla sugar
  - 2-4 tablespoons powdered sugar
  - Raisins
prepTime: 20 mins
cookTime: 30 mins
servings: 4

Swedish pancakes - thin, golden and delicious. Served with a traditional toro (sweet cheese) filling.

## Pancake Batter

Mix the flour with eggs and water. Mix with electric whisk. Then add milk and cooking oil. Mix again. Let it stand for 15 min. Start frying!

Eat with cocoa powder (add twice as much sugar as cocoa powder) or grate an apple into shreds and add cinnamon powder. Jorgubbam or any jam is also good.

## Toro's Pancake Filling (enough for 8-10 pancakes)

8 natural yogurt (pearl yogurt) or 1 liter A-fil. Boil 5-8 mins, low heat, without stirring, until the liquid differs (clear liquid comes out). Strain on tea filter (split tea filter if it is a bag so that it becomes larger / fold out) and use the pasta strainer. Let the liquid drain. Let it cool. Add 2 egg yolks and 150g vanilla sugar and 2-4 tablespoons powdered sugar. Put in a little raisin. Fill the pancakes with toro. Put in the oven at 200 degrees until it gets hot. Ready!

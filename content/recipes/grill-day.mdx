title: Grill Day Marinated Pork
date: '2022-02-11'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '15 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 800 g pork loin or pork carré (the slightly fattier cut)
  - 3 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 4 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  - 1 tsp paprika powder
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 tsp black pepper
  - 1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme (optional)
instructions:
  - Cut the pork into 1-cm thick slices.
  - Lay each slice between two sheets of cling film and pound to about 0.5 cm thick with a meat mallet.
  - Whisk together the mustard, rapeseed oil, garlic, paprika, salt, pepper, and thyme into a thick marinade.
  - Brush the marinade generously on both sides of each piece of pork. Stack in a dish, cover, and refrigerate at least a few hours — ideally half a day or overnight.
  - Bring up to room temperature 30 minutes before grilling.
  - Grill over hot coals (or a hot griddle pan) for 2–3 minutes per side, just until charred on the outside and juicy inside. Rest a couple of minutes and serve.
---

Summer grill day staple — the kind of pork that gets shoved in a baguette with a smear of more mustard. Don't skip the pounding; that's what keeps it tender and fast on the fire.

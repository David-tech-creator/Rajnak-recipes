ingredients:
  - 600 g coarsely ground beef (20% fat)
  - 1 tsp flaky sea salt
  - 0.5 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  - 1 tbsp neutral oil
  - 4 slices cheddar or aged cheese
  - 4 brioche burger buns
  - 4 leaves crisp lettuce
  - 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  - 1 small red onion, thinly sliced into rings
  - 4 pickled gherkins, sliced
  - 4 tbsp mayonnaise
  - 2 tbsp ketchup
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
instructions:
  - Divide the meat into four loose balls. Don't overwork the meat — just shape it. Flatten gently to patties slightly wider than the buns. Make a thumbprint dent in the centre of each so they don't dome.
  - Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides just before cooking.
  - Heat the oil in a cast-iron pan or on a grill over high heat until smoking.
  - Lay in the patties and cook 3 minutes without touching, then flip once. Top each with a slice of cheese and cook another 2–3 minutes for medium.
  - Toast the buns cut-side down in the same pan for 30 seconds while the patties rest.
  - Mix the mayo, ketchup and mustard for a quick burger sauce. Spread on both bun halves, stack with lettuce, tomato, onion, the patty, gherkins, and crown with the top bun.
---

The home burger — coarse beef, salt and pepper, and an honest hot pan. Don't smash, don't press, and don't overcomplicate the topping.

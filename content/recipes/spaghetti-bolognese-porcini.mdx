---
title: Spaghetti Bolognese with Porcini
date: '2022-03-12'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/spaghetti-bolognese-porcini.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '1 h 30 m'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 30 g dried porcini mushrooms
  - 2 dl boiling water
  - 500 g minced beef (80/20)
  - 250 g minced pork
  - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  - 2 carrots, finely diced
  - 2 sticks celery, finely diced
  - 4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  - 3 tbsp tomato purée
  - 2 dl dry red wine
  - 400 g good crushed tomatoes
  - 2 dl whole milk
  - 1 bay leaf
  - 1 tsp dried oregano
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 500 g spaghetti
  - 80 g parmesan, finely grated, to serve
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Soak the dried porcini in 2 dl boiling water for 20 minutes. Lift out, squeeze, and chop fine. Strain and reserve the soaking liquid.
  - Heat the olive oil in a heavy pot over medium heat. Sweat the onion, carrot, and celery with a pinch of salt for 10 minutes, until soft.
  - Add the garlic and porcini and cook 2 minutes. Push the vegetables to the side and add the minced beef and pork. Break up with a spoon and brown well — 8 minutes.
  - Stir in the tomato purée and cook 2 minutes. Pour in the wine and let it bubble down by half.
  - Add the crushed tomatoes, milk, bay leaf, oregano, and the strained porcini liquid. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  - Cover partially and simmer over very low heat for at least 1 hour 15 minutes, stirring every so often. Season with salt and plenty of pepper.
  - Cook the spaghetti in well-salted water until al dente. Drain, reserving a splash of pasta water. Toss the pasta with a few ladles of ragù, loosening with pasta water if needed.
  - Serve in deep bowls with extra ragù on top and a generous shower of parmesan.
---

The earthy porcini-boosted version of the classic ragù alla bolognese — long, slow, and well worth it. The dried mushrooms and the soaking water add a depth that no fresh mushroom can.

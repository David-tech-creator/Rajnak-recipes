servings: 8

ingredients:
  - 125 g butter, melted
  - 2 very ripe bananas, mashed
  - 1 additional banana, for the top
  - 0.5 dl sour cream (or Turkish yoghurt)
  - 3 eggs
  - 2.5 dl icing sugar
  - 100 g dark chocolate, chopped
  - 3 dl plain flour (about 180 g)
  - 1 dl cocoa powder
  - 1 krm (pinch) salt
  - 2 tsp baking powder
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Line a 1.5-litre loaf tin with baking paper.
  - Mash the two ripe bananas with the sour cream until smooth.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and icing sugar lightly until just combined.
  - Sift the flour, cocoa, salt, and baking powder together in a third bowl.
  - Pour the melted butter and mashed bananas into the egg mixture and stir. Fold in the dry ingredients, then the chopped chocolate.
  - Pour the batter into the lined tin. Peel and halve the remaining banana lengthwise and lay the two halves cut-side up on the surface.
  - Bake on the lower rack for about 50 minutes — a skewer inserted into the centre should come out clean.
  - Cool in the tin for 10–15 minutes, then lift out using the baking paper and finish cooling on a wire rack.
---

A Swedish weekday cake from Alltommat — uses up brown bananas and tastes like the best brownie you've ever had. The banana on top caramelises beautifully and tells everyone what's inside.

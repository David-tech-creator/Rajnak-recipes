A classic Sichuan stir-fry dish made easy. The shredded chicken is quickly cooked with carrot, bamboo shoots, and

peppers in a pungent sweet and sour garlic sauce.

Ingredients:

Marinade:

1 skinless chicken breast

2 teaspoons light soy sauce

2 teaspoons Shaoxing Wine

1 teaspoon cornstarch

For the sauce:

2 tablespoons all-purpose chili garlic sauce

2 tablespoons black vinegar

1 tablespoon light soy sauce

1 tablespoon sugar

2 teaspoons cornstarch

1/4 teaspoon salt

For the stir-fry

1 and 1/2 tablespoons vegetable oil

2 bell peppers

1/2 carrot

1/2 bamboo shoots (100g)

Steamed white rice

1. Slice chicken breast. Prepare and cut vegetables.

2. Combine chicken, light soy sauce and Shaoxing wine in a small bowl. Add cornstarch and mix well by hand, until

the thin starchy paste coats the chicken evenly.

3. Combine all sauce ingredients in a bowl and mix well.

4. Heat a wok over medium high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of oil, add quicken, cook for 20-30 seconds without stirring.

Stir and cook until the surface of the chicken just turns white, 1 minute. Turn to lowest heat and immediately to a

plate.

5. Add the remaining 1/2 teaspoon of oil, turn back to medium heat. Add carrot and cook until half cooked 1 min. Add

bamboo shoots, cook for 1 minute. Add bell pepper and chicken. Stir the spicy sauce and swirl it into the wok. Mix

everything well until all ingredients are covered with sauce.

6. Serve warm over steamed white rice.

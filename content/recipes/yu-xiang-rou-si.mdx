prepTime: '20 mins'

servings: 2
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 skinless chicken breast, cut into thin strips
  - 2 tsp light soy sauce
  - 2 tsp Shaoxing wine
  - 1 tsp cornstarch
  - 2 tbsp Sichuan chili bean paste (doubanjiang) or chili-garlic sauce
  - 2 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar
  - 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 2 tsp cornstarch
  - 0.25 tsp salt
  - 2 tbsp water
  - 1.5 tbsp vegetable oil
  - 3 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 thumb fresh ginger, minced
  - 2 spring onions, white and green separated
  - 2 bell peppers, cut into thin strips
  - 0.5 carrot, julienned
  - 100 g bamboo shoots, julienned
  - Steamed white rice, to serve
instructions:
  - Toss the chicken with the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and 1 tsp cornstarch in a bowl. Mix well by hand so the starch coats every strip evenly. Let stand 10 minutes.
  - In a small bowl combine the chili bean paste, black vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, 2 tsp cornstarch, salt, and water. Stir until smooth.
  - Heat a wok over medium-high heat and add 1 tbsp of the oil. Add the chicken and let it sit untouched for 20 seconds, then stir-fry for about 1 minute, until the surface just turns white. Transfer to a plate.
  - Add the remaining oil to the wok and reduce to medium heat. Stir-fry the garlic, ginger, and white spring onion for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  - Add the carrot and stir-fry 1 minute, then the bamboo shoots and peppers for another minute. Return the chicken to the wok.
  - Stir the sauce, pour it down the side of the wok, and toss everything until the sauce thickens and coats — about 30 seconds. Scatter the green spring onion over the top and serve immediately over steamed rice.
---

Sichuan's classic "fish-fragrant" stir-fry — no fish, just the sweet-sour-spicy-garlicky sauce traditionally used to cook fish. Have everything chopped before you light the wok.

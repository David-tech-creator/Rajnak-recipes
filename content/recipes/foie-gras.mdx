title: Foie Gras Terrine
date: '2022-01-29'
category: Christmas & Easter
image: /images/recipes/foie-gras.jpg
prepTime: '30 mins'
cookTime: '40 mins'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g raw foie gras, deveined
  - 0.5 dl Port wine
  - 0.5 dl cognac
  - 1.5 tsp fine salt
  - 0.5 tsp freshly ground white pepper
  - Pinch of sugar
  - Brioche or country loaf, to serve
  - Fig marmalade, to serve
  - Fleur de sel de Camargue, to serve
instructions:
  - Separate the liver into 4–6 larger pieces, working carefully to leave the lobes intact where possible.
  - Lay the pieces in a shallow dish. Pour over the Port and cognac, and season both sides with the salt, white pepper, and sugar. Cover and refrigerate for 24 hours, turning the pieces halfway.
  - Heat the oven to 120°C. Press the marinated pieces tightly into a small terrine, eliminating any air gaps. Pour any remaining marinade over the top.
  - Set the terrine in a deep ovenware dish and pour in just-boiled water to come halfway up the sides — a bain-marie.
  - Bake for 40 minutes. The fat will rise to the top — that is correct.
  - Cool to room temperature, then weight the top with a small board and refrigerate. Wait 1–2 days before serving for the flavour to bloom.
  - Serve thick slices on toasted brioche, with fig marmalade, flakes of fleur de sel, and a glass of champagne or Sauternes.
---

The Christmas Eve centrepiece — silky, slightly boozy, and made days ahead so the host can sit down. Don't skip the rest in the fridge; the texture and flavour bloom in those 48 hours.

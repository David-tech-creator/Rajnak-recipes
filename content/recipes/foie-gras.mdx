A luxurious homemade foie gras terrine marinated in Porto and cognac, slow-cooked in a bain-marie. Best prepared 1-2 days before serving. A centerpiece for the Christmas and Easter table.

## Instructions

1. Separate the liver into 4-6 larger pieces.
2. **Marinate:** Pour the Porto and cognac over the liver. Salt and pepper both sides. Cover and refrigerate for 24 hours (turn halfway through).
3. Push the pieces tightly into a terrine, making sure there are no air gaps.
4. **Bain-marie and oven:** Boil water and pour into a bigger ovenware dish. Place the terrine in the water bath. Put in the oven at 120C for 40 minutes.
5. Let cool, then place in the fridge.
6. Eat after 1-2 days for best flavor.
7. Serve with bread, salt flakes, figue marmalade, and champagne.

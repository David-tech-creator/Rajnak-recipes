  Bableves csülökkel — bean soup with smoked pork hock — is Hungarian
  winter food at its most utilitarian. White beans soaked overnight,
  cooked with a whole smoked knuckle until the meat slides from the
  bone, and finished with the country's defining flourish: a paprika
  roux whisked in at the end.

  straight into the simmering pot. The vinegar-and-sugar finish is the
  Transylvanian touch — it lifts the smoke and keeps the broth from
  feeling heavy. Like all paprika-and-pork dishes, it tastes better the
  second day.

ingredients:
  - 250 g dry white beans, soaked overnight
  - 5-6 carrots, cut into thick rounds
  - 600-800 g smoked pork hock or smoked pork tenderloin
  - 3 beef bouillon cubes
  - 2 l water
  - 1 tsp salt
  - "For the paprika roux:"
  - 1-2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 1-2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 20 g plain flour
  - "To finish:"
  - ½ tbsp white wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - 1-3 bay leaves
  - 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  - "Csipetke (pinched dumplings):"
  - 1 egg
  - About 75 g plain flour
  - Pinch of salt
instructions:
  - The night before, soak the beans in cold water. Drain.
  - Put the drained beans, carrots, smoked pork (whole), bouillon cubes, and salt in a pressure cooker with 2 litres of water. Cook for 40 minutes to 1 hour, until the meat is tender. Lift the pork out, slice it, and return to the pot.
  - In a separate pan, fry the chopped onion in oil until it just takes on colour. Pull off the heat, stir in the paprika so it doesn't burn, then add the flour and cook for a minute over low heat.
  - Ladle in about half a litre of the soup liquid a little at a time, whisking, until smooth. Tip the roux back into the soup and stir well.
  - Add the vinegar, sugar, and bay leaves.
  - For the csipetke, knead the egg with flour and a pinch of salt into a stiff dough. Pinch off small thumbnail pieces and drop them straight into the simmering soup.
  - Simmer 5-10 minutes more, until the dumplings float and are cooked through.
  - Fish out the bay leaves, scatter with parsley, and serve hot.
---

A Hungarian winter soup substantial enough to be the whole dinner. The smoked pork makes it; don't skip it. Better the next day, as with most paprika-and-pork dishes.

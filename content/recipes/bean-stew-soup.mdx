A rich and hearty Hungarian bean stew with smoked pork, thickened with a paprika roux and finished with hand-pinched csipetke dumplings. Soak the beans overnight before cooking.

## Instructions

1. Soak the beans overnight. Drain.
2. Add beans, carrots, pork, bouillon cubes, and salt in a pressure cooker with 2 liters of water. Cook for 40 minutes to 1 hour.
3. Separately, fry the onion in oil, add paprika powder and flour, and mix continuously.
4. Add liquid from the soup gradually into the thickening mixture.
5. Pour the thickening back into the soup.
6. Add the white wine vinegar, sugar, and bay leaves.
7. Prepare csipetke by mixing 1 egg with flour until a hard dough forms. Pinch small pieces into the soup.
8. Cook on medium heat for 5-10 minutes.
9. Decorate with parsley. Remove the bay leaves before serving.

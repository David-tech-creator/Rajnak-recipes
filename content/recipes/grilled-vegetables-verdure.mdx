  Verdure grigliate is the Tuscan and Emilian standard for summer
  vegetables — grilled dry over hot coals or a ridged pan, then dressed
  warm with olive oil, vinegar or lemon, parsley, and sometimes a
  whisper of garlic. The Italian rule is firm: no oil before the
  grill, only after.

  Cooking dry lets the vegetables char rather than steam, and the heat
  concentrates the sugars into the dark grill marks that carry the
  flavour. Served at room temperature on an antipasti spread, as a side
  for grilled meat, or piled on toasted bread with mozzarella the next
  day.

ingredients:
  - 1 yellow or red bell pepper
  - 1 large carrot
  - 1 eggplant (aubergine)
  - 1 zucchini
  - 1 dl extra virgin olive oil
  - 1 tsp balsamic vinegar (or 1 tbsp lemon juice)
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  - 1 clove garlic, very thinly sliced (optional)
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Whisk the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, parsley, salt and pepper in a small bowl. Set aside to let the flavours marry.
  - Wash and dry the bell pepper, eggplant and zucchini thoroughly. Peel the carrot.
  - Cut the pepper into wide strips, discarding the seeds and white pith. Slice the eggplant and zucchini into 3–4 mm rounds. Cut the carrot at an angle so the slices are larger.
  - Heat a grill or griddle pan to high. Grill the vegetables in batches without oil, 7–10 minutes per side, until soft but still al dente and well marked.
  - Arrange the warm vegetables on a wide platter and spoon the dressing generously over the top.
  - Optional variation — scatter the very thinly sliced garlic over the warm vegetables with a pinch of flaky salt and an extra drizzle of olive oil. Let stand 30 minutes before serving so the flavours marinate in.
---

The Tuscan way with summer vegetables — grilled dry over hot coals, dressed only after, never crowded. Serve at room temperature beside grilled meat or as part of an antipasti spread.

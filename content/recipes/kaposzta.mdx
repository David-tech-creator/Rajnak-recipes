---
title: Káposzta (Töltött Káposzta)
date: '2022-03-02'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/kaposzta.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '40 mins'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  - 2 tbsp lard or neutral oil
  - 2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 700 g stewing meat (veal, pork, or beef), cut into 3-cm cubes
  - 1.5 kg raw sauerkraut, squeezed of brine
  - 3 beef bouillon cubes
  - 1 smoked sausage piece (about 150 g), sliced thick
  - Water, to almost cover
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 200 ml sour cream or crème fraîche, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the lard in a heavy pot or pressure cooker. Add the onion and cook over medium heat until soft and golden, about 8 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika so it doesn't burn. Return to the heat and add the meat, turning to coat. Cook for one minute until the meat takes on colour.
  - Squeeze the brine from the sauerkraut and add it to the pot. Stir thoroughly.
  - Pour in just enough water to almost cover. Crumble in the bouillon cubes and add the smoked sausage. Season with pepper.
  - Bring up to pressure on high heat. Once the cooker reaches two rings, lower to a gentle simmer and cook for 25–30 minutes. (Without pressure: simmer covered for 90 minutes.)
  - Release pressure, taste, adjust salt, and serve hot with a generous spoonful of sour cream.
---

The slow-cooked Hungarian sauerkraut stew that turns up at every winter table. The smoked sausage is the soul of it — don't skip. Better the next day if you can wait.

  A French-style savoury tart — pâte brisée, beetroot, chèvre, eggs
  and cream. Beetroot and goat cheese is a near-universal pairing in
  French and Swedish bistros: the sweet earth of the root vegetable
  against the chalky sharpness of the cheese, with walnuts for
  texture.

  Two technical points carry the dish. First, blind-bake the shell
  fully — wet beetroot will turn an underbaked base into a sponge.
  Second, drain the cooked beetroot well, ideally pat dry, before
  arranging in the case. Cuts beautifully and travels well to a picnic.

ingredients:
  - 'For the pastry:'
  - 200 g plain flour
  - 125 g cold butter, cubed
  - 3 tbsp cold water
  - 1 pinch salt
  - 'For the filling:'
  - 4 medium cooked beetroots (about 400 g), sliced
  - 150 g soft chèvre (goat cheese log)
  - 3 eggs
  - 2 dl single cream
  - 1 dl whole milk
  - 1 tbsp honey
  - 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - A handful walnuts, roughly chopped
  - Rocket, to serve
instructions:
  - Rub the flour, cold butter, and salt together with your fingertips until it looks like coarse breadcrumbs. Bind with the cold water, press into a flat disc, wrap, and chill for 30 minutes.
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Roll the pastry out on a floured surface and line a 24-cm tart tin. Prick the base, line with paper and baking beans, and blind-bake for 15 minutes. Remove the beans and bake for 5 minutes more until pale gold.
  - Arrange the sliced beetroot in the pastry case in overlapping rounds. Crumble or slice the chèvre over the top.
  - Whisk the eggs with the cream, milk, honey, thyme, salt, and pepper. Pour into the case.
  - Scatter the walnuts on top and bake at 180 °C for 30–35 minutes, until the filling is just set with a slight wobble in the middle.
  - Let the pie cool for 10 minutes, then serve warm with a tangle of dressed rocket.

Margaux's earthy, sweet tart — magenta beetroots, melting chèvre, walnuts for crunch. Cuts beautifully and travels well to a picnic.

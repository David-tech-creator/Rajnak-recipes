title: Chocolate Muffins
date: '2026-05-23'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '22 mins'
servings: 12
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 260 g plain flour
  - 50 g cocoa powder
  - 200 g caster sugar
  - 2 tsp baking powder
  - ½ tsp baking soda
  - ½ tsp salt
  - 2 large eggs
  - 240 ml buttermilk (or milk + 1 tsp lemon juice)
  - 100 ml neutral oil
  - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - 200 g dark chocolate, chopped into chunks
  - 2 tbsp coarse sugar (for the tops)
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper cases.
  - Whisk together the flour, cocoa, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl.
  - In a second bowl, beat the eggs lightly, then whisk in the buttermilk, oil, and vanilla.
  - Pour the wet into the dry and fold gently with a spatula — stop the moment the flour disappears. The batter should be thick and a little lumpy.
  - Fold in the chocolate chunks, reserving a handful for the tops.
  - Divide the batter between the paper cases, filling them right to the brim. Scatter with the reserved chocolate and coarse sugar.
  - Bake for 5 minutes at 200°C, then drop the temperature to 180°C and bake another 15-18 minutes, until a skewer comes out with a few moist crumbs.
  - Cool in the tin for 5 minutes before lifting onto a rack.
---

Cocoa in the batter, real chocolate chunks throughout, the two-temperature method for tall domed tops. They keep well in a tin for two days and freeze beautifully.

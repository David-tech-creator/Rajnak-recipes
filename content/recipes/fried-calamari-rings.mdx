story: |
  Calamari fritti is the universal Mediterranean taverna starter —
  Greek, Italian, Spanish, Croatian, Turkish kitchens all claim a
  version, all of them more or less the same: dry squid, seasoned
  flour, hot oil, lemon.

  Three rules separate good from bad. Dry the rings until the kitchen
  paper comes away clean — wet squid spits and steams. Oil at a true
  180°C — too cool and you get a soggy crust, too hot and the squid
  toughens before it colours. Small batches — crowding drops the
  temperature.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g cleaned squid tubes, cut into 1-cm rings
  - 2 dl plain flour
  - 1 dl semolina or fine cornmeal
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 tsp black pepper
  - 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
  - 1 l neutral oil, for deep-frying
  - 2 lemons, cut into wedges
  - Flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to garnish
  - Aioli or tartare sauce, to serve
instructions:
  - Rinse the squid rings well and pat them very dry with kitchen paper — this is the trick to a crisp crust.
  - In a wide bowl, combine the flour, semolina, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  - Heat the oil in a deep, heavy pot to 180°C (test with a cube of bread — it should turn golden in 30 seconds).
  - Toss a handful of squid rings in the flour mixture and shake off the excess in a sieve.
  - Fry in small batches for 1.5–2 minutes, until pale gold and crisp. Don't crowd the pan or they'll steam. Lift out with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper.
  - Sprinkle immediately with a pinch of salt, scatter with parsley, and serve with lemon wedges and a bowl of aioli.
---

A taverna starter that should be eaten the moment it leaves the fryer. Dry squid + hot oil + small batches = the perfect crisp ring with a tender inside.

ingredients:
  - 2 eggs
  - 1.5 dl granulated sugar
  - Grated zest of 2 lemons and 1 orange
  - 0.5 dl freshly squeezed orange juice
  - 150 g ricotta (or quark, or kesella)
  - 1 dl olive oil
  - 2.5 dl plain flour
  - 1.5 tsp baking powder
  - 0.5 dl blue poppy seeds
  - Butter and breadcrumbs, for the tin
  - 25 g room-temperature butter (for frosting)
  - 2.5 dl icing sugar (for frosting)
  - 0.5 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 1 tsp lemon juice, plus a little grated zest
  - 50 g Philadelphia cream cheese
  - 1 tbsp poppy seeds, for decorating
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175°C. Butter a 20-cm round tin and dust with breadcrumbs.
  - Whisk the eggs and sugar to a pale, fluffy ribbon. Stir in the grated lemon and orange zest.
  - In a separate bowl, mix the squeezed orange juice with the ricotta, then drizzle in the olive oil in a thin stream while stirring.
  - Fold the ricotta mixture into the egg batter. Sift over the flour and baking powder, scatter the poppy seeds, then fold gently until just combined.
  - Pour into the tin and bake on the middle rack for 20–30 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean. Cool fully on a rack.
  - For the frosting, whisk the butter, icing sugar, vanilla sugar, lemon juice, and cream cheese to a smooth, spreadable consistency. Spread over the cooled cake and decorate with extra poppy seeds and grated lemon zest.

Ebba von Sydow's grown-up lemon and poppy seed cake, where the olive oil keeps the crumb tender and the cream cheese frosting wins over the kids. Lovely with afternoon coffee.

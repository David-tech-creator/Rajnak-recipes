---
title: Italian Tuscan Zucchini Soup
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/tuscany-zucchini-soup.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 800 g zucchini
  - 7 dl vegetable broth
  - 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra
  - 2 cloves garlic, peeled and halved
  - 3 small sage leaves
  - 1 dl dry white wine
  - 2 dl single cream
  - 1 dl sparkling mineral water (Vichy or any carbonated water)
  - 1 small aubergine, optional, for the chips
  - Neutral oil for frying
  - Salt and black pepper
instructions:
  - Bring the vegetable broth to a boil and set aside. Peel the zucchini with a vegetable peeler into long thin strips and save the peel for the chips. Cut the zucchini flesh into pieces about 5 mm thick.
  - Heat the olive oil gently in a wide saucepan with the garlic and whole sage leaves, stirring slowly until the garlic turns translucent. Remove and discard the garlic — don't let it burn.
  - Raise the heat and add the zucchini pieces. Fry for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring often so they don't catch, adding a little more oil if needed.
  - Pour in the white wine and let it bubble away. Blend the contents of the pan smooth with a stick blender or in a food processor (return to the pan if using the latter).
  - Add the broth in a steady stream while stirring, then the cream the same way. Simmer 8 to 10 minutes until full-bodied and silky. Season with salt and pepper, let cool, and chill thoroughly.
  - Just before serving, whisk in the sparkling water with a balloon whisk for a few seconds so the soup fluffs up. If you like, fry the zucchini peels and thin slices of aubergine in hot oil until crisp, drain on paper, salt, and float on top.
---

A Tuscan summer soup eaten chilled, with a final lift of sparkling water before it goes to the table. The fried zucchini and aubergine chips on top are the local flourish.

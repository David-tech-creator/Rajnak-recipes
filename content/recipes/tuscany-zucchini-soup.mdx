Boil the broth and set aside. Peel the zucchini with a potato peeler or cheese grater so that you get long narrow

strips. Save the peels for later. Cut the zucchini flesh into pieces, about 5 mm thick. Peel the garlic cloves and divide

them in half.

Put the garlic pieces in a large wide saucepan with the olive oil and the whole clean sage leaves. Heat slowly and stir

gently. Remove the garlic when it has become a little transparent. It is important that the garlic has not been burnt.

Raise the heat slightly, add the zucchini pieces and fry them soft for about 8-10 min.

Stir constantly and make sure it does not burn.

Add a little more olive oil if needed. Add the wine and let it boil. Mix the zucchini with a mixing stick or transfer to

a food processor. (Pour the mixture back into the saucepan if you are using a food processor)

Add the broth, a little at a time while stirring, and then the cream in the same way. Let it all simmer for 8-10 min.

Season with salt and pepper. The soup should now have a full-bodied and creamy texture. Let the soup cool, pour

into a suitable vessel and set in the fridge.

Before serving, add the vichy water.Whisk the soup with a balloon whisk or mix for a few seconds so that it fluffs up.

CHIPS OF AUBERGINE AND ZUCCHINI

• 1 eggplant

• zucchini peels

• oil for frying

Wash and dry the eggplant. Cut it into thin slices.

Heat the oil in a small frying pan and fry the zucchini strips and then the eggplant slices, a few at a time, until they

have turned golden brown and stiff. Drain on kitchen paper. Salt thoroughly and shake. Place on a platter with a

napkin underneath. Let each one garnish his soup.

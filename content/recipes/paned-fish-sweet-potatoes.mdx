ingredients:
  - 600 g firm white fish fillets (cod, haddock or pollock)
  - 100 g panko or coarse breadcrumbs
  - 2 tbsp paned spice mix (paprika, garlic powder, dried thyme, salt, pepper)
  - 2 eggs, beaten
  - 4 tbsp plain flour
  - 800 g sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 2-cm wedges
  - 3 tbsp olive oil
  - 300 g fresh spinach
  - 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  - 200 ml good mayonnaise
  - 1 clove garlic, crushed to a paste
  - 1 tsp lemon juice
  - 1 tsp Tabasco, plus more to taste
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Toss the sweet potato wedges with 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper, spread on a tray, and roast for 25 minutes until tender and caramelised at the edges.
  - Stir the mayonnaise with the crushed garlic, lemon juice, and Tabasco. Taste and add more Tabasco if you want a real kick. Chill until needed.
  - Mix the breadcrumbs with the paned spice mix. Pat the fish dry, dust in flour, dip in beaten egg, then press firmly into the seasoned crumbs.
  - Heat the remaining oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Fry the fish 3–4 minutes per side, until the crust is golden and the fish flakes easily.
  - Wilt the spinach in a separate pan with the sliced garlic and a pinch of salt, just until soft — a steam oven works beautifully here.
  - Plate the fish on the spinach, sweet potato wedges alongside, and a generous spoonful of Tabasco aioli for dipping.
---

A weeknight plate that feels like a small restaurant dinner: crisp-coated fish, soft roasted sweet potato, garlicky spinach, and a fiery aioli to pull it all together. The spice mix in the crumb is what makes the fish more than ordinary.

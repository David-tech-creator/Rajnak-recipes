story: |
  Julskinka is the anchor of the julbord, the long Swedish Christmas
  table that runs from herring at one end to coffee and pepparkakor at
  the other. The custom traces back to the medieval pre-Christmas
  slaughter, when the year's hog was cured to last the winter; the
  fresh ham went to the holiday table.

  The two-stage method — gentle simmer first, then a high-heat crust of
  mustard, egg yolk and breadcrumbs — is the Swedish signature. The
  result is sliced cold, eaten with strong mustard on dark rye, and
  reappears at every meal between the 23rd and Twelfth Night.

  Save the simmering broth. On Christmas Eve, the family dips bread
  into the hot pork broth — dopp i grytan, "dip in the pot" — a ritual
  older than the ham itself.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 3 kg lightly cured boneless ham (rimmad skinka)
  - 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  - 2 onions, halved
  - 2 bay leaves
  - 10 whole allspice berries
  - 10 whole black peppercorns
  - 3 tbsp coarse-grain Swedish mustard (Skånsk senap or similar)
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 1 egg yolk
  - 2 tbsp light brown sugar
  - 2 tbsp dry breadcrumbs
  - Whole cloves, optional, for studding
instructions:
  - Place the ham in a large pot, cover with cold water, and add the carrots, onions, bay, allspice, and peppercorns. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook with the lid on, allowing about 45 minutes per kilo, until a meat thermometer reads 72 °C in the centre.
  - Lift the ham from the broth and let it rest for 15 minutes (keep the broth — it's the base for dopp i grytan on Christmas Eve).
  - Carefully cut off the rind, leaving an even 5 mm layer of fat on top. Score the fat in a diamond pattern and, if you like, push a clove into each crossing.
  - Whisk the two mustards with the egg yolk and brown sugar to make a thick glaze. Brush over the ham and sprinkle the breadcrumbs on top, pressing them gently into the glaze.
  - Place the ham in a roasting tin and bake at 225 °C for 10 to 15 minutes, watching closely, until the crust is golden and crackling. Let it rest 20 minutes before slicing.
---

The undisputed centre of the Swedish julbord. The mustard-and-breadcrumb crust is non-negotiable — it's what turns a boiled ham into Christmas. Save the cooking broth for dopp i grytan on Christmas Eve.

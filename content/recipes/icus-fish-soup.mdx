story: |
  The Mediterranean fish soup tradition runs along the whole northern
  coast — bouillabaisse in Marseille, soupe de poisson all along
  Provence, cacciucco in Livorno, suquet in Catalonia — all variations
  on the same idea: cheap fish, aromatic vegetables, tomato, finished
  with a sauce stirred in at the table.

  This is the home-kitchen reading of that tradition: cleaner, lighter,
  Hungarian-French in its small choices (the fennel and the paprika
  warmth). The dollop of yoghurt or aioli at the end is the version of
  the Provençal rouille — bound mayonnaise that thickens the broth
  into something almost stew-like.

ingredients:
  - 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  - 2 stalks celery, diced
  - 1 fennel bulb, diced
  - 1 yellow onion, diced
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 can (400 g) chopped tomatoes
  - 2 fish stock cubes
  - 1 l hot water
  - 300 g salmon fillet, skinless, cut into chunks
  - 300 g firm white fish (cod, hake, or similar), cut into chunks
  - 2 dl plain yoghurt or aioli
  - 100 g small black olives (in aioli, optional)
  - 4 slices country bread
  - 100 g grated Gruyère
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
  - Flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to garnish
instructions:
  - Heat the olive oil in a heavy pot. Sauté the carrots, celery, fennel and onion gently for 10 minutes, until softened.
  - Add the chopped tomatoes, hot water, and crumble in the fish stock cubes. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  - Cook covered for 15 minutes so the vegetables turn tender and the broth tastes of the sea.
  - Season with salt and pepper to taste. Lower the heat to barely a simmer.
  - Add the salmon and white fish chunks and cook for 4–5 minutes, just until the fish flakes — don't boil hard or it will toughen.
  - Toast the bread and top with grated Gruyère; melt under the grill until bubbling.
  - Ladle the soup into bowls. Stir a spoonful of yoghurt or aioli into each, scatter with olives if using, garnish with parsley, and serve with the cheesy toast on the side.
---

Icu's clean, brothy fish soup — a Hungarian-French marriage of paprika-warmed vegetables and Mediterranean fish. The dollop of yoghurt or aioli at the end is what pulls it all together.

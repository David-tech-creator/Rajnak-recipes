story: |
  The club sandwich is a New York invention from the late 1890s,
  generally traced to the Saratoga Club House gambling parlour
  (though several Manhattan clubs claim it). Three slices of toast,
  chicken, bacon, lettuce, tomato, mayonnaise — secured with four
  cocktail sticks and cut into four triangles, point-up on the plate.

  The construction is the recipe. Toast deeply golden, mayo on both
  sides of the middle slice, Dijon in the mayo for the hotel-club
  flavour rather than school-lunch flatness. Cut diagonally and stand
  the triangles upright so the layers face the diner; that's the
  signature.

ingredients:
  - 6 slices good white sandwich bread
  - 6 rashers streaky bacon
  - 2 chicken breasts (about 300 g total)
  - 1 large ripe tomato, sliced
  - 4 leaves baby gem lettuce
  - 1 small ripe avocado, sliced (optional)
  - 4 tbsp mayonnaise
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - 4 cocktail sticks
  - A handful of crisps, to serve
instructions:
  - Slice each chicken breast in half horizontally to make 4 thin pieces. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Heat the olive oil in a frying pan over medium-high. Cook the chicken for 3 minutes a side, until golden and cooked through. Rest.
  - Fry the bacon in the same pan until very crisp. Drain on kitchen paper.
  - Toast all six slices of bread to a deep golden brown.
  - Stir the mayonnaise with the mustard and spread on one side of all the toast.
  - Build each sandwich in this order: first slice mustard mayo up, lettuce, tomato (with a pinch of salt), chicken, second slice mayo both sides, bacon, avocado, lettuce, third slice mustard mayo down.
  - Press the stack gently. Spear with cocktail sticks at four corners.
  - Cut diagonally into quarters and stand each triangle upright, point-up, on a plate with crisps alongside.
---

Three layers, four cocktail sticks, four triangles per sandwich — that's the rule. Crisp bacon, juicy chicken, properly toasted bread. The Dijon in the mayo is what makes it taste like a hotel club, not a school lunch.

ingredients:
  - 800 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 3-cm chunks
  - 200 ml plain yoghurt
  - 2 tbsp lemon juice
  - 2 tsp ground cumin
  - 2 tsp ground coriander
  - 2 tsp garam masala
  - 1 tsp turmeric
  - 1 tsp chilli powder
  - 4 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 thumb ginger, grated
  - 50 g butter
  - 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp tomato paste
  - 400 g passata or crushed tomatoes
  - 200 ml double cream
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - Salt, to taste
  - Fresh coriander, to serve
  - 4 naan breads, to serve
  - 300 g basmati rice, to serve
instructions:
  - Mix the yoghurt, lemon juice, half the spices, half the garlic, and half the ginger in a bowl. Add the chicken and marinate for at least thirty minutes, ideally overnight.
  - Cook the basmati rice in plenty of salted water for 10–12 minutes, drain, and keep warm under a clean towel.
  - Heat a heavy frying pan over high heat. Sear the chicken in batches until charred on the edges, 3–4 minutes per batch. Set aside.
  - In the same pan, melt the butter and sweat the onion until soft. Stir in the remaining garlic, ginger, and spices and cook for one minute.
  - Add the tomato paste and passata, simmer for ten minutes until thick, then pour in the cream and sugar. Stir, taste, and season with salt.
  - Return the chicken to the sauce and simmer gently for ten minutes until cooked through. Warm the naans, scatter with coriander, and serve everything with the rice.
---

The takeaway classic, cooked at home — yoghurt-marinated chicken charred in the pan, then folded into a buttery tomato-cream sauce. Worth marinating the chicken the night before.

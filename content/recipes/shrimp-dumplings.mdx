title: 'Shrimp Dumplings with Sweet Chili Sauce & Salad'
date: '2022-04-23'

prepTime: '30 mins'
cookTime: '10 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 400 g raw peeled shrimp, finely chopped
  - 2 spring onions, finely sliced
  - 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger
  - 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  - 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  - 1 tsp sesame oil
  - 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  - 0.5 tsp white pepper
  - 1 small handful coriander, chopped
  - 24 round dumpling wrappers (gyoza skins)
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil, for frying
  - 1 dl sweet chilli sauce, to serve
  - 1 small head little gem or mixed leaves
  - 1 carrot, julienned
  - 1/2 cucumber, sliced thin
  - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
instructions:
  - In a bowl, mix the shrimp, spring onions, ginger, garlic, soy, sesame oil, Shaoxing wine, white pepper, and coriander. Chill 15 minutes to firm up.
  - Place a wrapper on your palm, spoon a heaped teaspoon of filling in the centre, wet the edge with water, fold over into a half-moon, and pleat the front edge against the back. Repeat with all wrappers.
  - Heat the neutral oil in a non-stick frying pan with a lid. Arrange the dumplings flat-side down and fry 2 minutes, until golden underneath.
  - Pour in 1 dl water, cover immediately, and steam for 5 minutes. Uncover and cook another minute, until the water has gone and the bottoms are crisp.
  - Toss the salad leaves, carrot, and cucumber with the rice vinegar, olive oil, and a pinch of salt.
  - Plate the dumplings with the salad on the side and a small bowl of sweet chilli sauce for dipping.
---

Pan-fried shrimp gyoza — crisp on the bottom, juicy inside — with a fresh, crunchy salad and a sticky chilli dip. The pleating takes practice; the eating doesn't.

title: White Asparagus Soup with a Touch of Orange
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '20 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 bunch white asparagus (about 500 g)
  - 2 cubes vegetable bouillon
  - 1.25 l water
  - 40 g butter
  - 30 g plain flour
  - 2 egg yolks
  - 2 tbsp crème fraîche
  - 120 ml freshly squeezed orange juice
  - Salt and white pepper
instructions:
  - Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan and crumble in the bouillon cubes.
  - Snap off the woody base of each asparagus stalk and peel them. Cut the tips off and reserve. Add the stalks to the bouillon and simmer for 10 minutes, then add the tips and cook another 5 minutes.
  - Drain the asparagus, keeping the bouillon. Set the tips aside separately.
  - Melt the butter slowly in a clean saucepan. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1 minute, then gradually pour in 1 litre of the hot bouillon, whisking constantly. Add the asparagus stalks and simmer gently for 5 minutes.
  - Blend the soup smooth. Whisk the egg yolks together with the crème fraîche and orange juice, stir into the soup, and warm gently — do not let it boil. Add the reserved tips and season carefully with salt and white pepper.
  - Serve warm but not boiling, so the egg yolks stay silky and the orange stays bright.
---

A delicate French spring soup that takes the bitterness off white asparagus with a final lift of fresh orange juice. From the family's well-thumbed "Recettes de printemps".

ingredients:
  - 500 g IKEA frozen Köttbullar (or 500 g homemade meatballs)
  - 300 g Swedish macaroni (or short pasta)
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 2 tbsp plain flour
  - 3 dl beef stock, hot
  - 1.5 dl single cream
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - Salt, to taste
  - White pepper, to taste
  - 4 tbsp lingonberry jam, to serve
instructions:
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil and cook the macaroni until just tender, about 8 minutes. Drain and keep warm.
  - Meanwhile, heat the meatballs in a wide frying pan over medium heat, turning often, until golden and hot through — about 10 minutes from frozen.
  - Lift the meatballs out. In the same pan melt the butter, whisk in the flour, and cook for one minute.
  - Gradually whisk in the hot stock, then the cream. Simmer for two minutes until thick and silky.
  - Stir in the soy sauce and mustard. Taste and season with salt and white pepper.
  - Return the meatballs to the sauce, warm through, and serve over the macaroni with a dollop of lingonberry jam on the side.
---

The cheat's Swedish dinner — frozen IKEA Köttbullar, buttery short macaroni, and a quick brown cream sauce that tastes exactly like the cafeteria. Lingonberry jam is non-negotiable.

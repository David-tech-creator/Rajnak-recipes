  Arla — Sweden's biggest dairy co-operative — has published its own
  kladdkaka recipe for decades on butter wrappers and the back of milk
  cartons. This is that version: slightly thinner, slightly less sweet,
  one bowl, no fuss.

  Kladdkaka itself only appeared in Swedish home baking in the 1970s,
  and within a generation it had become a national fixture. The word
  kladdig means "sticky" or "gooey," and that's the entire point: a
  firm crust over a fudgy, almost-raw middle. Pull it from the oven a
  minute before you think it's ready.

ingredients:
  - 100 g Swedish butter
  - 2.5 dl caster sugar
  - 2 eggs
  - 1 dl plain flour
  - 3 tbsp cocoa powder
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
  - Icing sugar, to dust
  - Whipped cream and strawberries, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C and grease a 24-cm round tin with a removable edge, then dust with breadcrumbs.
  - Melt the butter in a saucepan and lift off the heat.
  - Stir the sugar and eggs into the warm butter and mix well.
  - Sift in the flour, cocoa, and vanilla sugar and stir gently until just combined.
  - Pour the batter into the prepared tin and bake in the middle of the oven for about 15 minutes. The cake should be low with a firm crust and a sticky middle.
  - Let the cake cool in the tin. Dust with icing sugar and serve with whipped cream and strawberries.

The Arla version of Sweden's national gooey chocolate cake — slightly thinner and a touch less sweet than most. Built around butter, cocoa, and timing: pull it before you think it's done.

servings: 12

ingredients:
  - 50 g fresh yeast (or 17 g instant dry yeast)
  - 1.5 dl whole milk, lukewarm
  - 100 g unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  - 1 egg, plus 1 egg for glazing
  - 1 dl caster sugar
  - 1 tsp ground cardamom
  - 0.5 tsp fine salt
  - 7 dl plain flour, plus more for kneading
  - 200 g almond paste (50% almonds)
  - 1 dl whole milk (for the filling)
  - 4 dl double cream
  - 1 tbsp icing sugar, plus more to dust
  - 1 tsp vanilla sugar
instructions:
  - Crumble the fresh yeast into the lukewarm milk and stir until dissolved (or whisk dry yeast into the flour later).
  - Beat in the melted butter, 1 egg, sugar, cardamom, and salt. Add the flour gradually and knead 10 minutes by stand mixer (or 15 by hand), until smooth and elastic. Cover and leave to rise 45 minutes, until doubled.
  - Tip the dough out, divide into 12 equal pieces. Shape each into a tight ball and arrange well apart on a parchment-lined tray. Cover and prove 30 minutes more.
  - Heat the oven to 220 °C. Brush the buns with beaten egg and bake for 8–10 minutes, until golden. Cool completely on a rack.
  - Slice a triangular lid off the top of each bun and scoop out a hollow from the middle. Crumble the bread into a bowl with the almond paste and the 1 dl milk, mashing to a thick paste. Spoon back into the hollows.
  - Whip the cream with icing sugar and vanilla sugar to firm peaks. Pipe a generous swirl onto each bun, replace the lids, and dust heavily with icing sugar.
  - Eat the same day, ideally with hot coffee or warm milk.
---

The cardamom-scented Swedish Lenten bun, eaten from Shrove Tuesday onwards. Almond paste in the middle, snowy whipped cream on top, icing sugar everywhere. Best within hours of filling.

story: |
  Filets de perches is the signature dish of the Lac Léman shore —
  small freshwater perch, filleted, dredged in seasoned flour and fried
  in foaming butter. Almost every lakeside restaurant from Lausanne
  through Vevey to Montreux has it on the menu, served with frites and
  tartare.

  Wild Lac Léman perch is rare now; most of what's served is pond-raised
  or imported from Eastern European lakes. The technique is what stays
  Swiss: a brief flour dredge, plenty of butter, very hot pan, fish out
  within two minutes. Wine pairing is non-negotiable: a cold Chasselas
  from the Lavaux vineyards above the lake.

ingredients:
  - 800 g perch fillets (skinless, boneless)
  - 1 dl plain flour
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 tsp white pepper
  - 80 g butter
  - 2 tbsp neutral oil
  - 2 lemons, cut into wedges
  - 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  - 800 g frozen French fries (or freshly cut potatoes)
  - Tartare sauce, to serve
instructions:
  - Cook the French fries according to package directions (or deep-fry fresh-cut potatoes at 180°C until golden).
  - Pat the perch fillets dry. Season the flour with salt and white pepper on a plate.
  - Dredge each fillet lightly in the seasoned flour and shake off the excess.
  - Heat half the butter and all the oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. When the butter foams, add the fillets in a single layer and fry for 1.5–2 minutes per side, until just golden — they cook in moments.
  - Lift onto warm plates, swirl the rest of the butter into the pan with the parsley until nutty, and spoon over the fish.
  - Serve immediately with the fries, lemon wedges, and a small bowl of tartare sauce.

The signature lakeside lunch around Lac Léman — small, sweet perch fillets fried gently in butter. Best eaten with cold white wine and your feet near the water.

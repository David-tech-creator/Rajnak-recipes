story: |
  Körözött is the paprika-pink cheese spread of the Hungarian breakfast
  table and the wine-bar (borozó) snack plate. Its origins are in
  shepherds' cheese (juhtúró) from the southern Great Plain — the same
  region that gave Hungary its paprika tradition. In the city version,
  feta and cottage cheese stand in for the sheep's curd.

  Caraway is the marker. Without it, körözött is just cheese with
  paprika; with it, the dish becomes unmistakably Central European.
  Rest it at least an hour so the paprika can colour the cheese
  through, ideally overnight. Eat with dark rye and pickled peppers.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 250 g cottage cheese (curd cheese)
  - 250 g feta cheese, crumbled
  - 1 tbsp soft butter
  - 100 g crème fraîche
  - 0.5 small white onion, finely grated
  - 1 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 tsp caraway seeds, lightly crushed
  - 0.5 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - Crusty bread or rye, to serve
  - Sliced radish or pickled peppers, to serve
instructions:
  - Press the cottage cheese through a sieve into a bowl to break up the curds.
  - Add the crumbled feta and mash everything together with a fork until almost smooth.
  - Beat in the soft butter and crème fraîche until creamy.
  - Stir in the grated onion, paprika, caraway, and salt.
  - Taste and adjust salt — feta is salty, so go gently. Cover and chill for at least one hour so the flavours come together.
  - Serve at cool room temperature with bread and pickles, dusted with a little extra paprika.
---

The pink-paprika cheese spread of every Hungarian breakfast table. Sharp from the feta, sweet from the paprika, lifted by caraway. Make it the day before — it gets better overnight.

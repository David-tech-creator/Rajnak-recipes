ingredients:
  - 1 sheet ready-made puff or shortcrust pie dough
  - 1 medium zucchini, thinly sliced
  - 2 ripe tomatoes, sliced
  - 150 g Philadelphia cream cheese
  - 2 tbsp Dijon mustard with grains
  - 100 g fresh spinach, washed
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1 tsp herbes de Provence
  - 80 g shredded cheddar
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Roll the pie dough into a tart tin and prick the base with a fork.
  - Spread the cream cheese evenly across the dough, then dot the Dijon mustard over the top.
  - Wilt the spinach briefly in a pan with a splash of olive oil and arrange it over the mustard layer.
  - Lay the zucchini and tomato slices in overlapping circles. Brush with the rest of the olive oil and sprinkle with herbes de Provence, salt and pepper.
  - Scatter the cheddar over the top and bake for 25–30 minutes, until the pastry is golden and crisp and the cheese is bubbling.
  - Let rest five minutes before slicing.
---

A crispy summer pie that turns whatever vegetables are in the fridge into a proper dinner. Serve warm with a green salad.

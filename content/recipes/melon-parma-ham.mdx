story: |
  Prosciutto e melone is the Italian summer antipasto that's been on
  trattoria menus from Emilia-Romagna to Sicily for generations. The
  classic pairing relies on Prosciutto di Parma — air-cured for at
  least twelve months in the hills around Parma, sliced as thin as
  paper — draped over ripe melon.

  Two rules. The melon must be properly ripe; underripe melon ruins
  the whole point. The ham comes out of the fridge fifteen minutes
  before serving so it can release its perfume. Pepper at the end,
  no salt — the ham brings all the salt the plate needs.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 ripe cantaloupe or Galia melon
  - 200 g Parma ham (prosciutto di Parma), thinly sliced
  - 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - A small bunch fresh basil or mint
  - Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  - 1 lemon, cut in wedges (optional)
instructions:
  - Halve the melon and scoop out the seeds.
  - Cut each half into wedges about 2 cm thick, then trim the rind from each wedge.
  - Arrange the melon wedges on a large platter or four individual plates.
  - Drape the slices of Parma ham loosely over and around the melon — don't flatten, the folds matter.
  - Drizzle with the olive oil and tear over the basil or mint leaves.
  - Finish with a generous crack of black pepper and a lemon wedge on each plate.
---

The Italian summer starter that never stops working — sweet, cold melon and salty cured ham, lifted with basil and pepper. The melon has to be ripe; everything else is a five-minute job.

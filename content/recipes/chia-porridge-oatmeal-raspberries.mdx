---
title: Chia Porridge with Oatmeal and Raspberries
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Quick & Easy
image: /images/recipes/chia-porridge-oatmeal-raspberries.jpg
prepTime: '10 mins (plus overnight)'
cookTime: '10 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - "Chia porridge:"
  - 4 tbsp chia seeds
  - 2 dl unsweetened almond milk
  - 1 tsp vanilla powder
  - 115 g frozen raspberries
  - 1 tbsp agave syrup
  - 1 dl natural skyr or yoghurt
  - "Toasted oatmeal:"
  - 125 g rolled oats
  - 1 tbsp butter
  - 1 tbsp maple syrup
  - 1 pinch salt
  - "To serve:"
  - 4 tbsp natural skyr or yoghurt
  - 10 fresh raspberries
  - 6 blackberries
  - 2 tbsp puffed quinoa
instructions:
  - The night before, stir the chia seeds together with the almond milk and vanilla powder in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate overnight so the seeds swell.
  - In the morning, blend the chilled chia mixture with the frozen raspberries and agave syrup until creamy. Stir in the dl of skyr or yoghurt.
  - For the toasted oatmeal, melt the butter in a frying pan over low heat. Add the oats and toast gently for 5 minutes, stirring.
  - Stir in the maple syrup and salt and toast another 2-3 minutes until the oats are golden and crisp.
  - Spread the oatmeal on a baking tray to cool — it crisps as it cools.
  - To serve, spoon the toasted oatmeal into the bottom of four glasses or jars.
  - Top with the chia-raspberry porridge.
  - Finish each with a spoon of skyr, fresh berries, and a scatter of puffed quinoa.
---

The Elle.se breakfast you'd make for guests you want to impress. The crunchy maple-toasted oats are the key — they keep their bite for a day even under the wet porridge. Prepare the chia the night before.

  Steak au poivre vert is the green-peppercorn variant of the classic
  French bistro steak — first codified at Maxim's in Paris in the early
  20th century, and a fixture on the table d'hôte menu ever since. The
  green peppercorns (brined, not dried black) are softer and more
  herbal, with the heat dialled down to fragrant.

  The sauce is a textbook pan reduction: shallot in the steak fond,
  crushed peppercorns, flame off the cognac, stock and cream reduced to
  a coating consistency, mustard at the end for shine. Green beans and
  frites are the French standard accompaniments.

ingredients:
  - 4 sirloin or entrecôte steaks (about 200 g each)
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 1 tbsp neutral oil
  - 2 shallots, finely chopped
  - 3 tbsp green peppercorns in brine, drained
  - 1 dl cognac or brandy
  - 2 dl beef stock
  - 2 dl double cream
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - 400 g fresh green beans, trimmed
  - Salt, to taste
instructions:
  - Take the steaks out 30 minutes before cooking. Pat dry and season with salt.
  - Bring a pot of salted water to a boil, blanch the green beans for 4 minutes until just tender, drain, and keep warm with a knob of butter.
  - Heat the oil and 1 tbsp butter in a heavy pan over high heat. Sear the steaks 3 minutes per side for medium-rare. Move to a board and rest, loosely tented.
  - Lower the heat. Add the rest of the butter and the shallots to the pan and cook gently until soft.
  - Add the green peppercorns, crushing some with the back of a spoon. Pour in the cognac and let it bubble away for 30 seconds.
  - Pour in the stock and cream, stir in the mustard, and simmer until the sauce thickens enough to coat a spoon — about 4 minutes. Taste for salt.
  - Slice the steaks against the grain, plate with the green beans, and spoon the sauce generously over the meat.

The bistro classic — peppercorn sauce done properly, with a real splash of cognac. Serve with green beans and crisp pommes frites if you want the full picture.

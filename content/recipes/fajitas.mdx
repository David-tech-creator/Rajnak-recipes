story: |
  The fajita started as a ranch-hand meal in 1930s south Texas, where
  Mexican-American vaqueros cooked the cheap skirt steak (faja, "belt"
  or "strip") that was part of their pay. It moved indoors in the 1970s
  when an Austin restaurant put sizzling-platter fajitas on the menu,
  and from there it travelled the world.

  The home version drifts further from the original — chicken instead
  of skirt steak, sour cream instead of crema — but the structure
  holds: marinated protein, charred peppers and onion, warm tortilla,
  cool topping. Slice the meat across the grain or it goes tough.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 2 chicken breast fillets
  - 2 tbsp soy sauce
  - 0.25 tsp Tabasco
  - 1 small ripe avocado
  - 1 tbsp lime juice
  - 1 tbsp chopped chives
  - 75 ml sour cream
  - 1 red pepper, sliced
  - 8 mushrooms, thickly sliced
  - 1 red onion, cut into eighths
  - 1 small iceberg lettuce or 1 bunch rocket
  - 4 wheat tortillas (about 20 cm)
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Stir the soy sauce and Tabasco together in a dish. Add the chicken, turn to coat, cover, and marinate for 30 minutes, turning once.
  - Halve the avocado, remove the stone and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Drizzle with lime juice and mash with a fork. In a second bowl, mix the sour cream with the chives. Season both with salt and pepper.
  - Slice the pepper and lettuce into strips. Cut the onion into wedges.
  - Heat a griddle pan over medium-high. Cook the chicken breasts about 5 minutes per side, then add the pepper, mushrooms, and onion to the pan and char until softened — about 5 minutes.
  - Warm the tortillas briefly in a dry pan or wrap them in foil and put in a low oven.
  - Slice the chicken. Spread avocado on each tortilla, top with lettuce, vegetables, chicken, and a spoon of chive cream. Roll and serve immediately.
---

A weeknight Mexican wrap that gets a Swedish twist with sour cream and chives. The 30-minute marinade is worth the wait.

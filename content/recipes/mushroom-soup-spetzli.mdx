  egg dough and drop them into simmering soup until they float.

  served thick enough to be a meal, sometimes thin enough to be a
  starter — both are correct.

ingredients:
  - 1 yellow onion, chopped
  - 1 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 450 g chestnut or button mushrooms, sliced
  - 4 carrots, sliced
  - 1 tbsp plain flour
  - 2 l water
  - 2–3 vegetable bouillon cubes
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 'For the spätzli (csipetke):'
  - 1 egg
  - 4 tbsp plain flour
  - 0.5 tsp salt
  - 'To serve:'
  - 2 tbsp sour cream per bowl
  - A small handful chopped parsley
instructions:
  - Heat the oil in a soup pot. Add the onion and cook over medium heat until soft and just coloured, about 6 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika. Return to the heat and add the mushrooms and carrots. Stir for a minute.
  - Sprinkle in the flour and stir to coat. Pour in the water, crumble in the bouillon cubes, and bring to the boil. Simmer for 20 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  - Meanwhile, whisk the egg, flour, and salt to a thick, sticky dough.
  - Once the soup is simmering, pinch off pea-sized pieces of dough with a teaspoon and drop them into the soup one by one. They are cooked when they float to the surface, after 3–4 minutes.
  - Taste, season with salt and pepper, and ladle into bowls. Top each with a spoon of sour cream and a scatter of parsley.
---

The Hungarian mushroom soup — paprika onions, lots of mushrooms, little pinched dumplings cooked right in the broth. Sour cream on top is essential.

ingredients:
  - 2 l water
  - 1 vegetable bouillon cube
  - 1 red chili, sliced
  - 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  - 150 g shiitake mushrooms (or other mushrooms)
  - 350 g silken tofu, cut into cubes
  - 4 spring onions, cut into 2-cm pieces
  - 180 g bean sprouts
  - 4 eggs
  - 3 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 6 tbsp Japanese soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp sesame oil
  - 200 g egg noodles
  - 2 tbsp white miso paste
  - 100 g spinach
  - 2 small pak choi, halved
  - 300 g sirloin steak, sliced thin (optional)
  - 1 tbsp black sesame seeds
instructions:
  - Bring the water to a boil with the bouillon cube. Add the sliced chili and garlic and simmer for 10 minutes to make the broth.
  - Boil the eggs for exactly 7 minutes for jammy yolks, then drop into ice water. Peel and halve.
  - Heat the rapeseed oil in a hot pan and fry the mushrooms until deeply golden. Set aside. In the same pan, sear the sliced beef briefly, just 30 seconds per side. Set aside.
  - Strain the broth into a clean pot, pressing on the aromatics. Bring back to a boil and stir in the soy sauce and sesame oil.
  - Add the egg noodles and cook for 4 minutes. In the last minute, add the pak choi to wilt.
  - Off the heat, stir in the miso paste (never boil miso). Add the tofu cubes, spring onions, and bean sprouts.
  - Place a handful of spinach in the bottom of each bowl and ladle over the hot broth and noodles. Top with seared beef, mushrooms, egg halves, and a sprinkle of black sesame seeds.
---

A weeknight ramen that doesn't pretend to be from a 12-hour broth, but earns its place anyway. The miso goes in off the heat — that's where the savoury depth comes from.

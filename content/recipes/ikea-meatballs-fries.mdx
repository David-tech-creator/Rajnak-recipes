ingredients:
  - 500 g minced beef and pork mix
  - 1 small yellow onion, finely grated
  - 1 dl breadcrumbs
  - 1 dl milk
  - 1 egg
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 tsp white pepper
  - 0.5 tsp ground allspice
  - 2 tbsp butter, for frying
  - 1 bag IKEA frozen French fries (about 750 g)
  - 4 tbsp lingonberry jam, to serve
  - 'For the cream sauce:'
  - 2 tbsp butter
  - 2 tbsp plain flour
  - 3 dl beef stock, hot
  - 1.5 dl single cream
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
instructions:
  - Soak the breadcrumbs in the milk for five minutes, then mix with the minced meat, grated onion, egg, salt, white pepper, and allspice until just combined.
  - Roll the mixture into walnut-sized balls with damp hands. Chill for ten minutes while you start the fries.
  - Bake the French fries on a tray according to the package, usually 200 °C for 18–20 minutes, turning once.
  - Melt the butter in a wide frying pan and brown the meatballs all over, shaking the pan so they keep their round shape. Cook in batches if needed, about 8 minutes total. Lift out and keep warm.
  - For the sauce, melt the butter in the same pan, whisk in the flour, then gradually add the hot stock and cream. Simmer two minutes, then stir in soy and mustard. Season.
  - Return the meatballs to the sauce, warm through, and serve with the crispy fries and a generous spoon of lingonberry jam on the side.

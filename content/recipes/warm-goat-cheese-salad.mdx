ingredients:
  - 1 small log (200 g) firm goat cheese, sliced into 4 thick rounds
  - 4 thin slices of baguette
  - 2 tbsp runny honey, plus more to finish
  - 1 small head iceberg or romaine lettuce, torn
  - 2 carrots, grated
  - 1 dl sweetcorn
  - 12 cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  - 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - 2 tbsp pine nuts or chopped walnuts, toasted
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 220 °C. Place each baguette slice on a baking sheet and top with a slice of goat cheese.
  - Drizzle a teaspoon of honey over each round of cheese and bake for 6 to 8 minutes, until the cheese is golden and bubbling.
  - Meanwhile, whisk the olive oil, vinegar, mustard, salt, and pepper in a small bowl for the dressing.
  - Toss the lettuce with the carrots, sweetcorn, and tomatoes in a wide bowl. Drizzle with the dressing and divide among four plates.
  - Place a hot goat cheese toast on each salad. Scatter the toasted nuts and finish with a final thread of honey before serving.
---

The salade chèvre chaud you find in every French bistro — crisp toast, hot bubbling goat cheese, cold salad underneath. The honey is non-negotiable.

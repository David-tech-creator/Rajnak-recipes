---
title: Spicy Tuna with Crispy Rice
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/spicy-tuna-crispy-rice.jpg
prepTime: '30 mins'
cookTime: '20 mins'
servings: 4
kitchenNote: Press the rice firm. Fry until truly crisp.
signoff: Itadakimasu.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 300 g sushi rice
  - 360 ml water
  - 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 300 g sashimi-grade tuna, finely diced
  - 3 tbsp mayonnaise (Kewpie if possible)
  - 2 tbsp sriracha
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  - 1 spring onion, finely sliced
  - 1 small jalapeño, thinly sliced
  - 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  - Neutral oil, for frying
  - Black and white sesame seeds, to garnish
instructions:
  - Rinse the rice until the water runs clear, then cook with the 360 ml water in a covered pot until tender, about 18 minutes. Stir in the vinegar, sugar, and salt while the rice is hot.
  - Press the warm rice into a parchment-lined tray to a thickness of about 2 cm. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight, until very firm.
  - Cut the chilled rice into rectangles roughly 4 by 6 cm. Heat a generous layer of neutral oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat and fry the rice pieces until deep golden and crisp on both sides, about 3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towel.
  - Mix the diced tuna with the mayonnaise, sriracha, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Fold in the spring onion. Taste and adjust heat with more sriracha if you like.
  - Top each crispy rice piece with a slice of avocado, a spoonful of spicy tuna, a slice of jalapeño, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Serve immediately while the rice is still warm.
---

The Nobu-style snack that took over Los Angeles. Make the rice the night before — it has to be cold and firm or it falls apart in the pan.

title: "Umamido Chef's Ramen with Pork"

prepTime: '30 mins'
cookTime: '4 h'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 800 g pork belly, in one piece
  - 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  - 1 thumb fresh ginger, sliced
  - 2 spring onions, white parts only
  - 2 l good chicken or pork stock
  - 4 tbsp white miso paste
  - 3 tbsp soy sauce
  - 2 tbsp mirin
  - 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
  - 1 tbsp tahini or unsweetened sesame paste
  - 4 portions fresh ramen noodles
  - 4 soft-boiled eggs, marinated in soy if you can
  - 2 sheets nori, halved
  - 1 small handful bean sprouts
  - 2 spring onions, sliced, for garnish
  - 1 small handful sliced bamboo shoots (menma), optional
  - Chili oil or rayu, to serve
instructions:
  - Roll the pork belly tight and tie with butcher's twine. Brown all over in a heavy pot, then add the garlic, ginger, spring onion whites, and enough water to cover. Simmer covered for 3 hours until completely tender. Cool the pork in the liquid, then refrigerate.
  - When ready to serve, slice the chilled pork into thin discs and sear them briefly in a dry pan until golden and a little crisp at the edges.
  - For the broth, bring the stock to a simmer in a clean pot. Whisk in the miso, soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, and tahini. Taste and adjust — it should be salty, deep, and slightly sweet.
  - Cook the ramen noodles in a large pot of boiling water according to the packet, then drain well.
  - Divide the noodles among four deep bowls. Pour the hot broth over.
  - Top each bowl with slices of pork, a halved soft-boiled egg, bean sprouts, bamboo shoots, sliced spring onion, a sheet of nori standing up at the side, and a drizzle of chili oil.
---

Inspired by Umamido in Tokyo and Brussels — a creamy, deeply savoury pork ramen built on a tonkotsu-style stock and white miso. Slow-cooked pork belly the day before makes assembly easy.

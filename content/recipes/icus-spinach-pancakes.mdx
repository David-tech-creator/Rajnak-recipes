---
title: "Icus' Spinach Pancakes with Pesto & Vegetables"
date: '2026-05-23'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/icus-spinach-pancakes.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '25 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 200 g fresh spinach
  - 3 dl plain flour
  - 4 dl whole milk
  - 3 eggs
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 2 tbsp butter, melted
  - Butter or oil, for frying
  - 6 tbsp basil pesto
  - 250 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 1 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  - 1 yellow pepper, sliced
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 150 g feta or goat cheese, crumbled
  - Salt and black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Wilt the spinach in a dry pan with a splash of water, just until collapsed. Cool slightly, squeeze out the water, and chop finely. Blend or finely mince to a near-purée.
  - In a bowl, whisk the flour with a pinch of salt. Add the eggs and gradually whisk in the milk to a smooth batter. Stir in the melted butter and the spinach purée — the batter will be a vivid green.
  - Let the batter rest 15 minutes.
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Toss the cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and pepper with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for 20 minutes until soft and lightly caramelised.
  - Heat a non-stick crêpe pan over medium-high. Brush with butter or oil. Pour a ladle of batter into the pan and swirl to coat. Cook 1.5 minutes, flip, and cook 30 seconds more. Stack on a warm plate. Repeat to make 8 pancakes.
  - Spread each pancake with pesto, top with roasted vegetables and crumbled feta, then roll or fold into quarters. Serve warm.
---

Icu's clever way to get green onto the dinner plate — spinach goes straight into the crêpe batter, then everything good gets folded inside. A vegetarian crowd-pleaser.

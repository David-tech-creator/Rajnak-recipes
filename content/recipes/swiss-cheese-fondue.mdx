---
title: Swiss Cheese Fondue
date: '2022-02-09'

image: /images/recipes/swiss-cheese-fondue.jpg
prepTime: '10 mins'
cookTime: '20 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 400 g Gruyère, grated
  - 400 g Vacherin Fribourgeois, grated (moitié-moitié blend)
  - 1 clove garlic, halved
  - 3 dl dry white wine (Fendant or another Chasselas)
  - 1 tbsp lemon juice
  - 1 tbsp cornflour
  - 2 tbsp kirsch
  - A few grinds of black pepper
  - Freshly grated nutmeg
  - 1 large rustic loaf, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - Boiled new potatoes, optional
  - Pickled cocktail onions and cornichons, to serve
  - Charcuterie (viande séchée des Grisons, jambon cru), to serve
  - Green salad, to serve
instructions:
  - Rub the inside of the caquelon (fondue pot) with the cut garlic clove, then discard.
  - Pour in the wine and lemon juice, place over medium heat, and bring to a gentle simmer.
  - Add the grated cheeses a handful at a time, stirring constantly in a figure-eight motion with a wooden spoon until smooth and silky.
  - Mix the cornflour with the kirsch in a small bowl and stir it into the fondue. Cook another minute, until thickened and glossy.
  - Season with black pepper and a small grating of nutmeg. Move the pot to the rechaud (table burner) at low flame.
  - Spear bread cubes (and potatoes, if using) onto long forks, dip and swirl through the cheese, and serve with pickles, charcuterie, and a sharp green salad.
---

Switzerland's national dish and the right thing to eat on the coldest evening of the year. The moitié-moitié (half Gruyère, half Vacherin) is the Fribourg standard — the smoothest, creamiest blend.

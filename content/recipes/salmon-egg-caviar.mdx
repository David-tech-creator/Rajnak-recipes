---
title: 'Salmon, Egg & Caviar for Christmas and Easter'
date: '2022-01-28'
category: Christmas & Easter
image: /images/recipes/salmon-egg-caviar.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '10 mins'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 400 g good cold-smoked salmon, thinly sliced
  - 6 eggs
  - 100 g lumpfish or trout caviar
  - 1 head soft butter lettuce, leaves separated
  - 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  - 1 small bunch fresh dill
  - 2 tbsp crème fraîche (optional, for the eggs)
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Buttered rye bread, to serve
instructions:
  - Bring a saucepan of water to a boil. Lower the eggs in gently and cook for 9 minutes for firm, just-set yolks. Cool under cold running water, peel, and slice in halves.
  - If you like, spoon a tiny dot of crème fraîche onto each egg half before plating.
  - Arrange the lettuce leaves in a fan on a large platter or six individual plates.
  - Drape the smoked salmon slices loosely over the lettuce, leaving room for the eggs.
  - Tuck the egg halves between the salmon. Spoon a generous teaspoon of caviar onto each egg.
  - Tear over plenty of fresh dill, tuck lemon wedges around the platter, and grind black pepper across everything. Serve at once with buttered rye bread.
---

A staple of the Rajnak family Christmas and Easter table — silky smoked salmon, soft-boiled eggs, and glistening caviar arranged on a platter. Simple, elegant, and always the first dish to disappear.

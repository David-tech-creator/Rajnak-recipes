ingredients:
  - 200 g digestive biscuits
  - 100 g butter, melted
  - 600 g full-fat cream cheese, at room temperature
  - 150 g caster sugar
  - 3 eggs, at room temperature
  - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - 200 ml sour cream
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - 1 tbsp plain flour
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 160°C. Lightly grease a 23-cm springform tin and line the base with baking paper.
  - Crush the biscuits to fine crumbs in a food processor (or by hand in a bag with a rolling pin). Stir in the melted butter, then press firmly into the base of the tin. Chill while you make the filling.
  - Beat the cream cheese with the sugar in a large bowl until completely smooth — don't whip air into it.
  - Add the eggs one at a time, mixing just enough to incorporate each one.
  - Stir in the vanilla, lemon zest, flour, and sour cream until smooth.
  - Pour the filling over the chilled base and smooth the top.
  - Bake for 45-50 minutes. The edges should be set but the centre should still wobble like jelly when you nudge the tin.
  - Turn the oven off, prop the door open with a wooden spoon, and let the cake cool inside for an hour. Then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
---

A proper New York-style cheesecake: dense, creamy, with the slightest tang from the sour cream. All ingredients must be at room temperature or the batter will split. A water bath helps prevent cracks but isn't essential if you cool it slowly.

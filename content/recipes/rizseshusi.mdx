story: |
  Rizseshús — "rice with meat" — is the Hungarian one-pot answer
  to risotto, but cooked the other direction: the meat takes the
  paprika base first, the rice is folded in to absorb every drop
  of the seasoned broth. School canteens, factory cafeterias and
  household kitchens have all served it for generations.

  Toasting the rice before adding the water is not optional. Dry
  grains in a little fat seal at the surface so each one cooks
  separate; tipped straight into liquid, they collapse into
  porridge. The pressure cooker is the modern shortcut — it
  finishes everything in twenty minutes — but a heavy lidded pot
  works on the stove if you let it rest off the heat.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  - 3 tbsp neutral oil or lard
  - 3-4 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 kg pork or veal stew meat, cut into 1-cm cubes
  - 6 to 7 dl long-grain rice
  - 3 meat stock cubes
  - 1.2 to 1.5 l water
  - 1 ripe tomato (optional)
  - 1 green pepper, sliced (optional)
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Sauerkraut-filled yellow peppers, gherkins, or Hungarian cucumber salad, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oil in a pressure cooker over medium heat, enough to cover the bottom. Add the chopped onion and fry until deep golden, about 8 minutes.
  - Pull the pot off the heat and stir in the paprika — it must not burn. Return to low heat, add the meat and salt, and pour in 1 dl water. Stir to coat everything in the paprika base.
  - In a separate dry frying pan, toast the rice with 2 tbsp oil over medium heat, stirring, until the grains turn glossy and just take on a hint of colour — do not let them brown.
  - Tip the rice into the pressure cooker. Pour in 1.2 to 1.5 l water and crumble in the meat stock cubes.
  - If you like, add a whole tomato and slices of green pepper for sweetness.
  - Close the pressure cooker, bring up to full pressure on maximum heat. After two rings (about 5 minutes), lower to a 3-4 strength simmer.
  - Cook for 20 minutes at low pressure, then release pressure naturally and rest 5 minutes before serving.
  - Spoon onto plates with sauerkraut-stuffed peppers, pickled gherkins, or Hungarian cucumber salad.

A Hungarian one-pot of paprika meat and rice cooked together — nagymama's pressure-cooker version that lands on the table in 30 minutes. The pickles on the side aren't optional.

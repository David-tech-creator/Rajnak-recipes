ingredients:
  - 1.5 kg minced meat (mixed beef and pork)
  - 2 yellow onions, chopped
  - 1–2 cloves garlic, pressed
  - 6 eggs
  - 2 meat bouillon cubes, dissolved in 0.5 dl hot water
  - 0.5 baguette, soaked in milk and squeezed
  - 2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 tsp dried marjoram
  - 2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - 1 tsp ground black pepper
  - 3 tbsp rapeseed oil, plus more for frying
  - 2 dl breadcrumbs, for rolling
instructions:
  - Heat the 3 tbsp rapeseed oil in a frying pan and cook the chopped onion over medium heat until soft and golden, about 8 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika so it doesn't burn. Let cool slightly.
  - In a very large bowl, combine the minced meat, paprika onions, pressed garlic, eggs, dissolved bouillon, soaked baguette, marjoram, salt, and pepper.
  - Mix evenly with your hands until well combined. Don't overwork.
  - Roll the mixture into small even balls (about a heaped tablespoon each). Roll each ball through breadcrumbs to coat lightly.
  - Fry the meatballs in plenty of rapeseed oil over medium heat, turning regularly, until deep golden all over and cooked through, about 10 minutes per batch.
---

The big-batch Hungarian household meatballs — paprika-perfumed, generously seasoned, breadcrumb-rolled so the outside crackles. Makes enough for a Sunday lunch with cold leftovers for sandwiches.

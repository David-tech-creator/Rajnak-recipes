story: |
  Red and black currants — vinbär in Swedish — grow on every other
  Swedish summerhouse plot, and by July there's always more fruit than
  jam jars to hold it. Kokosvinbärskaka is the standard rescue cake:
  one melted saucepan of butter, a folded batter, a top scattered with
  fresh berries.

  Coconut gives it the chew that currants alone wouldn't have, and the
  tart fruit cuts the sweetness back. Equally good with frozen berries
  in February, when you've forgotten what summer tasted like.

ingredients:
  - 125 g butter, plus extra for greasing
  - 3 eggs
  - 1.5 dl caster sugar
  - 2 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 2 dl plain flour
  - 1.5 dl desiccated coconut, plus 0.75 dl for the tin
  - 1 tsp baking powder
  - 225 g fresh or frozen red or black currants (about 3.5 dl)
  - Whipped cream or vanilla ice cream, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200 °C. Generously butter a 20-cm round tin with a removable edge and dust the inside with the 0.75 dl coconut.
  - Melt the butter in a small saucepan and lift off the heat.
  - Add the eggs, sugar, vanilla sugar, flour, the 1.5 dl coconut, and baking powder to the warm butter and stir to a smooth batter with a spoon.
  - Fold in half the currants. Pour the batter into the prepared tin and scatter the remaining currants on top.
  - Bake in the middle of the oven for 25–30 minutes. The edges should be lightly golden and the centre pale but no longer wet.
  - Cool under a tea towel. Serve lukewarm or cold with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

A Swedish summer cake — coconut and tart red currants in a one-saucepan batter. Forgiving, fast, and just as good with frozen berries in February.

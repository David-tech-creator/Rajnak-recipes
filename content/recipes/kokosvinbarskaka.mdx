1. Grease a round form, preferably with a removable edge or pie form,

about 20 cm in diameter and “toast” it with coconut flakes or breadcrumbs.

Turn on the oven at 200 ° C.

(A tip is to use a small spatula to grease the mold with butter.)

about 0.75 dl coconut flakes to the mold

2. Bring out a saucepan and melt the butter. Remove the pan from the heat.

3. Add eggs, powdered sugar, vanilla sugar, wheat flour and coconut flakes

(1.5 dl) and stir to a smooth batter with a spoon or fork.

4. Add half of the currants to the batter and mix them down.

5. Pour the batter into the mold. Sprinkle over the rest of the currants.

6. Bake the currant cake in the middle of the oven at 200 ° C for about 25-30

minutes until the cake has a slightly golden color at the edges but pale in

the middle. It may look a little damp in the middle but should not be sticky.

7. Let the currant cake cool under a baking towel. Serve the currant cake

lukewarm or cool as it is or with ice cream or whipped cream or custard.

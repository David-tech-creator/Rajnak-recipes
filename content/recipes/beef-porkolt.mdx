title: Beef Pörkölt
date: '2022-01-15'

image: /images/recipes/beef-porkolt.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '2 h'
servings: 4
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Add paprika off the heat, never on it.'
signoff: 'Jó étvágyat.'
ingredients:
  - 800 g - 1 kg beef shin or chuck, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 2-3 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 big yellow onion (or 2 small), finely chopped
  - 1-2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - 1 large ripe tomato, chopped
  - 1 green pepper, sliced (optional)
  - 2 beef bouillon cubes
  - Water, to cover
  - 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oil in a heavy pot. Add the chopped onion and fry over medium heat until it takes on a golden colour, about 8 minutes.
  - Pull the pot off the heat and stir in the paprika — it must not burn or it will turn bitter. Tip the beef in straight away and stir to coat.
  - Return to medium heat. Stir for a few minutes so the meat browns on all sides, then salt it.
  - Pour in just enough water to cover the meat, drop in the bouillon cubes, and put the lid on tight.
  - Simmer gently for about 2 hours, or 40 minutes in a pressure cooker (start high, then turn down to medium once two rings show).
  - About halfway through, add the chopped tomato and green pepper if using.
  - Cook until the beef is fork-tender. The sauce should be neither thin nor thick — coat-the-spoon consistency.
  - Serve in deep plates over freshly made nokedli (spätzli), scattered with parsley.
---

The Hungarian backbone of our family table. Paprika and onions and beef, slow-cooked until the meat surrenders. Always with nokedli on the side — the little dumplings are made for catching the sauce.

  Sweet chili sauce — sticky, garlicky, lightly chilli-hot — is Thai
  in origin (nam jim kai) but became a global supermarket fixture in
  the 1990s. Used here as a one-bottle glaze, it does ten ingredients'

  Squeeze lime over the avocado the moment you cut it, or it browns
  before you serve.

ingredients:
  - 800 g chicken thigh fillets, cut into bite-sized pieces
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 4 tbsp sweet chili sauce
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 300 g jasmine rice
  - "Avocado sauce:"
  - 2 ripe avocados
  - 2 dl crème fraîche
  - 1 tbsp lime juice
  - 1 small clove garlic, minced
  - Pinch of salt and black pepper
  - 1 tbsp chopped coriander, to serve
instructions:
  - Cook the rice in plenty of salted water until tender, then drain and keep warm.
  - Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium-high. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer and season with salt.
  - Fry the chicken for about 6 minutes, turning occasionally, until lightly browned all over.
  - Reduce the heat to medium, pour in the chili sauce, and toss to coat. Cook another 3-4 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and clings to the chicken.
  - For the avocado sauce, scoop the avocado flesh into a bowl and mash with a fork. Stir in the crème fraîche, lime juice, garlic, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  - Spoon rice onto each plate, top with the sticky chili chicken, and add a generous dollop of avocado sauce.
  - Scatter with coriander and serve.
---

Quick chili-glazed chicken with a cooling avocado sauce — the contrast is the dish. Use thigh meat for juiciness. Equally good over rice or with potato wedges and grilled zucchini on the side.

title: Potato Gratin
date: '2022-02-16'

prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '55 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 kg large floury potatoes
  - 2 cloves garlic
  - 4 dl double cream
  - 200 g grated cheese (gruyère or a mix of gruyère and cheddar)
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 generous pinch black pepper
  - Freshly grated nutmeg, to taste
  - 1 knob butter, for the dish
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C. Butter a gratin dish and rub the inside with one of the garlic cloves, halved.
  - Peel the potatoes and slice them very thinly (a mandoline is ideal).
  - Layer the potato slices in the dish, seasoning each layer with salt, pepper, and a little nutmeg. Finely chop the second garlic clove and scatter between the layers.
  - Pour the cream over so the potatoes are almost — but not quite — covered.
  - Bake for 30 minutes at 175 °C. Remove and scatter the grated cheese evenly over the top.
  - Return to the oven and bake at 200 °C for another 25 minutes, until the top is golden brown and bubbling. Cover loosely with foil if it browns too quickly.
  - Let the gratin rest for 5 minutes before serving — it needs that time to set.
---

A rich, creamy potato gratin with garlic and a golden lid of melted cheese. A French classic that goes with anything roasted, especially lamb or beef. Don't skip the resting — straight from the oven, it runs.

servings: 4

kitchenNote: 'One almond hidden inside. Whoever finds it marries first.'
signoff: 'God jul.'
ingredients:
  - 150 g short-grain pudding rice (risgryn)
  - 5 dl whole milk
  - 2 dl water
  - 20 g butter
  - 50 g caster sugar
  - 20 g vanilla sugar
  - 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  - 1 pinch salt
  - "To serve:"
  - Ground cinnamon
  - Cold milk
  - 1 whole blanched almond
instructions:
  - Put the rice, water, milk, salt, and the cinnamon stick if using into a heavy pot.
  - Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring once or twice. Watch carefully — milk loves to boil over.
  - Lower the heat as far as it goes, cover with a lid, and cook very slowly for 35-40 minutes, stirring every five minutes or so, until the rice is soft and the porridge thick and creamy.
  - Stir in the butter, sugar, and vanilla sugar at the end. Fish out the cinnamon stick.
  - Hide one whole blanched almond somewhere in the pot — tradition says whoever finds it will marry in the year ahead.
  - Spoon into bowls and dust with cinnamon. Pass a jug of cold milk for each diner to pour into the well of their porridge.
---

The Swedish Christmas porridge — for the Tomte (the household elf) on the porch, and for the family at the table. The almond is the heart of the tradition. Eat it slowly, with cold milk and lots of cinnamon.

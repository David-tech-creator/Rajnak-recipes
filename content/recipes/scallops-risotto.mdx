title: 'Scallops with Green Beans & Risotto'
date: '2022-04-07'
category: Found Recipes
image: /images/recipes/scallops-risotto.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '30 mins'
servings: 4
kitchenNote: Scallops bone-dry. Pan smoking.
signoff: Buon appetito.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 12 large scallops (3 per person), roe removed
  - 250 g fine green beans, trimmed
  - 300 g carnaroli or arborio rice
  - 1 shallot, finely chopped
  - 1 dl dry white wine
  - 1 l hot fish or light chicken stock
  - 50 g unsalted butter, divided
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 60 g parmesan, finely grated
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - 1 tbsp neutral oil, for the scallops
  - Flaky sea salt and black pepper, to taste
  - Chervil or chives, to garnish
instructions:
  - Keep the stock hot in a small saucepan. Cook the green beans in boiling salted water for 4 minutes, drain, and refresh in iced water.
  - Sweat the shallot in olive oil and half the butter for 4 minutes. Stir in the rice and toast for 2 minutes. Pour in the wine and stir until absorbed.
  - Add hot stock one ladle at a time, stirring gently and waiting until each is absorbed before adding the next. After 18 minutes, the rice should be creamy with a slight bite.
  - Pull the pan off the heat. Beat in the remaining butter, parmesan, and lemon zest. Season and cover to rest.
  - Pat the scallops bone-dry and season with sea salt. Heat the neutral oil in a heavy frying pan until shimmering. Sear the scallops 90 seconds on one side, until deeply golden, then flip and cook another 30 seconds. Lift onto a warm plate.
  - Warm the green beans through in the scallop pan with a knob of butter.
  - Spoon the risotto into shallow bowls, top with three scallops each, lean a few beans alongside, and finish with cracked pepper and chervil.
---

A restaurant-style plate that works at home if you do everything in the right order: risotto loose, beans blanched, pan smoking before the scallops go in. Get those three right and it's effortless.

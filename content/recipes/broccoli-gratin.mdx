story: |
  A weekday gratin in the French-Swiss tradition: blanched vegetable,
  béchamel, cheese, oven. The construction is medieval at heart — the
  white sauce dates to the court cooking of Louis XIV and the gratin
  surface from the verb gratter, "to scrape." What's earned its keep
  for three centuries is the simplicity.

  Two technical points. Blanch the broccoli for one minute, no more,
  and drain it bone-dry; otherwise the gratin will swim. Build the
  béchamel patiently so it's silky, not gluey. A grating of nutmeg is
  the classical signature.
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Blanch the broccoli still bright green.'
signoff: 'Tuck in.'
ingredients:
  - 800 g broccoli, cut into florets
  - 150 g streaky bacon, sliced
  - 150 g grated cheese (Gruyère or matured cheddar)
  - 3 tbsp butter
  - 4.5 tbsp plain flour
  - 6 dl whole milk
  - 1 tsp sea salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - A grating of nutmeg (optional)
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 225°C and butter a large gratin dish.
  - Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil. Drop the broccoli florets in for 1 minute — just to set the green colour. Drain very well.
  - 'Make the white sauce: melt the butter in a small pan over low heat. Stir in the flour and cook for one minute without colouring.'
  - Whisk in the milk in a steady stream, then cook gently for 4-5 minutes, stirring, until silky and thick. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  - Spread the drained broccoli in the gratin dish and lay the bacon slices over the top.
  - Pour the white sauce evenly over the broccoli and bacon.
  - Scatter all the grated cheese on top.
  - Bake for 20-30 minutes, until the cheese is deeply golden and the sauce is bubbling at the edges. Rest 5 minutes before serving.
---

A perfect cold-evening side or vegetarian-ish main. The bacon and broccoli char a little under the cheese and that's where the flavour is. Don't overcook the broccoli before baking — it goes again in the oven.

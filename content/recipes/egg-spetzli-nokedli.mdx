story: |
  Nokedli in Hungarian, Spätzli in Switzerland and southern Germany —
  the same small, rough egg noodle pressed straight from the batter
  into boiling water. They are the canonical accompaniment to pörkölt
  and paprikás csirke in Hungary, and to game and braises across the
  Alps.

  The dough is on purpose unrefined: thick, sticky, almost lazy. You
  press it through a Spätzlehobel (or a colander with large holes) so
  the noodles fall in irregular shapes, with all the rough edges that
  catch sauce. Make them right before serving — they stiffen as they
  cool.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g plain flour
  - 2 whole eggs
  - 1 tsp salt
  - About 3 dl water, more as needed
  - 2 tbsp olive oil, for tossing
  - 1 spätzli maker (or a colander with large holes)
instructions:
  - Bring a wide pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil.
  - In a bowl, whisk the eggs with the salt. Add the flour and enough water to make a thick, sticky dough — neither too soft nor too firm. Stir just enough to combine; don't overmix.
  - Mix the dough right before cooking, since it firms up while standing.
  - Hold the spätzli maker over the boiling water and press the dough through the holes, letting the noodles drop in. Stir gently with a wooden spoon so they don't stick.
  - They are ready as soon as they rise to the top — about 1–2 minutes. Lift out with a slotted spoon.
  - Rinse briefly under cold water, drain, then toss in a bowl with the olive oil to keep them loose.
---

The Hungarian-Swiss handmade noodle that turns up beside every pörkölt at home. Make a fresh batch each time — they keep about a day in the fridge but never as good as straight from the pot.

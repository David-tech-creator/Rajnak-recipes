ingredients:
  - 500 g mixed ripe tomatoes (cherry, heirloom, plum), various colours
  - 400 g thick Greek yoghurt
  - 1 small clove garlic, crushed
  - 1.5 tbsp lemon juice
  - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus more to finish
  - 1 tsp sumac
  - 2 tsp dried mint (or 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped)
  - 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  - 1 tbsp toasted pine nuts (optional)
  - Flaky sea salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - A pinch of Aleppo or chilli flakes
  - 'To serve:'
  - Warm flatbread or sourdough
instructions:
  - Cut the tomatoes into a mix of wedges, halves, and slices — different shapes make it look more lively.
  - Sprinkle the tomatoes lightly with salt and leave to sit for 10 minutes so they release some juice.
  - Stir the yoghurt with the crushed garlic, 0.5 tbsp lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Spread in a wide swoop on a large platter.
  - Whisk the remaining lemon juice, olive oil, half the sumac, and the dried mint with a pinch of salt to make a quick dressing.
  - Arrange the tomatoes over the yoghurt. Spoon the dressing over generously.
  - Finish with the pomegranate molasses zigzagged across, pine nuts if using, the remaining sumac, chilli flakes, and a final drizzle of olive oil. Serve with warm flatbread.

servings: 4
difficulty: Easy
story: |
  Pickled herring in countless variations is the cornerstone of the
  Swedish julbord and the påskbord. Abba, founded in Kungshamn on the
  west coast in 1838, made the mustard-and-onion version a household
  staple — sweet, sharp, and ready from the jar.

  The plate is built on contrast: hot waxy potatoes, cold creamy
  herring, sharp raw onion, dry crisp knäckebröd. A sliver of aged
  Västerbotten or another firm Swedish cheese closes the circle. Plenty
  of dill, always.
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Hot potato, cold herring. Plenty of dill.'
signoff: 'God jul.'
ingredients:
  - 2 jars Abba mustard & onion herring (250 g each)
  - 800 g small new potatoes, scrubbed
  - 2 dl sour cream (gräddfil works too)
  - 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  - 150 g aged hard cheese, sliced (Västerbotten or similar)
  - 1 bunch fresh dill, chopped
  - 1 packet knäckebröd (crisp rye bread)
  - Sea salt, for the potato water
  - Freshly ground black pepper
instructions:
  - Bring a pot of salted water to the boil and simmer the new potatoes until tender, about 20 minutes. Drain and keep warm.
  - Spoon the herring into a small serving bowl, straight from the jar. Don't drain off the mustard-onion sauce — that's the point.
  - Tip the sour cream into a second bowl and scatter the chopped red onion on top with a grind of pepper.
  - Arrange the sliced cheese on a board with the knäckebröd alongside.
  - 'Set everything on the table at once. Each person builds their own plate: hot potatoes, a spoon of herring, a dollop of sour cream and onion, a wedge of cheese, and knäckebröd on the side.'
  - Shower with fresh dill just before eating.
---

Abba's mustard & onion sill is the cornerstone of every Swedish julbord and a fixture at Easter too. The combination of warm potato, cold creamy herring, sharp onion, and dry crispbread is the whole point of Christmas in our house.

title: "Burger & Lobster Hamburger"

prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '10 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 800 g chuck mince (about 20% fat), divided into 4 patties
  - 4 brioche buns, halved
  - 4 slices mature cheddar
  - 1 large red onion, sliced into thin rings
  - 4 leaves baby gem lettuce
  - 1 large beef tomato, sliced
  - 8 sliced gherkins
  - 1 tbsp neutral oil
  - Flaky sea salt and black pepper
  - "Burger sauce:"
  - 4 tbsp mayonnaise
  - 2 tbsp ketchup
  - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  - 1 tsp gherkin brine
  - ½ tsp smoked paprika
  - A few drops Worcestershire sauce
instructions:
  - Stir the sauce ingredients together in a small bowl and set aside.
  - Shape the mince loosely into four 200-g patties, slightly wider than the buns. Press a thumb-print into the middle of each to stop them doming.
  - Heat a heavy frying pan or cast-iron griddle until smoking hot. Brush the patties with oil and season generously on both sides with salt and pepper.
  - Cook the patties 2 minutes the first side, flip, add a slice of cheddar to each, and cook another 1½-2 minutes for medium. Rest on a plate.
  - Toast the cut sides of the brioche buns in the same pan until golden.
  - Spread sauce on the base. Layer lettuce, tomato, then the cheesy patty, then onion rings and gherkins.
  - Cap with the top bun and press down gently. Eat immediately, with napkins.
---

The Burger & Lobster house burger — the trick is well-fatted chuck, fierce heat, and not pressing the patties as they cook. Brioche buns are mandatory; toasted, not warmed.

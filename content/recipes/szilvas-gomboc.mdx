story: |
  Szilvás gombóc — plum dumplings — is the great autumn dessert of the
  former Habsburg lands, eaten in Hungary, Austria, the Czech Republic,
  and Slovakia under different names (Zwetschkenknödel in Austria,
  švestkové knedlíky in Czech). The season is short and specific: the
  small, oval, dark-blue Italian or damson plums that ripen in late
  August and September.

  The defining technique is the potato dough — boiled potatoes pressed
  through a ricer and bound with just enough flour, semolina, and egg
  to hold together. Cool the potato before adding the flour, and don't
  overwork it; the dough should be soft, not elastic, or the dumplings
  will turn gluey.

  Often eaten as a main course, not a dessert. The buttery cinnamon
  breadcrumbs are non-negotiable.

ingredients:
  - 750 g floury potatoes, scrubbed
  - 250 g plain flour, plus extra for shaping
  - 1 large egg
  - 30 g semolina
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 16 small Italian or damson plums, washed
  - 16 sugar cubes (or small pieces of cinnamon stick)
  - 150 g unsalted butter
  - 200 g dry breadcrumbs
  - 4 tbsp caster sugar
  - 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  - Icing sugar, to dust
instructions:
  - Boil the potatoes whole in their skins until completely tender. Drain, peel while still hot, and press through a ricer onto a clean surface. Let them cool to lukewarm.
  - Slit each plum along the seam, remove the stone, and tuck a sugar cube (or a small cinnamon piece) inside.
  - Sprinkle the riced potato with the salt, semolina, and flour, make a well in the centre, and crack in the egg. Knead just until you have a soft, smooth dough — don't overwork it or it will turn gluey.
  - Divide the dough into 16 pieces. Flatten each in your palm, wrap around a plum, and seal completely, rolling smooth between floured hands.
  - Bring a wide pot of lightly salted water to a gentle boil. Drop in the dumplings in batches and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes — they're done about 2 minutes after they float.
  - While they cook, melt the butter in a wide pan and toast the breadcrumbs with the cinnamon and 2 tbsp of the sugar over medium heat until deep golden.
  - Lift the dumplings out with a slotted spoon, roll thoroughly in the buttery cinnamon crumbs, and dust with icing sugar before serving warm.
---

A Hungarian autumn classic from grandmother's kitchen — sweet plums hidden inside a soft potato dumpling, rolled in buttered cinnamon breadcrumbs. Eat them the day they're made.

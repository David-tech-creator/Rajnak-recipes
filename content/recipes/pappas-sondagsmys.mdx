story: |
  This is äggtoddy, the old Swedish nursery treat — a single egg
  separated, the yolk beaten with sugar and lemon to a pale foam,
  the white whipped to snow. Before vitamin pills it was given to
  children who needed building up; before whipped cream came in
  cans, it was the everyday Sunday dessert in Swedish homes from
  the 1900s through the 1960s.

  The technique is straight out of classic patisserie: a sabayon
  (or zabaglione) is exactly the same — yolk and sugar whipped
  over heat — and a French île flottante uses the same whipped
  whites floating on top. The lemon juice in the yolk is doing
  two jobs: brightening the sweetness and stabilising the foam
  against the sugar's weight.
difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 large egg
  - 3 tsp caster sugar
  - A few drops of fresh lemon juice
  - A pinch of salt (for the white)
instructions:
  - Crack the egg and carefully separate the yolk from the white into two bowls.
  - Whisk the egg white with a pinch of salt until it holds stiff peaks.
  - In the other bowl, beat the yolk with the sugar and lemon juice until pale, foamy, and slightly thickened.
  - Spoon the yellow lemon cream into a small glass and top with the snowy whipped white.
  - Eat immediately with a teaspoon, ideally with the Sunday newspaper.
---

A Swedish Sunday morning treat from Pappa's childhood — whipped egg whites alongside a sweet lemony egg yolk cream. Tiny, simple, and finished in under ten minutes.

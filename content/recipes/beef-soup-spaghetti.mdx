---
title: "Beef Soup with Mini Spaghetti & Carrots & Toast with Paprika Bone Marrow"
date: '2022-03-05'
category: Family Recipes
image: /images/recipes/beef-soup-spaghetti.jpg
prepTime: '20 mins'
cookTime: '2 h 30 m'
servings: 6
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 kg beef shin, on the bone
  - 2 large marrow bones
  - 3 carrots, peeled and cut into thick rounds
  - 2 parsnips, peeled and cut in thirds
  - 1 large onion, halved, skin on
  - 2 ripe tomatoes, halved
  - 1 small celeriac wedge (optional)
  - 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  - 3 beef bouillon cubes
  - 2 bay leaves
  - 5 black peppercorns
  - 1 small bunch parsley, plus extra to garnish
  - 2 tsp salt
  - 3 l cold water
  - 150 g mini spaghetti or vermicelli
  - "For the marrow toast:"
  - 6 slices country bread
  - 1 tsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - Flaky salt
instructions:
  - Put the beef shin and marrow bones in a tall pot with cold water. Bring slowly to a simmer, skimming off any foam.
  - Add carrots, parsnips, onion, tomatoes, celeriac, garlic, bouillon cubes, bay leaves, peppercorns, parsley stalks, and salt.
  - Simmer very gently, lid ajar, for about 2½ hours, until the meat is tender. Don't let it boil hard.
  - Lift the marrow bones out 30 minutes before serving. Scoop the marrow out with a small knife and keep warm.
  - Strain the broth through a fine sieve. Slice the beef and keep some carrot rounds.
  - Bring the strained broth back to a simmer and cook the mini spaghetti directly in it for 5-6 minutes, until just tender.
  - Toast the bread, smear with marrow while still warm, sprinkle with paprika and flaky salt.
  - Ladle the soup into deep bowls with meat, carrots, and pasta. Scatter with parsley. Serve the marrow toast alongside.
---

The full Hungarian Sunday treatment: clear húsleves with mini noodles, and on the side a slice of toast smeared with hot bone marrow and dusted with paprika. The marrow toast is the whole point of cooking with the bones in.

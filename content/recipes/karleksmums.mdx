ingredients:
  - 3 eggs
  - 3 dl caster sugar
  - 100 g butter, melted and cooled
  - 1 dl whole milk
  - 3 dl plain flour
  - 3 tbsp cocoa powder
  - 2 tsp baking powder
  - 2 tsp vanilla sugar
  - 'For the glaze:'
  - 100 g butter
  - 3 dl icing sugar
  - 3 tbsp cocoa powder
  - 3 tbsp strong coffee, hot
  - 2 tsp vanilla sugar
  - Desiccated coconut, to top
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 175 °C. Line a 20 by 30 cm rectangular tin with baking paper.
  - Whisk the eggs and sugar in a stand mixer until pale and fluffy, about three minutes.
  - Mix the flour, cocoa, baking powder, and vanilla sugar in a separate bowl. Fold into the egg mixture together with the melted butter and milk.
  - Pour into the tin and smooth the top. Bake in the middle of the oven for 22–25 minutes until a skewer comes out clean.
  - For the glaze, melt the butter in a saucepan, then whisk in the icing sugar, cocoa, hot coffee, and vanilla sugar until smooth.
  - Spread the glaze over the still-warm cake and immediately scatter generously with desiccated coconut. Let cool, then cut into 20 small squares.
---

Swedish fika at its sticky best — a soft chocolate sponge under a glossy coffee-chocolate glaze and a snowfall of coconut. Cut small, serve plenty: they disappear.

  Cod baked under a cream sauce is a Swedish weekday classic — torsk i
  ugn — and a fixture of cold-weather midweek dinners. The crème

  with stocks dwindling, line-caught Norwegian skrei or sustainable
  Pacific cod is the modern substitute. Pat the fish bone-dry before
  it meets the cream, and pull the tray the moment the flesh turns
  opaque.

ingredients:
  - 4 cod fillets (about 180 g each), skin off
  - 2 dl crème fraîche
  - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 60 g parmesan, finely grated
  - 1 tbsp chopped chives
  - Zest of ½ lemon
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - A few sprigs of dill, to garnish
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 200°C. Lightly oil a baking dish just big enough to hold the cod in one layer.
  - Pat the cod fillets dry and arrange in the dish. Season with salt and pepper and drizzle with the olive oil.
  - Stir together the crème fraîche, mustard, half the parmesan, the chives, and the lemon zest in a bowl.
  - Spoon the crème fraîche mixture evenly over the cod, covering each fillet completely.
  - Scatter the remaining parmesan on top.
  - Bake for 15-18 minutes, until the topping is golden and bubbling and the fish flakes easily.
  - Scatter with dill and serve with boiled new potatoes and peas.
---

A Swedish-style oven baked fish that's almost too easy — the crème fraîche keeps the cod moist while the parmesan crisps on top. Don't overbake; cod goes dry the moment you do.

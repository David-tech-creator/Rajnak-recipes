story: |
  Lecsó is Hungary's high-summer pan dish — onions, sweet peppers,
  tomatoes, and paprika cooked down together until the peppers go silky
  and the tomatoes collapse. It's the country's answer to French
  ratatouille or Basque piperade, and it draws on the same Ottoman
  legacy of pepper-and-tomato stews found across the Balkans.

  The traditional pepper is the pale yellow tv-paprika that fills
  Hungarian markets in August. Outside Hungary, sweet pointed romanos
  do the same job. The dish is finished different ways in different
  households: with eggs softly scrambled through, with smoked sausage,
  with rice, sometimes just as a sauce for noodles.

  Paprika goes in off the heat. Always.

ingredients:
  - 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  - 2 tbsp rapeseed oil or lard
  - 1 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 600 g sweet pointed bell peppers, deseeded and sliced into rings
  - 500 g ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  - 1 dl water
  - 1 tbsp tomato paste
  - 1 beef bouillon cube
  - 200 g smoked Hungarian sausage (kolbász), sliced thick
  - 1.5 dl long-grain rice
  - 4 eggs, lightly beaten
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Heat the oil in a wide pot. Add the onion and fry over medium heat until soft and just coloured, about 8 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika. Return to the heat and add the pepper rings. Stir to coat in the paprika oil.
  - Add the tomatoes, water, tomato paste, and crumbled bouillon cube. Bring to a simmer.
  - Meanwhile, cook the rice in salted water until tender, then drain.
  - When the peppers are soft and the tomatoes have collapsed (about 15 minutes), stir in the sliced sausage and the cooked rice. Simmer five minutes.
  - Pour in the beaten eggs and stir gently over low heat for a minute or two until just set. Season and serve.
---

The Hungarian summer pan — peppers, tomatoes, smoky sausage, and eggs softly scrambled through at the end. Best with the late-August peppers that taste of sun, but works year-round.

title: Liver Pörkölt (Pirított Máj)

prepTime: '10 mins'
cookTime: '20 mins'
servings: 4
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 600 g chicken livers (or veal liver), trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  - 2 large yellow onions, chopped fine
  - 3 tbsp lard or rapeseed oil
  - 2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 clove garlic, minced
  - 0.5 tsp ground black pepper
  - Salt, to taste, added at the end
  - 800 g floury potatoes, peeled
  - 50 g butter
  - 1 dl whole milk, warm
  - Chopped parsley, to serve
instructions:
  - Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 20 minutes. Drain and mash with the butter and warm milk. Keep warm.
  - Heat the lard in a wide frying pan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook gently until soft and golden, about 10 minutes.
  - Off the heat, stir in the paprika and garlic so they don't burn.
  - Return the pan to medium-high heat. Add the liver pieces and toss to coat in the paprika onions.
  - Cook for 4–6 minutes, stirring often, until the liver is just cooked through and still pink in the middle.
  - Season with black pepper and salt only at the end (salt earlier toughens the liver). Serve over the mashed potatoes with parsley scattered on top.
---

A weekday Hungarian classic — paprika onions, livers cooked fast so they stay tender, mashed potato underneath to catch the juices. Salt at the end, not before, or the liver goes rubbery.

ingredients:
  - 600 g chicken breast or thigh, cut into 2-cm cubes
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 3 tbsp Thai red or yellow curry paste
  - 400 ml coconut milk (1 tin)
  - 300 g basmati rice
  - 6 dl water (for the rice)
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - 1 tbsp fish sauce
  - 1 tsp sugar
  - Juice of ½ lime
  - A small handful coriander leaves
instructions:
  - Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then cook with 6 dl water and a pinch of salt: bring to the boil, cover, lower the heat and cook 12 minutes. Rest off the heat with the lid on.
  - Pat the chicken cubes dry and season with salt.
  - Heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium-high. Fry the chicken in a single layer for 4-5 minutes, until lightly browned all over.
  - Stir in the curry paste and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
  - Pour in the coconut milk, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir and bring to a gentle simmer.
  - Cook 6-8 minutes more, until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  - Finish with lime juice and taste for salt.
  - Serve over the steamed rice, scattered with coriander.
---

A 20-minute curry for a weekday — Thai paste does the heavy lifting. Use thigh meat if you can; it stays juicier than breast. Lime at the end is what makes it sing.

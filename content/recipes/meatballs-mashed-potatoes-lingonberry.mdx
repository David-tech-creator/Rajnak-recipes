ingredients:
  - 'For the meatballs:'
  - 500 g minced beef and pork mix
  - 1 small yellow onion, finely grated
  - 1 dl breadcrumbs
  - 1 dl whole milk
  - 1 egg
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 tsp white pepper
  - 0.5 tsp ground allspice
  - 2 tbsp butter, for frying
  - 'For the mashed potatoes:'
  - 1 kg floury potatoes, peeled
  - 80 g butter
  - 1.5 dl warm whole milk
  - Salt and white pepper, to taste
  - 'To serve:'
  - 4 tbsp lingonberry jam
  - 8 small pickled gherkins (smågurka), sliced
  - Chopped parsley, optional
instructions:
  - Soak the breadcrumbs in the milk for five minutes. Mix with the minced meat, grated onion, egg, salt, white pepper, and allspice until just combined. Don't overwork.
  - Roll into walnut-sized balls between damp hands and place on a tray. Chill for 10 minutes.
  - Meanwhile, boil the potatoes in salted water for 20 minutes until tender. Drain, return to the dry pan to steam off, and mash with the butter and warm milk. Season.
  - Melt the butter in a wide frying pan. Brown the meatballs over medium-high heat in batches, shaking the pan so they stay round. They take about 8 minutes total to cook through.
  - Divide the mash between four plates and pile the meatballs alongside.
  - Top each plate with a spoon of lingonberry jam, a small fan of pickled gherkin, and a scatter of parsley.
---

The Swedish national dinner — small, perfectly round meatballs, a soft mountain of mashed potato, sweet lingonberry, tart gherkins. The textures and temperatures only work when all four are on the plate at once.

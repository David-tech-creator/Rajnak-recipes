story: |
  Pytt i panna — literally "small bits in a pan" — is the Swedish
  Monday dinner: yesterday's roast, the heel of a sausage, a few
  cold potatoes, all diced fine and fried golden in butter. It
  exists in every Nordic country (pyttipannu in Finland, biksemad
  in Denmark, pyttipanne in Norway) and follows the same logic:
  nothing wasted.

  The three rules are even dice, a hot pan, and patience. Tiny,
  uniform cubes mean every piece develops the same crust. A
  too-crowded pan steams instead of frying. The signature topping
  — fried egg, pickled beetroot, pickled gherkin, a splash of
  Worcestershire — turns it from leftovers into a meal.

ingredients:
  - 10 medium potatoes, raw (or boiled and cold), peeled and cut into 1-cm dice
  - 2 yellow onions, finely diced
  - 2-3 tbsp butter
  - 400 g cooked meat, diced 1 cm (mix of beef, pork and something smoked)
  - 1 to 1.5 tsp salt
  - 2-3 pinches freshly ground black pepper
  - 4 eggs
  - Pickled beetroot, to serve
  - Pickled gherkins, to serve
  - A few dashes Worcestershire sauce, to finish
instructions:
  - Dice the peeled potatoes, then rinse the cubes briefly in cold water to wash off the starch — this stops them from sticking together in the pan. Drain very well in a colander.
  - Heat a large frying pan over medium-high heat and brown 2 tbsp of the butter. Add the potatoes and fry for 8–10 minutes, turning often with a spatula, until they take colour and feel almost tender.
  - When the potatoes are roughly half-cooked, add the remaining butter and the onions. Continue frying so the onions turn lightly golden alongside the potatoes.
  - Add the diced meat (or fry it briefly on its own first if the pan is too full) and toss until everything is hot, browned, and well mingled. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Just before serving, splash in a few drops of Worcestershire sauce and stir through — it lifts the whole pan.
  - Pile onto warm plates and top each portion with a fried egg. Serve with pickled beetroot and gherkins.
---

Sweden's classic use-up-the-leftovers hash: small even dice of potato, onion, and whatever cooked meat is in the fridge, fried until crisp and brown. The smoked element (ham, sausage) is what gives it its character.

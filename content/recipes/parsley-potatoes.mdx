kitchenNote: 'Butter off the heat. Parsley last so it stays green.'
story: |
  Persiljepotatis is the Swedish side dish — the one that goes
  with stekt strömming, with kalops, with kåldolmar, with almost
  any homestyle main. Small waxy potatoes (mandelpotatis or
  amandine), boiled in their skins or peeled, then rolled hot in
  butter and chopped flat-leaf parsley. The French call the same
  thing pommes persillées; the technique is identical.

  Two small rules separate the good plate from the great one. The
  butter goes in off the heat — high heat splits it and turns the
  parsley grey within seconds. And the potatoes must be properly
  dried after draining: a minute back in the warm pan to drive off
  the surface water, so the butter coats rather than slides.
difficulty: Easy
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 1 kg small waxy potatoes, peeled
  - 50 g unsalted butter
  - 1 large handful flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  - 1 tsp flaky sea salt
  - Black pepper, to taste
instructions:
  - Cut the potatoes into even chunks, about 3 cm each.
  - Put them in a saucepan, cover generously with cold water, and add a good pinch of salt.
  - Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer and cook for 15 minutes, until a knife slides in easily.
  - Drain and return the potatoes to the warm pan over very low heat for a minute, to dry them off.
  - Add the butter and chopped parsley, season with sea salt and pepper, and toss gently until every potato is glossy and green-flecked. Serve at once.
---

A classic side that asks for nothing more than good potatoes, good butter, and a heap of fresh parsley. Pairs with almost any main — roast chicken, fried fish, meat stews.

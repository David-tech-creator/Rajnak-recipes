ingredients:
  - 6 salmon fillets (150 g each), skin removed
  - 150 g cream cheese
  - 1 small clove garlic, crushed
  - Zest of ½ lemon
  - 25 g fresh white breadcrumbs
  - 25 g parmesan, coarsely grated
  - 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped, plus extra to garnish
  - 1 tsp paprika, for dusting
  - Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
instructions:
  - Preheat the oven to 200°C and lightly grease a baking tin.
  - Season both sides of the salmon fillets with salt and pepper and arrange them on the tin.
  - In a bowl, mash the cream cheese with the crushed garlic, lemon zest, a good pinch of salt, and a grind of pepper.
  - Divide the mixture into six and spread evenly over the top of each salmon fillet.
  - In a separate bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, parmesan, and chopped parsley with a little salt and pepper. Sprinkle this over the cream cheese layer and dust with paprika.
  - Bake for about 15 minutes, until the salmon has turned from translucent to opaque pink and the crust is golden.
  - Serve immediately, garnished with extra chopped parsley. New potatoes and a green salad are all you need.
---

Mary Berry's reliable method — the cream cheese keeps the fish moist, the parmesan crust crisps up, and the whole tray is on the table in under half an hour. Don't overbake; salmon is happier slightly under than over.

ingredients:
  - 'For the sponge:'
  - 175 g butter, soft
  - 175 g caster sugar
  - 3 large eggs, separated
  - 175 g self-raising flour
  - 1 tsp baking powder
  - 2 tbsp milk
  - Zest of 2 unwaxed lemons
  - 'For the meringue topping:'
  - 3 egg whites (from above)
  - 175 g caster sugar
  - 25 g flaked almonds
  - 'To finish:'
  - 300 g strawberries, sliced
  - 250 ml double cream, whipped
  - 4 tbsp lemon curd
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 180 °C. Grease two 20-cm round sandwich tins and line the bases with baking paper.
  - Cream the butter and 175 g sugar in a stand mixer until pale and fluffy. Beat in the egg yolks one at a time, then fold in the flour, baking powder, milk, and lemon zest. Divide between the tins and smooth.
  - For the meringue, whisk the egg whites in a clean bowl to stiff peaks. Add the 175 g sugar a spoonful at a time, whisking until glossy and stiff.
  - Spoon the meringue evenly over the two sponges and swirl the surface. Scatter the flaked almonds over one of them.
  - Bake for 30–35 minutes, until the meringue is pale gold and crisp and the sponge is cooked. Cool in the tins for 10 minutes, then transfer carefully to a rack — meringue side up.
  - To assemble, place the plain meringue layer on a plate, meringue up. Spread with whipped cream, lemon curd, and half the strawberries. Top with the almond meringue layer. Finish with the remaining strawberries.
---

Mary Berry's clever cake — sponge baked under its own meringue, sandwiched with cream, lemon curd, and strawberries. Looks showstopping for very little fuss; best assembled close to serving.

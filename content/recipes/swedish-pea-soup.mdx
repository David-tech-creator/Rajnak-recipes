---
title: Swedish Pea Soup (Soldatens Ärtsoppa)
date: '2022-04-10'

image: /images/recipes/swedish-pea-soup.jpg
prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '2 h'
servings: 6
kitchenNote: Thursdays, with mustard and punsch. Always.
signoff: Smaklig måltid.
featured: false
ingredients:
  - 500 g yellow split peas, soaked overnight
  - 2 l water
  - 500 g lightly salted pork shoulder or smoked ham hock
  - 2 yellow onions, finely chopped
  - 1 tsp dried marjoram
  - 1 tsp dried thyme
  - 2 bay leaves
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - Swedish mustard, to serve
  - Crisp bread (knäckebröd), to serve
instructions:
  - Drain the soaked peas and put them in a large pot with the 2 l water. Skim off any pea skins that float to the surface.
  - Add the pork, onions, bay leaves, marjoram, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then lower to a gentle simmer.
  - Cook with the lid ajar for about 1.5 hours, until the peas are completely soft and the pork is fork-tender. Top up with hot water if it dries out too much.
  - Lift the pork from the pot, slice or shred it, and set aside. Discard the bay leaves.
  - Season the soup generously with salt and pepper. Return the pork to the pot to warm through. Serve with a spoonful of strong Swedish mustard stirred in at the table, plus knäckebröd alongside.
---

Eaten every Thursday in Sweden, traditionally followed by pancakes and lingonberries — a habit from the army that became a national ritual. The mustard is not optional.

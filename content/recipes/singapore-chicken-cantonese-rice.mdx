  Cantonese fried rice — chow fan — was the inevitable invention
  of a kitchen that wasted nothing: yesterday's rice, cold and
  dry, tossed in a screaming wok with whatever was on the counter.
  Cold rice is the rule because hot rice still holds surface
  moisture, which means the grains clump and steam instead of
  separating and toasting.

  The "Singapore chicken" glaze here — soy, honey, ketjap manis,
  five-spice — is closer to a Cantonese roast (char siu)
  marinade than anything actually Singaporean; the name is the
  loose travel-borrowing of restaurant menus across Southeast
  Asia. The sticky-sweet chicken against fluffy fried rice is the
  point, whatever the geography.

ingredients:
  - 600 g chicken thigh fillets, cut into chunks
  - 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  - 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  - 2 tbsp honey
  - 2 tbsp ketjap manis (sweet soy)
  - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  - 2 cloves garlic, grated
  - 1 tbsp grated ginger
  - 1 tsp Chinese five-spice
  - 3 dl jasmine rice
  - 2 eggs, beaten
  - 100 g cooked ham or Chinese sausage, diced
  - 1 dl frozen peas
  - 2 spring onions, sliced
  - 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
  - 200 g broccoli florets
  - 200 g pak choi, quartered
  - 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve
instructions:
  - Whisk the soy sauces, honey, ketjap manis, vinegar, garlic, ginger, and five-spice in a bowl. Toss the chicken through and marinate 15 minutes.
  - Rinse the jasmine rice well and cook it: 4.5 dl water, pinch of salt, bring to boil, lid on, low heat 12 minutes, off heat to rest 10 more. Spread on a tray to cool slightly.
  - Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok over high heat. Pour in the beaten egg, scramble loosely, and tip onto a plate.
  - Add another tablespoon of oil. Stir-fry the ham and peas for 1 minute, then add the rice and toss hard until each grain is hot and lightly toasted. Stir back in the egg and spring onions. Keep warm.
  - In a clean wok or pan, heat the last tablespoon of oil and stir-fry the chicken in its marinade over high heat for 6–8 minutes, until sticky and dark.
  - Steam the broccoli and pak choi for 4 minutes, until tender-crisp.
  - Pile rice on each plate, top with sticky chicken and a heap of steamed greens, and finish with sesame seeds.

A sticky, sweet-savoury chicken stir-fry over fluffy Cantonese fried rice — a popular Sunday dinner in the family. The trick to good fried rice is rice that's cooled, never freshly hot.

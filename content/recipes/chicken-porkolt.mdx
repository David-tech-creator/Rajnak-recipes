prepTime: '15 mins'
cookTime: '1 h'
servings: 4
featured: false
kitchenNote: 'Onion to meat — one-to-one by weight.'
signoff: 'Jó étvágyat.'
ingredients:
  - 800 g - 1 kg chicken drumsticks (or 800 g mixed mushrooms)
  - 2-3 tbsp rapeseed oil
  - 1 big yellow onion (or 2 small), finely chopped
  - 1-2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  - 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  - 1 large ripe tomato, chopped
  - 1 green pepper, sliced (optional)
  - 2 chicken bouillon cubes
  - Water, to cover
  - 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to serve
instructions:
  - Heat the oil in a heavy pot. Add the chopped onion and fry over medium heat until it takes on a golden colour, about 8 minutes.
  - Pull the pot off the heat and stir in the paprika — it must not burn or it will turn bitter. Add the chicken drumsticks (or mushrooms) immediately and turn to coat.
  - Return to medium heat. Stir for a few minutes so the chicken browns, then salt it.
  - Pour in just enough water to cover the chicken, drop in the bouillon cubes, and put the lid on tight.
  - Simmer gently for about 50 minutes (or 30 minutes for mushroom). The chicken should be very tender, almost falling off the bone.
  - Add the chopped tomato and green pepper halfway through.
  - The sauce should be neither thin nor thick — coat-the-spoon consistency.
  - Serve in deep plates over freshly made nokedli, scattered with parsley.
---

The lighter sister of beef pörkölt — chicken cooks in half the time and gives a milder, glossier sauce. The mushroom version is the Hungarian vegetarian classic. Same paprika rules: never let it burn.
